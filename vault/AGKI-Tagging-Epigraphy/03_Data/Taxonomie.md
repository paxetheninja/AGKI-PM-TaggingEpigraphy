	# Hierarchical Taxonomy for Ancient Greek Inscriptions

This taxonomy is organized into three facets:

1. **Content (Text & Function)**
2. **Type (Object & Material)**
3. **State (Condition & Preservation)**
---
## 1. Content (Text & Function)

1. **Official and Legal Documents**
   2. Decrees
   3. Laws and Regulations
   4. Edicts and Ordinances
   5. Treaties and Agreements
   6. Contracts and Private Legal Acts
   7. Manumission Records
   8. Official Letters

9. **Religious and Dedicatory Texts**
   10. Votive Dedications
   11. Prayers and Invocations
   12. Sacred Laws and Rules
   13. Curses and Magic
      1. Curse Tablets (Defixiones)
      2. Protective Charms
   14. Oracular and Prophetic Texts
   15. Hymns and Sacred Poetry
   16. Confession Inscriptions
   17. Orations and Praise of Gods

18. **Honorific and Commemorative Inscriptions**
   19. Honorific Inscriptions
   20. Funerary Inscriptions (Epitaphs)
   21. Commemorative Monuments
   22. Agonistic / Victory Inscriptions
   23. Building Commemoration

24. **Administrative Records and Lists**
   25. Accounts and Financial Records
   26. Inventories
   27. Lists of Officials or Citizens
   28. Donor and Subscription Lists
   29. Calendars and Timetables
   30. Registers and Catalogues

31. **Identification and Ownership Marks**
   32. Ownership Marks
   33. Makerâ€™s Marks and Signatures
   34. Labels and Captions
   35. Boundary Markers (Horoi)
   36. Centurial and Construction Marks

37. **Didactic and School Texts**
   38. Abecedaria (Alphabetical Exercises)
   39. Writing Exercises and School Texts
   40. Literary Quotations and Excerpts
   41. Didascalic Epigrams
   42. Catalogues of Knowledge

43. **Graffiti and Personal Expressions**
   44. Graffiti Messages
   45. Acclamations and Slogans
   46. Humor, Insults, and Competitive Graffiti
   47. Drawings and Figure Labels (Textual Components)

## 2. Type (Object & Material)
### 2.1 Object Type (Physical Object Form)

1. **Monumental Stone Objects**
   2. Stele
   3. Statue Base
   4. Altar
   5. Column or Pillar
   6. Wall or Building Surface
      1. Rock-cut / Rupestral Inscriptions
   7. Tombstone (Gravestone)
   8. Sarcophagus
   9. Mosaic Floor or Pavement
   10. Other Architectural Elements
      1. Architraves / Friezes / Archways
      2. Cornerstones / Building Blocks
      3. Assembling / Setting Marks

11. **Portable Objects**
   12. Ceramic Vessels and Ostraca
      1. Painted Vessel Inscriptions
      2. Incised Vessel Inscriptions
      3. Ostraca (Shards Used as Writing Supports)
   13. Lead or Metal Tablets
      1. Lead Tablets (e.g., curse tablets, letters)
      2. Bronze Tablets (e.g., laws, treaties)
   14. Plaques and Plates
      1. Metal Plaques / Leaves (e.g., votive leaves)
      2. Clay or Stone Tablets (portable)
   15. Weapons and Armor
      1. Helmets
      2. Shields
      3. Swords / Blades
   16. Tools and Utensils
      1. Strigils
      2. Mirrors
      3. Loom Weights
      4. Measuring Weights
   17. Jewelry and Gems
      1. Rings
      2. Engraved Gemstones
      3. Inscribed Jewelry Items
   18. Coins (Numismatic Legends)
   19. Organic Supports (Rare / Context-dependent)
      1. Wooden Tablets
      2. Other Organic Objects (bone, ivory, shell)

## 3. State (Condition & Preservation)
1. **Complete / Intact**
   2. Complete (Text fully preserved)
   3. Intact (Object unbroken)

4. **Incomplete / Fragmentary**
   5. Incomplete (Text partially missing)
   6. Fragmentary (Survives in fragments)
   7. Broken (Physical breakage affecting text)
   8. Non-contiguous Fragments (Fragments do not join)
   9. Contiguous / Rejoinable Fragments (Fragments join or have been joined)

10. **Legibility**
   11. Damaged but Legible
   12. Good (Minor wear; largely readable)
   13. Worn / Weathered (Erosion reduces readability)
   14. Illegible (Text unreadable; only traces)

15. **Loss and Documentation Status**
   16. Lost (Object/text no longer extant)
   17. Lost but Recorded (Known from copies/squeezes/transcriptions)

18. **Reconstruction and Intervention**
   19. Reconstructed (Text and/or object reconstructed from fragments)
      1. Multiple Fragments Joined
      2. Partial Reconstruction (Some parts missing)
   20. Mutilated / Erased (Intentional removal)
      1. Damnatio Memoriae-like Name Erasure
      2. Chiselled / Defaced Surface
   21. Reused / Palimpsest (Earlier text overwritten/removed for reuse)
