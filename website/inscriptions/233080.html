AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant Athenian public document from the mid-3rd century BC (archonship of Diomedon). It records a decree proposed by Theophemos of Marathon following a motion by Eurykleides of Kephisia, a prominent statesman. The decree calls for a voluntary subscription (epidosis) to secure the 'safety of the city' and the 'protection of the countryside,' likely due to military threats during the post-Chremonidean War period. The text is followed by a long list of donors, including citizens, metics, and the Peripatetic philosopher Lyco of Troas. The inscription was originally a large marble stele erected in the Athenian Agora.
