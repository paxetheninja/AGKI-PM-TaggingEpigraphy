AI Analysis Summary

The inscription is the Greek version of the Decree of Canopus (238 BC), a trilingual decree (Hieroglyphic, Demotic, and Greek) issued by a synod of Egyptian priests. It honors Ptolemy III Euergetes and Berenice II for their military successes, the return of stolen statues from Persia, and their famine relief efforts. Crucially, it introduces a leap year into the Egyptian calendar and deifies the young princess Berenice, who died during the synod. The text is largely complete and provides vital evidence for Ptolemaic ruler cults and administrative-religious reforms.
