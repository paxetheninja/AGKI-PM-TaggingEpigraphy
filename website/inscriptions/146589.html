AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant fragment of a building specification (syngraphe) for the Temple of Zeus Basileus at Lebadeia. It dates to the 2nd century BC and provides detailed technical and legal regulations for the construction process. The text mentions the 'naopoioi' (temple-builders or commissioners) who are responsible for contracting out the remaining works ('ta kataloipa ton ergon'). It explicitly details the penalties for the 'ergones' (contractors) if they damage the stone blocks ('lithon diaphtheirei') during the work, requiring them to replace the damaged stone at their own expense. The mention of lead-pouring ('molubdochotounton') refers to the common Greek practice of securing stone blocks with metal clamps set in lead. Any damaged stone is declared 'hieros' (sacred), indicating it becomes property of the sanctuary even if unusable.
