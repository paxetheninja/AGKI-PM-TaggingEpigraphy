This inscription, dated to 116 BC, is a significant legal document from Hellenistic Crete. It details an arbitration process (epitrope) where Knossos serves as the mediator between the neighboring cities of Lato and Olous. The text outlines the procedural requirements for resolving disputes, including the timeline for the decision, the publication of the results on stone stelae in multiple pan-Cretan and international sanctuaries (Knossos, Lato, Olous, and Delos), and the requirement for financial sureties (10 Alexandrian talents) to ensure compliance. The mention of specific Cretan months (Spermios, Theodaisios, Eleusynios) and magistrates (kosmoi) provides precise chronological and administrative context.
