This inscription (LW 71) is a decree from the Cretan city of Eleutherna, dating to approximately 204-203 BC. It is part of a series of decrees from various Greek cities recognizing the 'asylia' (inviolability) and 'hiera' (sacred) status of the city of Teos and its territory. The decree was prompted by a diplomatic mission led by the Rhodian Aigesandros, son of Eukrates, who acted as an ambassador for Teos. The mission was supported by the Hellenistic monarchs Antiochus III the Great and Philip V of Macedon, both of whom are mentioned as having sent envoys (Aigesandros and Perdiccas respectively) to advocate for Teos. The text highlights the traditional kinship (syngeneia) and friendship (philia) between the cities and emphasizes the religious devotion to Dionysus, the patron deity of Teos.
