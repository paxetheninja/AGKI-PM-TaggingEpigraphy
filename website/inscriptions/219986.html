AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a priestly decree from 39 BC, issued at Karnak (Thebes/Diospolis Magna) during the reign of Cleopatra VII and Ptolemy XV Caesarion. It honors Callimachus, a high-ranking official (strategos and epistrategos), for his exceptional philanthropy and leadership during a period of severe famine. He is credited with saving the city's population and ensuring the continuity of religious life. The decree orders the erection of three statues (one bronze, two stone) and the publication of the text on a bilingual stele (Greek and Egyptian) to be placed in the temple of Amun-Ra (Amonrasonther).
