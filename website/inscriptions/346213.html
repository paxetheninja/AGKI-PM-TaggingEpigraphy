This inscription, found at Sagalassos and dating to the early Hellenistic period (ca. 333-200 BC), is a major legal document often referred to as the 'Sagalassos Stasis Law'. It outlines strict measures to prevent civil unrest and the illegal occupation of the acropolis (akran). The text prescribes capital punishment (death) for those who attempt to overthrow the city's constitution or engage in armed rebellion. It also details religious consequences, declaring offenders as enemies of the gods and requiring massive sacrifices of white bulls, rams, and goats. The document concludes with a list of local magistrates (archontes) whose names (e.g., Moasis, Kilasarbes) are characteristically Pisidian, providing invaluable evidence for the onomastics and social organization of the region during its transition into the Hellenistic world.
