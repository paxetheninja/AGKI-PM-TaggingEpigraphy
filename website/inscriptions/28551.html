🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major portion of Stele A of the Epidaurian Iamata (IG IV² 1, 121), dating to the late 4th century BC. It contains a series of miracle stories (iamata) that served as public testimonials to the healing powers of Asclepius. The text follows a consistent structure: the patient's name and origin, their specific ailment (e.g., long-term pregnancy, blindness, tattoos/marks, paralysis), the ritual of incubation in the abaton, a dream vision of the god performing a surgical or magical cure, and the patient's departure in a healthy state. The text also highlights the importance of 'pistis' (faith) over skepticism, as seen in the stories of Ambrosia and the man with the paralyzed fingers.
