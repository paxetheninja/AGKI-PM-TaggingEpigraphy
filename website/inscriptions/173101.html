AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Tomis, dating to approximately 100 BC, records a decree of the council and the people. It describes a period of severe distress for the city, characterized by plague (loimike) and economic hardship (aporia), which led many citizens to abandon the city. In response, the city appointed forty 'epilektoi' (select men) to guard the gates and walls day and night. The decree honors the leaders of this group and the guards themselves, ordering that their names be inscribed on a white stone slab (telamon) to be placed in a prominent location. The text provides valuable insight into the civic defense mechanisms and religious life (sacrifices to the Mother of the Gods and Dioskouroi) of Tomis during the Hellenistic period.
