This inscription from Tomis (ca. 100 BC) is a significant civic decree documenting a period of emergency. The city faced a 'loimike peristasis' (plague or pestilence) and economic hardship, leading to a shortage of citizens capable of defending the walls. The decree records the appointment of two commanders, Apollos and Poseidonios, who organized a force of forty 'epilektoi' (selected guards) to secure the gates and patrol the city day and night. The text honors these men for their 'philoponia' (diligence) and ensures the preservation of their names on a white stone stele to encourage future civic virtue (philotimia). The list of names provides valuable prosopographical data for Hellenistic Tomis.
