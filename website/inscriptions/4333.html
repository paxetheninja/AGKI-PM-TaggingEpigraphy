AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Athenian ephebic catalogue from the Roman Imperial period (169/170 AD). It documents the civic and religious life of the ephebeia, listing the antikosmetes Trophimos and various sophronistai (moral overseers) and technical instructors (paidotribes, hoplomachos). The text is structured by the thirteen Athenian tribes (including the later additions of Hadrianis and Attalis) and lists both citizen ephebes and 'epengraphoi' (foreigners or non-citizens). It also serves as a record of liturgical service, noting the gymnasiarchs for each month and the agonothetai for imperial festivals like the Antinoeia and Adrianeia.
