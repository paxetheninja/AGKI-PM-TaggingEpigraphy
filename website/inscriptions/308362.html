AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a comprehensive 'diagraphe' (cult regulation) from the island of Kos, dating to the late Hellenistic period. It regulates the sale of the priesthood of Aphrodite (under the epithets Pontia and Pandamos) and establishes the financial and ritual framework for the cult. Key features include the division of sacrificial fees between the priestess and the public treasury, the involvement of civic magistrates (prostatai and tamiai), and specific contributions required from sailors and manumitted slaves. The text also includes penal clauses for non-compliance and instructions for the public display of the law on a stone stele near the altar of Aphrodite.
