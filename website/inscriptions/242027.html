AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to 182 BC during the archonship of Damosthenes, records a major endowment by Alkesippos of Kalydon to the sanctuary of Apollo at Delphi. The text serves multiple purposes: it is a votive dedication of a significant sum of gold and silver, a sacred law establishing the 'Alkesippeia' festival and sacrifice, and a legal document for the manumission of a slave named Theutiman. The detailed list of witnesses and the involvement of city officials (prytaneis, archons) highlight the integration of private philanthropy into the civic and religious life of Hellenistic Delphi.
