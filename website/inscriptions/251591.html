This inscription (IEry 2) is a fragmentary legal decree from Erythrai dating to the 5th century BCE. It details the organization of a judicial body, specifying that judges should be selected from the tribes (phylai) and that the court must consist of at least sixty members. It establishes fines in staters for legal infractions and mandates the publication of the decree on a stone stele to be placed near the altar of Zeus Agoraios in the second prytany. The text reflects the early democratic or semi-democratic administrative structures of Ionian cities, emphasizing procedural transparency and the role of the prytaneis.
