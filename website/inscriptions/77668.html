This inscription is the 'Marmor Parium' (Parian Marble), a Hellenistic chronographic document dated to 264/3 BC. It provides a chronological list of events in Greek history, starting from the reign of Cecrops in Athens (traditionally 1581 BC) down to the mid-3rd century BC. The text is a vital source for ancient Greek chronology, particularly for the dates of poets (Sappho, Sophocles, Euripides) and the establishment of festivals like the Panathenaia.
