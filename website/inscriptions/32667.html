This inscription is a posthumous honorific decree issued by the Achaean League (Koinon of the Achaeans) for Saion, son of Polycharmos, a citizen of Megalopolis. Saion held the prestigious religious office of Hierophant of the Great Gods (likely associated with the sanctuary at Lykosoura or Megalopolis). The decree, proposed by Pompeius Apelles during the term of the strategos Claudius Teimandros, highlights Saion's exceptional character (sophrosyne), his noble lineage, and his Panhellenic reputation. It mandates the creation of bronze statues to be placed in the major Panhellenic sanctuaries of Olympia, Isthmia, and Nemea, as well as in his home city. The cost of the statue mentioned at the end was covered by his wife, Aristokleia. The text is a significant witness to the social and religious life of the Peloponnese in the late 2nd or early 3rd century AD, demonstrating the continued prestige of the Achaean League and the local elite's involvement in traditional cults.
