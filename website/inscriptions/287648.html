AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IK Ilion 10), dated to 77 BC, records a comprehensive settlement between the cities of the Troad (the Koinon of Athena Ilias) and the sanctuary of Athena at Ilion. The agreement was mediated by the Roman quaestor Lucius Julius Caesar to resolve the financial distress of the cities, which were heavily indebted to the sanctuary. The text regulates the reduction of interest rates, the management of sacred funds, and the organization of the Panathenaic festival, including sacrifices to Athena and Zeus Polieus, athletic and musical contests, and the dispatch of sacred embassies (theoroi). It provides a valuable list of representatives from the major cities of the region.
