🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a highly significant building contract (syngraphe) from the 2nd century BC for the Temple of Zeus Basileus in Lebadeia. It provides an exhaustive technical description of the masonry work required for the temple's paving (katastrotēres) and architectural ornaments (thringoi). It outlines the administrative and legal framework of public works in Hellenistic Boiotia, including the roles of the naopoioi (temple wardens), the architect, and the Boiotarchs. The text specifies the use of local Lebadeian stone, the methods for lead-pouring (molybdocheo), and the strict penalties for 'kakotechnia' (bad workmanship). The presence of brackets indicates that while the text is extensive, it is reconstructed from fragments.
