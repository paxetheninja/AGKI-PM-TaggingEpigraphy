AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Delphic manumission record dating to the late 2nd century BC. It documents the 'sale' of a female slave named Zoe (also called Koradion) to Apollo Pythios by Peitholaos and his wife Eratis for 4 silver minae. The text includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring Zoe to remain in service to her former owners until their death. It also specifies that any children born to her during this period shall be free. The document is dated by the archonship of Xenokrates and lists several local officials and witnesses, including priests and bouleutai.
