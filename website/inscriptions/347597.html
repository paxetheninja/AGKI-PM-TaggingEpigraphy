This inscription is an Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Hippias (181/0 BC). It honors a board of officials (the 'men elected for the revenues') who managed various state income streams. The text is significant for its detailed list of specific revenue categories, including taxes on mills (mylothrikon), bread-selling (artopolikon), grain (siteron), and wine (oineron). It provides instructions for the publication of the decree on two stone stelae: one to be placed in the city near the Bouleuterion and another in Piraeus near the Strategion in the Emporion. The treasurer of military funds, Philon, is tasked with the expenditure for the stelae.
