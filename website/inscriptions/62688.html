🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of the Delian 'comptes des hiéropes' (accounts of the temple administrators) from the period of Delian independence. It is divided into two functional sections: the first records the five-year leases of sacred real estate (houses, rooms, and workshops) to private individuals, including the names of guarantors and the annual rent. The second section is a meticulous inventory of votive offerings (mostly silver phialai, rhyta, and jewelry) stored in various temple buildings, such as the Porinos Naos, the Aphrodision, and the Asklepieion. The mention of dedications from Alexandria, Knidos, and Rhodes highlights the international panhellenic status of the Delian sanctuary during the Hellenistic period.
