This inscription from Prusias ad Hypium, dating after the Constitutio Antoniniana of 212 AD (as evidenced by the high frequency of the nomen 'Aurelius'), is a monumental honorific text for Publius Aelius Octavianus Tyrannus. It details his distinguished career, including his service as agoranomos (market overseer), treasurer of the grain fund (sitonika chremata), ambassador, and his current roles as first archon and priest/agonothetes of Zeus Olympios. The monument was dedicated by the phylarchoi (tribal leaders) of the city's twelve tribes (Sebastene, Thebais, Germanike, Sabiniane, Faustiniane, Dionysias, Tiberiane, Prusias, Adriane, Megaris, Iuliane, and Antoniane) in recognition of his benefactions. The text provides a complete list of these tribes and their respective leaders, offering a rare and detailed snapshot of the civic and tribal organization of a Bithynian city in the 3rd century AD.
