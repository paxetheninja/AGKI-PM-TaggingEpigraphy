AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IPri 22) is a Hellenistic honorific decree from the city of Priene, dating to the eponymous magistracy of the stephanephoros Leomedon (c. 3rd century BC). It honors Nymphon, son of Protarchos, for his exemplary conduct as phrourarchos (commander of the garrison) of the city's citadel (akra). The text details that he guarded the citadel justly and returned it safely to the people. As a reward, the boule and demos voted to crown him with a golden crown during the Dionysia festival and to display the decree on a stone stele in the sanctuary of Athena Polias. The text is fragmentary but largely reconstructible through standard epigraphic formulae.
