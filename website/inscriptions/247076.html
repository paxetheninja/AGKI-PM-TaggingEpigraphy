AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IDid 26) belongs to the extensive series of building accounts from the Hellenistic Temple of Apollo at Didyma. It provides a meticulous record of the construction process, specifically detailing the placement of 'white stones' (leukoi lithoi) and 'petrinoi' in the opisthodomos, the labyrinth, and various walls. It names the prophet Phormion, the architect Leon, and numerous individuals responsible for specific blocks or tasks. The text is highly technical, using architectural terms like 'paragonios', 'diatoichos', and 'amphiskeparnos', and records measurements in feet and costs in drachmas, serving as both a financial audit and a permanent record of the temple's physical assembly.
