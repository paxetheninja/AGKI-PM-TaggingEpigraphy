AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is the Greek portion of the famous Rosetta Stone. It contains a decree issued by the priests of Egypt gathered at Memphis in 196 BC to commemorate the coronation of Ptolemy V Epiphanes. The text lists the king's benefactions, including tax remissions for temples, military victories at Lykopolis, and his piety toward the sacred animals (Apis and Mnevis). In return, the priests grant him divine honors, establishing his cult in every temple of Egypt and ordering the decree to be inscribed in three scripts: hieroglyphic, demotic, and Greek.
