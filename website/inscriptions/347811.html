This inscription, dated to the archonship of Peithoboulos (274/3 BC), is a significant administrative document from the Athenian Asklepieion. It records a decree of the Athenian assembly ordering an inventory and audit (exetasmos) of the sacred treasures and votive offerings within the sanctuary. The text provides an exhaustive list of dedicated objects, including anatomical votives (eyes, legs, breasts), jewelry (rings, necklaces), and vessels (phialai, kymbia), often specifying the material (gold, silver, bronze), the weight, and the name of the dedicator. This document is a primary source for understanding Athenian religious practice, sanctuary administration, and the social history of dedicators in the 3rd century BC.
