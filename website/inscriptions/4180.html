This inscription is an Athenian ephebic catalogue dating to the archonship of Antipatros the Younger (45/46 AD). It lists the ephebes of that year, organized by their tribes (Erechtheis, Aegeis, Pandionis, Leontis, Ptolemais, Akamantis, Oineis, Kekropis, Hippothontis, Aiantis, and Antiochis). The text highlights the role of Tiberius Claudius Novius, a prominent figure in 1st-century Athens, serving here as gymnasiarch for the second time. The document is a crucial source for Athenian prosopography and administrative history during the early Roman Imperial period.
