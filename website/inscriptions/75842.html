AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Thera, dated to 149 AD (during the consulship of Scipio Orfitus and Sosius Priscus), documents a significant act of euergetism by Flavius Kleitosthenes Claudianus. The text details a formal promise (eisangelia) made to the city to restore a dilapidated stoa near the agora. The benefactor emphasizes that the work is funded from his own means (ek ton idion) and involves his family members in the dedication. The text is highly formal, including imperial titulature for Antoninus Pius and his family, and references to the local administrative processes of the boule and demos.
