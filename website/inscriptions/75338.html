This inscription is a significant administrative and legal document from the island of Nasos (Moschonesi) in the Hecatonnesi archipelago, dating to the early Hellenistic period. It records a series of financial penalties (zamiai) and settlements, specifically mentioning 'liponautai' (those who failed in naval duties) and 'paroikoi' (resident aliens). The text is structured around the terms of office of various prytaneis (notably Paisikrates) and strategoi. A critical historical element is the reference to a letter from Alexander (the Great), which likely provided the legal framework or royal mandate for the settlements and fines described. The use of gold staters as the unit of account highlights the high stakes of these civic and legal transactions.
