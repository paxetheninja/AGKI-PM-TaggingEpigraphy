This inscription is a major inventory from the Serapeion C on Delos, dating to the period after 145 BC. It provides a meticulous record of votive offerings dedicated to the Egyptian deities (Isis, Sarapis, Anubis) and other gods. The text is organized by the physical location of the items within the sanctuary (e.g., the temple of Isis, the temple of Anubis, the exedra, the pastophorion). It lists a wide variety of objects including statues, phialai, rings, crowns, and incense burners, often specifying their material (gold, silver, bronze, wood), weight, and the name of the donor. The presence of Roman names like Marcus Lollius and Gaius Messius reflects the cosmopolitan nature of Delos as a commercial and religious hub during the 2nd century BC. The 'koinon of the therapeitai' (an association of worshippers) appears as a collective donor and administrative body.
