AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a manumission record from Delphi, dated to the mid-1st century AD (20-46 AD) during the archonship of Lysimachos. It records the sale of a female slave named Agale to Apollo Pythios by Euphrosyne. The document is highly formulaic, including the price of 4 silver minae, a 'paramone' clause requiring Agale to serve Euphrosyne for life, and the appointment of Satyros as the legal guarantor. The text concludes with a list of witnesses, including priests of Apollo and city officials, and instructions for the record to be inscribed both in the temple of Apollo and in the city's public archives.
