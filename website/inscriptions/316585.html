This inscription from Pergamon dates to 145/4 BC, the 14th year of the reign of Attalus II Philadelphus. It records the names of youths (paides) who were selected (ekkrithentes) to become ephebes. The text lists the presiding officials, including four paidonomoi (supervisors of boys) and the gymnasiarchos Herakleon. The list is dedicated to Attalus, the son of King Eumenes II, who would later reign as Attalus III. The text contains several lacunae and missing lines, indicating its fragmentary state, but the primary administrative and prosopographical data remains legible.
