AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the Athenian 'phialai exeleutherikai' records, dating to approximately 330 BCE. These documents record the outcome of legal proceedings (dikai apostasiou) where a slave's manumission was confirmed. Upon acquittal, the freed person dedicated a silver bowl (phiale) weighing 100 drachmas to Athena. The text lists the freed person's name, their occupation (such as wool-worker, farmer, or shopkeeper), their place of residence, and the name of their former master who acted as their legal representative (prostates). The stoichedon 17 style is characteristic of Athenian public inscriptions of this period.
