It records the resolution of a long-standing territorial dispute between Lato and Olous. The process involved arbitration by Knossos and was eventually overseen by a Roman commission led by Quintus Fabius Maximus. The text includes detailed boundary descriptions, financial penalties for non-compliance (talents of Alexander), and instructions for the public display of the decree in major sanctuaries of the involved cities. It reflects the increasing role of Rome as an arbitrator in Greek interstate affairs during the late 2nd century BC.
