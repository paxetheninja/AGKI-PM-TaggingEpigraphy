AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to 70 BC, preserves a petition from Philippos, a Corinthian serving as a high-ranking official (chiliarch and 'friend of the king') in Ptolemaic Egypt. He requests that King Ptolemy XII and Queen Cleopatra Tryphaena grant the right of asylum (asylia) to two temples in the village of Theadelphia: the temple of Isis Eseremphis and the temple of Herakles Kallinikos. The text details the royal approval and the subsequent order sent to Apollonios, the strategos of the Arsinoite nome, to enforce this sanctity. It specifically forbids illegal entry (eisbiazesthai) and the harassment of priests or those seeking refuge. The inscription was intended to be displayed on stone steles at the temple entrances to serve as a public legal notice of the temples' protected status.
