AI Analysis Summary

This inscription consists of three decrees issued by the people of Adramyttion (in Mysia) to honor a judicial commission from Andros. The commission, led by the judge Timokritos and the secretary Iphikrates, was invited to settle legal disputes. The text praises their impartiality and adherence to the laws. A significant historical detail is the mention of the Roman official Gnaeus Aufidius (antistrategos), which dates the text to the late 2nd or early 1st century BC and illustrates the interaction between Greek cities and Roman provincial administration. The honors granted include gold crowns, bronze and painted statues, and public hospitality in the prytaneion.
