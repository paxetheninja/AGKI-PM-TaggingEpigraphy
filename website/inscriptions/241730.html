This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic manumission decree dating to the late 2nd century BC. It follows the standard formula: dating by local archons (Damon in Delphi, Aristodemos in Amphissa), the 'sale' of the slave Bakchos (a Theban by birth) to Apollo Pythios for 6 silver minae, the declaration of his freedom (eleutheros), and the naming of a guarantor (Sokrates) and witnesses. The text is well-preserved and provides significant prosopographical data for both Delphi and Amphissa.
