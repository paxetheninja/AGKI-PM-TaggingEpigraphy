This inscription is a large stone stele from the Sanctuary of Asclepius at Epidauros, dating to the first half of the 3rd century BC. It records a series of proxeny decrees issued by the Epidaurian state. Each entry typically begins with the month (Pellaios), the date, and the presiding council members. The decrees grant the status of 'proxenos' (diplomatic representative), 'euergetes' (benefactor), and 'thearodokos' (official host for sacred envoys announcing the Asclepieia games) to various foreigners from across the Greek world, including Macedonians, Cretans, and Peloponnesians. The text also specifies privileges such as 'ateleia' (tax exemption) and 'asylia' (inviolability) for the honorands and their descendants.
