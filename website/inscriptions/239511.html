AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to 138 BC during the archonship of Timokrates. It documents the sale of a male slave named Soteros by Euangelos and Charixa to the god Apollo Pythios for a sum of six minae of silver. This 'sale to the god' was the standard legal mechanism in Delphi to ensure the slave's permanent freedom (eleutheria) and protection from future seizure (anaphaptos). The text includes a list of officials (archons, prytaneis, secretary) and witnesses to validate the transaction. The presence of square brackets in the transcription indicates that parts of the text are restored from fragments.
