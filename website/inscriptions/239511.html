This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to 138 BC during the archonship of Timokrates. It documents the sale of a male slave named Soteros by Euangelos and Charixa to Apollo Pythios for six silver minae. The text follows the standard Delphic formula, ensuring the slave's status as 'eleutheros' (free) and 'anaphaptos' (immune from seizure) for life. It includes a list of witnesses, including the priests of Apollo and the city's archons, and names Eidouros as the legal guarantor (bebaioter) of the transaction.
