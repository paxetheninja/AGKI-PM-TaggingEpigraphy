🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major honorific decree from the city of Priene for a prominent citizen named Krates. Dated to the early 1st century BC, it provides a detailed account of his career as a diplomat and legal advocate. Krates served as an ambassador (presbeutes) to Roman officials, including Gaius Julius Caesar (the father of the dictator) and Lucius Lucilius, and acted as a public advocate (ekdikos) in disputes against the publicani (tax farmers) and neighboring cities like Miletus and Erythrae. The decree also records his financial generosity as an agonothetes (festival organizer) and his religious piety. The text concludes with the grant of high honors, including a gold crown and a public funeral, and orders the decree to be inscribed on the Sacred Stoa.
