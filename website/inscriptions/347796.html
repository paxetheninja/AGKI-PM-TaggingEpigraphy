It records honors granted by the Athenian state to the people of Tenos (the Tenians) for their continued support and virtue. The honors include a gold crown worth 1,000 drachmae and the granting of 'isotelia' (equal tax status) to Tenians residing in Athens. The decree also honors Tenian ambassadors and orders the publication of the text on the Acropolis. This document is a significant source for Athenian foreign policy and its relations with the Cycladic islands during the Hellenistic period.
