AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of the Athenian 'Traditio' (handover) records. It dates to 418/7 BCE and belongs to the series of inventories of the Treasurers of Athena. It specifically lists the items kept in the Pronaos (front porch) of the Parthenon. The text follows a strict formula: naming the outgoing treasurers and their secretary, the incoming board, and then a list of items (silver bowls, horns, cups, and a gold crown) with their weights recorded in the acrophonic numeral system. The stoichedon 61 layout is characteristic of high-classical Athenian public inscriptions.
