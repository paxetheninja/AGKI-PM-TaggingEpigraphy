This inscription is an Athenian honorary decree dated to the archonship of Euthykritos (328/7 BC). It honors Dendrokles, son of Kleinos, from the deme of Kerameis, for his exemplary service as the priest of Asclepius. The text details his diligent performance of sacrifices and his oversight of the sanctuary and the theater. The decree grants him a golden crown worth 500 drachmas and orders the inscription to be set up on a stone stele in the Asklepieion. The text is highly formulaic, following the standard Athenian stoichedon style of the late 4th century BC.
