This inscription is a classic Delphic manumission record dating to the mid-2nd century BC. It follows the 'sale to the god' formula where the slave Soterichos, identified as being of Armenian origin (Armenios), is sold to Apollo Pythios by the sons of Diodoros (Antigenes, Mnasitheos, and Kallikrates) for 18 minae. The legal act ensures Soterichos's freedom (eleutheron) and protection from seizure (anaphapton). The text includes a detailed list of officials (Archon Damostratos), guarantors (bebaioteres), and witnesses, including the priests of Apollo, which is characteristic of the legal transparency required for such acts in the Delphic sanctuary.
