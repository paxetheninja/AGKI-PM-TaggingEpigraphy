This inscription belongs to a large series of records found in the Prytaneion of Ephesus, dating to the Roman Imperial period (2nd-3rd century AD). It documents the names of citizens and officials who performed ritual sacrifices (ethysan) during specific months of the Ephesian calendar (e.g., Kaisareion, Neokaisareion, Maimakterion). The lists are dated by the eponymous magistrate, the Prytanis, including the well-known Julia Lydia Laterane. The recurring mention of M. The presence of 'hierologoi' (sacred heralds) further emphasizes the official religious and administrative nature of the document.
