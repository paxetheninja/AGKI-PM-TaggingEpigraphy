AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a prytany decree and catalogue from Athens, dated to the archonship of Euxenippos (305/4 BC). It honors the prytaneis of the tribe Akamantis. The text includes the publication formula directing the treasurer to pay for the engraving of the stele and its placement in front of the Bouleuterion. The list of names is organized by demes belonging to Akamantis, such as Cholargos, Kephale, and Thorikos. Several known Athenian officials of the late 4th century BC are identified, including the herald Eukles and the secretary Autolykos.
