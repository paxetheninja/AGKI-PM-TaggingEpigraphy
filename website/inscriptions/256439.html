AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Hellenistic diplomatic letter from the Cretan city of Kydonia to the Ionian city of Teos. It belongs to a large corpus of similar documents from the late 3rd or early 2nd century BC, where various Greek cities recognized the 'asylia' (inviolability) of Teos and its territory, which was consecrated to the god Dionysos. The text mentions the arrival of Teian ambassadors, Polyadotos and Kolotas, who presented a decree and successfully advocated for the recognition of their city's sacred status. The inscription also includes a clause regarding the protection of Teian residents from potential harm by Kydonians.
