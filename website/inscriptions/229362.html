🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This is the Greek version of the Decree of Canopus (238 BC), a trilingual decree (Hieroglyphic, Demotic, and Greek) issued by a synod of Egyptian priests. It honors Ptolemy III Euergetes and Berenice II for their benefactions, including the return of sacred statues from Persia and famine relief. It also deifies their deceased daughter Berenice and introduces a significant calendar reform by adding an intercalary day every four years to the Egyptian civil calendar. The text provided contains several lacunae restored by editors.
