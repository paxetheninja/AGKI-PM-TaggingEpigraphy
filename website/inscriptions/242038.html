This inscription is a formal decree from the city of Delphi dating to approximately 118 BC, during the archonship of Amyntas. It honors two brothers, Kleodoros and Thrasyboulos, for their musical performances (epideixeis) dedicated to Apollo and their role in teaching local children. The decree follows the standard Delphic diplomatic formula, granting the honorees 'proxenia' (the right to represent Delphic interests), 'promanteia' (priority in consulting the oracle), 'prodikia' (right of priority in legal trials), and 'asylia' (inviolability). The text is significant for documenting the intersection of civic honors, religious performance, and education in the Hellenistic period.
