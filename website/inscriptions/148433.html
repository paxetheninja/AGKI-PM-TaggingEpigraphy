AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IG IX,2 517) is a crucial historical document from Larisa, Thessaly, dated to 214 BC. It preserves a dossier concerning the expansion of citizenship in the city. King Philip V of Macedon writes to the Larisaeans twice, urging them to grant citizenship to resident aliens (Thessalians and other Greeks) to replenish the population depleted by wars and to ensure the land is cultivated. Notably, Philip V cites the Roman example of enfranchising freedmen as a successful model for increasing state power. The text includes the king's letters, the city's decrees (written in the local Thessalian dialect), and a long list of the new citizens categorized by their city of origin. The stelae were to be set up in the sanctuaries of Apollo Kerdoios and Athena on the acropolis.
