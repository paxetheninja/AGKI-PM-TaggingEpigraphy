This inscription (IG XIV 760) from Neapolis, dated to 71 AD, contains three distinct decrees (psephismata) passed by the city council in honor of Tettia Casta. The first decree acknowledges her piety (eusebeia) and her donation of silver statues to the gods, granting her a public statue and a public funeral. The second decree confirms the public funding for her burial rites. The third decree specifies the exact location for her tomb near the city walls. The text is highly significant for its prosopography, listing several local officials (archons and antarchons) and providing precise dating through the mention of Roman consuls, including the future emperor Domitian and suffect consuls Flavius Fimbria and Atilius Barbarus.
