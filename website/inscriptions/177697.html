This inscription (IG XIV 760) is a significant public document from Neapolis, dating to 71 AD (based on the consulships of Domitian, Valerius Festus, and the suffect consuls Fimbria and Barbarus). It records consolatory decrees passed by the local senate (proskletos) honoring Tettia Casta, a priestess of a specific house (oikou dia biou). The text details the legislative procedure: a proposal by the antiarchon Tranquillius Rufus, followed by a vote to grant a public funeral (dapane demosiai), a statue (andrianti), and a burial plot (topon eis kedeian). The inscription concludes with a private dedication by her sons, the Domitii Lepidi. The text is fragmentary, with several lacunae restored by editors.
