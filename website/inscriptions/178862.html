AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a victor list from the Isthmian and Caesarean games held in Corinth around 3 AD. It records the names, patronymics, and ethnic origins of winners across three age categories: boys (paides), beardless youths (ageneioi), and men (andres). The events listed include traditional athletic contests like the stadion, diaulos, pentathlon, wrestling, boxing, and pankration, as well as equestrian events and artistic competitions for heralds, poets, and musicians. The text mentions the agonothetes Marcus Servilius and Lucius, and references the victory of Caesar, likely Augustus.
