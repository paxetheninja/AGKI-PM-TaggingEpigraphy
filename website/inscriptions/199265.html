AI Analysis Summary

This 2nd-century BC inscription from Lebena, Crete, is a decree (psephisma) regulating the administration of the sanctuary of Asclepius. It focuses on the inventory and transfer of sacred property (skeua), listing various ceramic vessels such as kanastra (baskets), chutrai (pots), and skyphoi (cups). It defines the responsibilities of the neokoros (temple warden) and the hierorgos (sacred official), including financial accountability (apologismos) for temple revenues and expenditures, such as payments to a potter (kerameus). The mention of Menedemos likely serves as a chronological marker for the year of the decree.
