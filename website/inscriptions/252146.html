🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Eudemos Decree' (Milet I 3, 145), dates to 200/199 BC. It records a significant private endowment by Eudemos, son of Thallon, for the public education of free children in Miletus. The text is a primary source for Hellenistic educational administration, detailing the hiring of four paidotribai (physical trainers) and four grammatodidaskaloi (literacy teachers), their monthly salaries (30 and 40 drachmas respectively), and the religious rituals involving Apollo Delphinios, Hermes, and the Muses that accompanied the educational year.
