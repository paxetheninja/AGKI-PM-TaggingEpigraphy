AI Analysis Summary

This extensive inscription from the late 2nd century BC documents a comprehensive treaty of alliance (isopoliteia) between Hierapytna and Lato. It covers military cooperation, mutual legal rights (marriage and property ownership), detailed territorial boundaries, and trade regulations involving Alexandrian silver currency. The document concludes with a solemn religious oath invoking the major deities of the Cretan pantheon and specifies the locations where the stelae recording this agreement were to be erected, including the sanctuary of Athena Polias at Lyttos and the sanctuary of the Twelve Gods.
