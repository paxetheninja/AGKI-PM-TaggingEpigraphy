This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic manumission record dating to the Roman Imperial period (ca. 75 AD). It documents the sale of a slave girl named Aristoboula to the god Apollo by her owners Thesia, Aphrodeisia, and Sosikles for the sum of 20 silver minae. The text includes the standard Delphic dating formula (archonship of Euthydamos), the legal conditions of freedom (being 'free and untouchable' for life), and a list of witnesses. The presence of individuals with the nomen 'Tiberius Claudius' reflects the Roman citizenship granted to local elites in Phokis during the 1st century AD.
