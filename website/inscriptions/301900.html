This inscription is an honorific decree from Pergamon, likely dating to the Roman period. It honors Epistratos, a high priest (archiereus), for his exceptional virtue and generosity. The decree mandates the erection of a bronze statue (eikon chalke) and an iconic image (agalma eikonikon) in the Hermaion. It also details the ritual honors to be performed, including sacrifices during the Hermaia festival and the decoration of the statue with fillets (tainiai) by the gymnasiarch and hypogymnasiarch. The text is heavily restored but follows standard Pergamene epigraphic formulas for civic honors.
