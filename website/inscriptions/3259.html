AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant ephebic decree from Athens, dated to the early 1st century BCE (c. 83-78 BCE). It follows the standard formula for such documents: an opening invocation to the gods, the naming of the archon, the decree of the Boule praising the kosmetes (Dailos of Lamprai) for his religious and administrative diligence, and a detailed list of the ephebes who served under him. The list is organized by the ten Cleisthenic tribes plus the later additions (Antiochis, Attalis). Notably, it includes foreign royalty (the sons of King Ariobarzanes II of Cappadocia), reflecting the international prestige of the Athenian ephebeia during the Hellenistic period.
