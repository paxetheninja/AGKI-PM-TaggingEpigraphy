AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from the sanctuary of Zeus Panamaros and Hera near Stratonikeia (Caria) records the extensive liturgical and financial contributions of Kleoboulos and Iason. During their priesthood, they provided public banquets (pandaisia) for the entire population, funded the gymnasiarchy by providing oil and scented unguents for the baths, and undertook the expensive marble veneering (skoutlosis) of the temple of Hera and the Hadrianeion. The text is a classic example of Hellenistic/Roman imperial euergetism, where elite families competed for social prestige through massive public spending on religious and civic infrastructure.
