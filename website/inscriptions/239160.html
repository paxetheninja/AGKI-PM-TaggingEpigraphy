AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, found at Delphi and dated to the early 3rd century BC, represents a judicial convention (symbolon) between the Delphians and the Pellanians (of Achaia). It is a highly technical legal document that outlines the mechanisms for resolving private disputes between citizens of the two states. It covers the appointment of judges, the administration of oaths (horkos), the presentation of evidence and witnesses (martyria), and specific regulations for cases involving theft (klops) and the status of free persons versus slaves (andrapoda). The text is fragmentary but preserves significant details of Hellenistic international law and interstate arbitration.
