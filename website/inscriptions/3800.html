This inscription belongs to the series of 'Poletai' records from Athens, dating to the mid-4th century BC. It documents the leasing of silver mines in the Laurion region. The text specifies the names of the lessees (e.g., Phanostratos, Aristoteles), the classification of the mines (such as 'palaion anasaximon' - an old mine being reopened, or 'ergasimon' - a workable mine), and their precise topographical locations using demes and neighboring landmarks like workshops (ergasteria) or other mines.
