AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Samian decree from the early 3rd century BC (Ptolemaic period) honoring foreign judges from Myndos. The judges, Theokles and Hierophantos, were invited to resolve outstanding legal disputes (meteora symbolaia) between Samians and citizens of neighboring Ionian and Carian cities (Miletus, Myndos, Halicarnassus). Philokles, the King of Sidon and a high-ranking Ptolemaic official, played a diplomatic role in the arrangement. The decree grants the judges various honors, including golden crowns, proxeny, and Samian citizenship, and orders the text to be inscribed on a stone stele in the Heraion of Samos. The text is largely complete, preserving the preamble, the narrative of the judges' actions, the list of honors, and the publication instructions.
