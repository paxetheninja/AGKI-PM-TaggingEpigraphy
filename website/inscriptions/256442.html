AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (LW 60) is a significant historical document dating to approximately 193 BC. It contains a letter from the Roman Praetor Marcus Valerius Messalla and the Roman Senate to the city of Teos. The letter responds to an embassy led by Menippos, an envoy of King Antiochus III the Great. The primary purpose of the document is the Roman recognition of Teos as 'hiera' (sacred), 'asylos' (inviolable), and 'aphorologetos' (tax-exempt). This was part of a broader diplomatic effort by Teos to secure its status from major powers during the Hellenistic period, particularly given its importance as the seat of the Dionysiac Artists. The text provided contains several lacunae where letters are missing, hence the classification as fragmentary.
