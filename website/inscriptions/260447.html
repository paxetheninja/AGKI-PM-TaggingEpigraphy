AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IMagn 16) is a foundational document for the history of Magnesia on the Maeander in the late 3rd century BC (c. 208 BC). It recounts the epiphany of Artemis Leukophryene and the subsequent oracular confirmation from Apollo at Delphi that the city and its territory should be considered sacred and inviolable (asylia). The text serves as a historical record of the city's diplomatic efforts to have this status recognized by other Greek states and kings, and it establishes the crown games (stephanitic games) known as the Leukophryena. It uses Athenian archons and Olympic victors, such as Hegesidemos of Messene, to provide a precise chronological framework for these religious and political developments.
