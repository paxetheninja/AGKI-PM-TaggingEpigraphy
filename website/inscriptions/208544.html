This inscription is a judicial curse tablet (defixio) from Amathous, Cyprus, dating to the late 3rd century AD. It belongs to a well-known corpus of lead tablets from the site. The text employs standard magical praxis, including the invocation of chthonic deities (Pluto, Kore, and 'silent demons') and the use of 'voces magicae' (e.g., 'ablanathanalba'). The primary goal is to 'bind' (katadesate) and 'muzzle' (phimotikon) the tongues and spirits of legal adversaries (antidikoi) to prevent them from opposing the curser in a legal matter.
