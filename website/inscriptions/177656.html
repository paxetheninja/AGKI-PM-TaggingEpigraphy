AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant honorific decree from the phratry of the Artemisians in Neapolis, dated to 194 AD. It records the phratry's decision to honor L. Munatius Hilarianus for his lavish benefactions, which included the architectural renovation of their meeting house (oikos) with expensive marbles and a gilded ceiling, as well as the provision of a banquet. The text is historically important for its mention of the joint rule of Septimius Severus and Clodius Albinus, the latter of whom suffered damnatio memoriae as evidenced by the erasure of his name. Uniquely, the inscription also preserves the response of Hilarianus, who accepts the honors but requests a reduction in the number of statues and images to be erected, demonstrating a performance of civic modesty (sophrosyne).
