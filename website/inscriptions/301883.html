AI Analysis Summary

This significant inscription (OGIS 335) from Pergamon records the arbitration of a territorial dispute between Pitane and Mytilene. The text is a complex legal and historical document that outlines the mediation process led by five Pergamene ambassadors. It provides crucial historical evidence for the early Hellenistic period, including the sale of the 'Pedion' territory by Antiochus I to Pitane for 330 talents, the financial support of Philetaerus, and references to the victory of Seleucus I over Lysimachus. The latter part of the inscription describes the physical demarcation of the territory using boundary stones (stelae) and concludes with honors for the mediators.
