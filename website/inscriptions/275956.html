AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Side, Pamphylia (ca. 220-240 AD), honors Aurelius Mandrianus Longinus for his extensive civic and religious career. He served as a councilor, high priest of the imperial cult, and held numerous magistracies including agoranomos, eirenarch, and archisitonos. The text is particularly valuable for its mention of gladiatorial games (monomachiai) and beast hunts (kynigesia) funded by the legacy of the consular Licinius Mucianus. It also references his role in the transport of military supplies (annona). The statue was erected by his wife, Aurelia Killaramotiane, as a testament to her devotion (philandria).
