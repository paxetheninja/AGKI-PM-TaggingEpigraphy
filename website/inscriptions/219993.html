This inscription, known as the Edict of Gnaeus Vergilius Capito (48 AD), is a crucial document for understanding Roman provincial administration in Egypt. It was inscribed on the gateway of the Temple of Hibis in the Kharga Oasis. The text addresses systemic corruption, specifically the illegal requisitioning of transport and hospitality (angareia) by soldiers and officials. It establishes strict penalties (tenfold and fourfold fines) for those who extort the local population and mandates that all financial accounts be submitted to the imperial auditor Basileides. The mention of Emperor Claudius provides a firm date, despite the metadata's '48 BC' typo.
