This inscription is a decree passed by the soldiers (stratiotai) and those stationed in the open air (hypaithroi) at the fortress of Rhamnous. It honors Theotimos, son of Theodoros of Rhamnous, who served as general (strategos) of the coastal region (Paralia) six times. The text details his administrative and military care, including the maintenance of garrisons, the safety of the countryside, and ensuring the soldiers received their grain and pay (opsonia). He is also praised for his religious piety in performing sacrifices with the troops. The decree dates to the archonship of Kallistratos (207/206 BC), a period of significant military activity in Hellenistic Attica.
