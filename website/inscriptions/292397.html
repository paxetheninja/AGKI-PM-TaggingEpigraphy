AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Athenian ephebic decree from the late 3rd century BC (archonship of Polydorus/Piodotus). It follows a standard structure: a preamble dating the decree by archon and secretary, a narrative section detailing the pious and military activities of the ephebes (including sacrifices, processions to Eleusis, and military training), a formal resolution to honor them and their supervisors, and finally a catalogue of the ephebes listed by their tribes. The text is fragmentary but largely restorable through parallel inscriptions.
