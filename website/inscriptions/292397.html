AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian ephebic decree dating to the late 3rd century BC (archonship of Apollodoros, 204/3 BC). It honors the ephebes who served during the previous year (archonship of Diodotos, 205/4 BC) for their participation in religious sacrifices, processions (including the Panathenaia and Eleusinia), and their military training. The decree highlights their discipline (eutaxia) and piety (eusebeia). It also honors their kosmetes, Nikoboulos, and their paidotribes, Harmodoros. The text concludes with a detailed list of the ephebes categorized by their tribes, which is a standard feature of such monumental records in Hellenistic Athens.
