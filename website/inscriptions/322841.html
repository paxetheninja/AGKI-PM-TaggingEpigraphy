AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IG XII,9 7) is a significant financial document from Karystos, Euboea, dating to approximately 370 BC. It records the repayment of principal and interest on loans contracted by the city from various foreign lenders, primarily from Thebes and Histiaia. The text provides a detailed look at the administrative procedures of the Karystian state, naming specific archons (Archestratos and Kleomedon) and boards of treasurers (tamiai) who managed the public funds. The use of acrophonic numerals and the mention of specific interest rates (epigdoon) highlight the complexity of inter-city banking and credit in the 4th century BC.
