AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the annual inventory of the treasures of Athena kept in the Pronaos of the Parthenon for the year 417/6 BCE. It follows the standard Athenian formula for such records, listing the outgoing and incoming boards of treasurers (Tamiai of the Sacred Money of Athena) and their secretaries. The text meticulously catalogs silver and gold vessels, including phialai, drinking cups (poteria), and a crown (stephanos), recording the weight of each item or group of items. The use of the stoichedon style and the specific demotics of the officials are characteristic of 5th-century Attic administrative epigraphy.
