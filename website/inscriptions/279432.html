AI Analysis Summary

This 2nd-century AD inscription from Klaudioupolis (modern Bolu) is a sophisticated funerary epigram for a woman named Tertulla, daughter of Krateros. The text is composed in high-style Greek verse, utilizing traditional epic and lyric vocabulary to extol the deceased's virtues, including 'sophrosyne' (prudence) and 'dikaiosyne' (justice). It reflects the cultural milieu of the Second Sophistic in Bithynia, where local elites used elaborate Greek epigraphy to signal their education and status. The poem concludes with a philosophical reflection on the soul (psyche) departing to the 'unbroken home' of the air (aither), contrasting the physical decay of the body in the earth with the immortality of the spirit.
