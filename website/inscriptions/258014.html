AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (GIBM 897) is a public decree from Halicarnassus concerning the financing and construction of a stoa dedicated to Apollo and King Ptolemy (likely Ptolemy II Philadelphus). The text outlines the administrative process for managing loans provided by citizens (pro-daneistai) to fund the building. It specifies that the names of these creditors should be inscribed on the doorpost (parastas) of the stoa as a public record. The decree involves various officials, including treasurers (tamiai) and supervisors (epimeletai), and mentions the sale of surplus construction materials like columns and tiles to support the project's economy.
