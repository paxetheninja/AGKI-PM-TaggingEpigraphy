This inscription is a significant fragment of the Athenian naval catalogues (tabulae curatorum navalium), specifically IG IIÂ² 1614, dating to 353/2 BC. It documents the inventory of the Athenian dockyards (neoria), listing the 'skeue' (equipment) for specific triremes. Ships are identified by the shipwright who built them (e.g., 'Mentos' work', 'Hierokles' work'). The text meticulously records the presence and condition of wooden equipment like rudders (pedalia), ladders (klimakides), and poles (kontoi), as well as hanging gear like sails and anchors. The term 'dokimos' indicates that the equipment was inspected and found fit for service.
