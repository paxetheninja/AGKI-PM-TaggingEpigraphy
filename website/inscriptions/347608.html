AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant ephebic decree from the mid-2nd century BC Athens. It records the honors bestowed upon the ephebes of the year of the archon Eucharistos (172/1 BC) and their supervisor (kosmetes), Aiantides of Paiania. The text provides a detailed account of the ephebic curriculum, including religious participation in major festivals (Dionysia, Panathenaia, Eleusinian Mysteries), military training (marches to Marathon, naval exercises at Salamis, and catapult practice), and civic engagement. The inclusion of a complete list of ephebes by tribe makes it a vital prosopographical source for Hellenistic Athens.
