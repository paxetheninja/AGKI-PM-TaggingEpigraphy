AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a typical Delphic manumission act dating to the late 2nd century BC. It records the sale of a female slave named Sophrona and her son Heracleidas by a woman named Estia to Apollo Pythios. The 'sale' is a legal fiction used to grant the slaves their freedom (eleutheria). The text specifies that they are to be free and 'anepaptoi' (untouchable/free from seizure) for all time. It names the archon Damosthenes, the guarantor Thambos, and several witnesses, including priests of Apollo, which provides a precise chronological and social context within the sanctuary of Delphi.
