AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dating to the late Ptolemaic period (69/68 BC), records a petition from Dionysodoros, an Athenian resident of Euhemeria, to King Ptolemy XII. Dionysodoros requests permission to restore a ruined temple of Ammon and the 'synnaoi theoi' at his own expense. Crucially, he asks for the temple to be granted the right of asylia (inviolability/sanctuary) to protect its staff (pastophoroi) and those seeking refuge. The king's official, Athridis, approves the request, and the scribe Ptolemaios records the transaction. The text was intended to be displayed on a stone stele to serve as a public record of the temple's new status and the benefactor's piety.
