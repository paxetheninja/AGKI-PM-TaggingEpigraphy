This inscription is a 2nd-century AD honorific decree from the city of Delphi for Sosandros, son of Pleistarchos, a citizen of Patara. Sosandros is praised for his piety (eusebeia) and his distinguished service as an agonothetes and as an epimeletes of the Amphictyonic League. In recognition of his contributions, the city grants him several prestigious privileges: promanteia (priority in consulting the oracle), proedria (reserved seating at games), ateleia (tax exemption), asylia (inviolability), and enktesis (the right to own land and a house in Delphi). The decree also mandates the erection of a bronze statue of Sosandros in the sanctuary of Pythian Apollo and orders that copies of the decree be sent to his home city of Patara and to the Thessalian League. The text is heavily reconstructed but follows standard Delphic epigraphic formulas for the Roman imperial period.
