This inscription consists of two distinct manumission records from Delphi. The first record details the sale of a female slave named Zoila by Nikarchos, son of Arton, to Apollo Pythios. The second record describes the sale of a slave named Hodopis. Both documents follow the standard Delphic formula where the slave is 'sold' to the god to achieve legal freedom (eleutheria) and protection from future claims (asylia). The text includes dating by local archons (Kall... and Laidas), mentions of the council (boule), and specific conditions such as the 'paramone' clause, which required the manumitted person to remain in service for a specified period.
