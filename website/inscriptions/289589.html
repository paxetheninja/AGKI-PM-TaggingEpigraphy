This inscription is a decree from the city of Apollonia ad Rhyndacum, found in Miletus and dating to the mid-2nd century BC. It records the renewal of diplomatic relations and the reaffirmation of 'syngeneia' (ancestral kinship) between the two cities. The text highlights that Apollonia was a colony (apoikos) of Miletus, a fact confirmed by the Milesians after examining historical records (historias) and documents (eggrapha). It specifically mentions the role of Apollo Didymeus as the divine guide of the original colonial expedition to the Hellespont and Propontis regions. The decree concludes with the decision to send ambassadors to Miletus to deliver the resolution and perform sacrifices.
