This inscription is a decree from Delphi dating to 86 BC, honoring Polygnota, daughter of Sokrates, a professional harpist from Thebes. The text explains that due to the 'present war' (the First Mithridatic War), the Pythian Games could not be conducted as usual. Polygnota performed voluntarily for three days, demonstrating piety and skill. In recognition, the city awarded her 500 drachmas, a crown, and the status of proxenos (public guest/representative), along with other privileges like priority in consulting the oracle (promanteia). It is a significant document for understanding the role of female musicians and the impact of Roman-Pontic conflicts on Greek religious life.
