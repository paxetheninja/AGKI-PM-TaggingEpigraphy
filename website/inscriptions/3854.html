AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the Athenian Naval Catalogues (IG II² 1631), dating to the archonship of Cephisodorus (323/2 BCE). It records the administrative oversight of the Athenian fleet, specifically the transfer and debt of naval equipment (skeue) for triremes and quadriremes. It identifies numerous trierarchs and syntrierarchs by name and deme, lists specific ship names (e.g., Soizousa, Paralia, Eunoia), and cites the Law of Diphilos as the legal basis for the transactions. The text provides critical evidence for the logistics and financial administration of the Athenian navy at the end of the 4th century BCE.
