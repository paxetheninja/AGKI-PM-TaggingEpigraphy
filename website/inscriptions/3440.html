AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an honorific decree from the mid-3rd century BCE (c. 250 BCE) issued by the Salaminians to honor Herakleitos, son of Asklepiades, of the deme Athmonon. Herakleitos served as the Macedonian-appointed general (strategos) of the Piraeus under King Antigonos II Gonatas. The text details his benevolence toward the people of Salamis, including his efforts to rebuild the island's walls and protect the territory from pirates during the conflict involving Alexander of Corinth. It also mentions his role in ransoming a captive and ensuring justice for the inhabitants. The inscription provides significant evidence for the administrative and military control of Attica by the Macedonians during the Hellenistic period.
