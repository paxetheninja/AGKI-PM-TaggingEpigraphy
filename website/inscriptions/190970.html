This inscription from Lindos, Rhodes, dating to approximately 115 BC, represents a significant subscription list (epidosis). It records the names of citizens and residents who contributed funds for the manufacture of golden crowns dedicated to Athena Lindia and Zeus Polieus. The text is structured as a series of entries listing the primary donor (usually a male citizen), his patronymic, and his deme, followed by contributions made on behalf of his wife, children, and sometimes parents. The preamble establishes the religious and civic motivation, citing piety (eusebeia) and the fulfillment of a decree (dedogmena). The document is a vital source for Rhodian prosopography and the social history of religious financing in the late Hellenistic period.
