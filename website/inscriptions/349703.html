🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant historical document from the early Hellenistic period (ca. 300 BC) found at the Asklepieion of Kos. It records a decree concerning the reconciliation (dialysis) of the people of Telos, who were experiencing internal civil strife or legal disputes. The Koans acted as intermediaries, requesting arbitrators (diallaktai) from Keos to resolve the conflicts. The text details the settlement of sacred and public debts, the restoration of democratic governance, and the imposition of a civic oath to prevent future sedition. It also highlights the religious dimension of civic life through the repair of the altar of Asclepius and the regulation of sacrifices.
