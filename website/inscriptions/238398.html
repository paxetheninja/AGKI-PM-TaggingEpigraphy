AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant administrative document from the 4th century BC, specifically the accounts of the Delphic naopoioi (temple builders) for the year 335/4 BC. It details the financial management of the reconstruction of the Temple of Apollo after its destruction in 373 BC. The text lists payments for specialized labor (architects, stone-cutters, woodworkers), materials (ivory, tin, cypress wood), and transport costs from ports like Kirra and Sicyon. It provides insight into the pan-Hellenic cooperation involved in the project, mentioning contractors from Athens, Thespiae, and Argos.
