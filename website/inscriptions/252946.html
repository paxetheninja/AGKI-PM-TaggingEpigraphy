🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This extensive honorific decree from Priene (IPri 108) dates to the late 2nd century BC. It honors Moschion, son of Kydamos, a prominent citizen who performed numerous liturgies, including serving as a stephanephoros, theoros, and ambassador. The text is historically significant for its mention of the Roman consul Marcus Perperna (130 BC) and his victory in Asia, likely against Aristonicus. It details Moschion's financial generosity during grain shortages (sitometria), his diplomatic missions to Hellenistic kings (Demetrius, Ptolemy) and cities (Magnesia, Tralles, Kibyra), and even a mission to Petra in Arabia. The decree concludes with the grant of high honors: golden crowns, statues, and specific instructions for his funeral procession involving the city's youth and officials.
