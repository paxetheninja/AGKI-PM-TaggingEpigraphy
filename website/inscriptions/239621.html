This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic manumission by fictive sale to Apollo Pythios, dated to the archonship of Sosipatros (ca. 139 BC). It details the manumission of Glaukias, a Dardanian slave, by Mithridates for the price of five minae. The document includes a 'paramone' clause, stipulating that Glaukias must serve Mithridates as long as he lives, after which he will be fully free and inherit Mithridates' belongings, provided he performs the customary funeral rites. The text lists various Delphic officials and witnesses, ensuring the legal validity of the act under the laws of the polis.
