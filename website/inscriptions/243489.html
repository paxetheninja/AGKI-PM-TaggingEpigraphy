This inscription, dated to 214 AD by the consulship of Messalla and Sabinus, serves as a career summary for the athlete Aurelius Septimius Eirenaios. It follows the standard format of agonistic inscriptions, listing victories in chronological or prestige order. Eirenaios competed in the 'boys' (paidon), 'youths' (geneion), and 'men' (andron) categories. The text is significant for its extensive list of cities and specific games (Actia, Pythia, Sebasmia), illustrating the vibrant athletic culture and the mobility of professional competitors in the 3rd century AD. The presence of local dating formulas at the end (Xandikos, Gretus, and Seleucus) provides valuable data for the local calendar and administration of Laodicea.
