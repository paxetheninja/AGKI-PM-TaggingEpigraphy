This inscription is part of the well-known series of financial accounts from Tauromenion (modern Taormina), Sicily. These documents, typically inscribed on the walls of the bouleuterion or a similar public building, record the monthly financial activities of the city's magistrates during the Hellenistic period (2nd-1st century BC). The text meticulously tracks the 'eisodos' (income), 'exodos' (expenditure), and 'loipon' (remainder/balance) for different administrative bodies: the Hieromnamones (sacred treasurers), Tamiai (general treasurers), and Sitophylakes (grain commissioners). It also mentions specific grain funds (siton) associated with individuals like Phronios and Eukleidas. The accounts are dated by the local month (e.g., Artemisios, Dionysios, Panamos) and the name of the proagoros (the eponymous chief magistrate). The use of 'litrai' alongside 'talanta' reflects the specific monetary system of Hellenistic Sicily.
