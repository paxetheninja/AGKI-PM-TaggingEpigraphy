AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian decree (psephisma) dating to the archonship of Iason (109/108 BC). It was found in Larisa, where a copy was erected to honor the city and its citizens who served as foreign judges. These judges (Pandokos, Theopropides, Philotas, Kaphisias, and Apollodoros) were sent to adjudicate legal disputes between Athens and Sicyon according to a judicial treaty (symbolon). The text praises their conduct and fairness, awarding them gold and olive crowns and inviting them to the public hearth (hestia) for hospitality (xenia). It provides significant evidence for inter-city judicial cooperation and diplomatic relations in the late Hellenistic period.
