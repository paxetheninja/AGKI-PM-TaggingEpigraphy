AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of a Delian temple inventory and financial account, likely from the Sanctuary of Apollo. It dates to the late 3rd century BC, specifically shortly after the archonships of Mynnios and Anchitheides. The text documents the administrative handover between boards of hieropoioi (sacred officials), listing the physical assets of the temple (phialai, crowns, statues) and the financial state of the sacred treasury, including outstanding loans and interest. The presence of dedications from Hellenistic monarchs like Ptolemy III and Antigonus II/III highlights the international religious and political significance of Delos during this period.
