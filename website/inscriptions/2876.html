This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Athens, dated to 283/2 BC (though the metadata suggests 287/6 BC, the archon Euthios is traditionally associated with the later date in some chronologies, or the text refers back to events in 287). It honors the comic poet Philippides of Kephale for his extensive diplomatic and financial support of Athens. The decree details his role as a mediator with King Lysimachos, his donation of grain (10,000 medimnoi), his provision of equipment for the Panathenaic festival, and his humanitarian efforts in burying Athenian dead and ransoming prisoners after the Battle of Ipsos (301 BC). The honors granted include a golden crown, a bronze statue in the theater, and sitesis (free meals) in the Prytaneion, illustrating the high value placed on elite benefaction during the Hellenistic period.
