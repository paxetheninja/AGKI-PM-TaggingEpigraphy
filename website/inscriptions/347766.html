It honors Herodoros, a friend and agent of the Macedonian kings Antigonos I and Demetrios Poliorketes, for his diplomatic assistance to Athens. The honors are exceptionally high, including a gold crown to be announced at the Dionysia, a bronze statue in the Agora (with the specific legal exclusion of being placed near the Tyrannicides), and the grant of Athenian citizenship (naturalization). The text provides insight into the complex relationship between Athens and the Diadochi during the early Hellenistic period.
