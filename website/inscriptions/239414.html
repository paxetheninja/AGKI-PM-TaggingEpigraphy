AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal letter from the archons of Elateia to the Delphic authorities, requesting the public recording of a manumission act. The subject, Laodike, was a war captive ransomed by Agon and Timandros. The document specifies her status as free and 'anaphaptos' (untouchable/not to be seized), subject to a 'paramone' clause requiring her to stay with Timandros for the duration of his life. It includes religious sanctions involving the sanctuaries of Asklepios in Elateia and Apollo in Delphi, and lists several witnesses, including priests and local officials.
