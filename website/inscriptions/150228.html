AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a civic decree from Battyna in Orestis, Macedonia, dated to 193 AD. It addresses a dispute over the use of public land (demosioi topoi). The citizens complained that 'eparchikoi' (provincials or non-local residents) were encroaching on public lands and displacing the poor (penetes). The decree, following an earlier regulation by Gentianus (likely the former governor Terentius Gentianus), restricts the use of public land to those who have been specifically assigned for it and forbids eparchikoi from purchasing or occupying it. It establishes a fine of 5000 denarii for non-compliance or official negligence. The document concludes with a long list of citizens who likely ratified the decision.
