AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Hyettos, Boiotia, dates to the period after the Constitutio Antoniniana (212 AD), as evidenced by the prevalence of the nomen 'Aurelius'. It records a decree by the 'sacred gerousia' of Asklepios Soter. The document serves multiple purposes: it records the donation of land plots (styphlarion and ampelikon) by Aurelius Aristeas and Aurelius Menekrates Eratonianos to the council, defines the boundaries of these lands, lists the current members of the council, and establishes hereditary rules for membership. The text is a significant source for the social and religious organization of local councils in Roman Greece.
