AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Delos, dated to 221 BC during the archonship of Aristoboulos, is a formal inventory of sacred silver vessels (argyromata) and ritual equipment. It documents the handover of these items between officials, specifically mentioning the transfer from Polystratos to his successor. The text lists a variety of objects including incense burners (thymiateria), wine jugs (oinochoai), and various types of cups (skaphia, phialai). Many of these are identified as votive offerings (anathema) dedicated by individuals to the sanctuary, including a reference to the 'Hieron of Hestia'. The document is a crucial source for understanding the administration of temple treasures and the prosopography of Hellenistic Delos.
