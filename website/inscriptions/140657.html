AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Tabula Halaesina' (IG XIV 352), is a major administrative document from the city of Halaesa in Sicily. It serves as a cadastre or land register, meticulously recording the boundaries of sacred land lots (klaroi) that were leased out to citizens. The text describes the topography using landmarks such as roads (hodoi), rivers (potamoi), towers (pyrgoi), and specific olive trees (elaiai), which are noted as being 'inscribed' or marked as part of the plot. It mentions several sanctuaries, including those of Apollo, Hadranos, and Zeus Meilichios, which served as topographical reference points. The document provides critical evidence for the agrarian economy, land tenure, and administrative organization of Hellenistic Sicily.
