AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Monumentum Ephesenum', contains the Greek translation of the Lex Portorii Asiae. It was set up in Ephesus in 62 AD during the reign of Nero. The text is a compilation of various regulations dating back to the Republican period (75 BC) and updated through the early Empire. It provides a detailed list of customs stations (paraphylakai) and cities across the province of Asia and neighboring regions, establishing the legal framework for the collection of the 2.5% customs tax by publicani.
