This inscription is a significant Athenian decree from the late 1st century BC (20/19 BC) found at the sanctuary of Demeter and Kore in Eleusis. It honors Themistokles of Hagnous, a member of the Kerykes family, for his tenure as Dadouchos (torch-bearer), one of the most prestigious priesthoods in the Eleusinian Mysteries. The text is particularly valuable for prosopography and the study of Athenian religious administration, as it provides a detailed genealogy of the priestly lineage and lists numerous contemporary religious officials. The decree emphasizes the continuity of the priesthood through hereditary succession and the 'megaloprepes' (magnificence) with which Themistokles performed the sacred rites.
