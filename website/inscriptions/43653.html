AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Amphissa, dating to the early 1st century AD, contains two distinct manumission records. The first record details the manumission of a home-born slave named Nikostratos by Zopyra, daughter of Menandros. The second record details the manumission of Nikostrata and her children by Sosicha, daughter of Zoos. Both acts are framed as a sale or dedication to the god Asclepius (Asklapios) and include a 'paramone' clause, requiring the freed persons to remain in service to their former owners until the owners' deaths. The documents are categorized as 'cheirographa' (private legal documents) and were deposited with the local priest (theokolos) for safekeeping and legal validity.
