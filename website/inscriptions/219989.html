AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a comprehensive decree from an association of landowners in Psenemphaia, Egypt, dated to 5 BC (Year 25 of Caesar). It honors Apollonius, son of Theon, for his exceptional generosity in rebuilding the association's meeting house and its facilities at his own expense after they were destroyed by a flood. The decree outlines specific honors, such as the right to wear a crown and the placement of a shield-monument, and establishes a heavy fine of 3000 drachmas for anyone attempting to rescind these honors. The text concludes with instructions for the decree to be recorded in the association's books and engraved on a stele.
