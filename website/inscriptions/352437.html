This Hellenistic decree (ca. 230-200 BC) was issued by the city of Colophon to honor ambassadors from Priene. The text outlines the honors bestowed, including a golden crown for their virtue (arete) and goodwill (eunoia). It specifies that the honors should be announced at major festivals: the Dionysia in Colophon, the Great Klaria (at the sanctuary of Apollo at Klaros), and the Antiochia. Crucially, the decree mandates the creation of two stelae: one for the sanctuary of Apollo at Klaros and another for the sanctuary of Athena Polias in Priene, demonstrating the inter-city diplomatic protocols and the importance of public visibility for such honors.
