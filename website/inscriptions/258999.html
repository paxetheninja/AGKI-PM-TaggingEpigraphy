AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Iasos, dating to the 2nd century BC, is a subscription list (epidosis) for the funding of theatrical and musical performances. It follows a strict formula: dating by the stephanephoros (Apollo, for the fourth time), naming the agonothetes (Moschion), and then listing donors who acted as choregoi. These donors funded specific performers, such as the comic actor Athenodoros and the harpist Kleinon, for set durations (one or two days). A notable social detail is the mention of a choregos specifically for the metics (resident aliens). The text records that the revenue (prosodos) from these events was donated back to the city (egeneto dorean), highlighting the liturgical and philanthropic nature of civic life in Hellenistic Iasos.
