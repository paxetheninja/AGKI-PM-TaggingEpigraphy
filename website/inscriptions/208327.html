AI Analysis Summary

This document consists of two related decrees from Kos, dating to the early 2nd century BC. The primary decree is a response to a Milesian embassy requesting that the Didymeia games be recognized as 'stephanitic' and that the sanctuary of Apollo at Didyma be recognized as 'asylos' (inviolable). The text emphasizes the shared religious traditions involving Leto and Zeus and the diplomatic friendship between Kos and Miletus. It concludes with the appointment of ambassadors (Demosthenes and Simos) to carry the resolution to Miletus. The text is fragmentary, with significant lacunae in the second decree, but the administrative and religious intent is fully preserved.
