AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major historical document from the Asklepieion of Pergamon, serving as both an honorific monument and a funerary record for Gaius Julius Quadratus Bassus. Bassus was a high-ranking Roman senator of Pergamene origin who served as a general under Trajan during the Dacian Wars. The text meticulously lists his career milestones, including his consulship (105 AD) and his numerous provincial governorships. Crucially, it records his death while on campaign in Dacia and the specific imperial instructions from Hadrian to have his body escorted back to Pergamon with full military honors, funded by the imperial fiscus. The text is fragmentary, as indicated by the numerous lacunae and reconstructions in the provided transcription.
