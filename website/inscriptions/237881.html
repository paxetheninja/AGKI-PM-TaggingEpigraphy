This inscription (IG IIÂ² 360) records a series of Athenian decrees from 330-324 BC honoring Herakleides of Salamis (Cyprus). Herakleides was a merchant who provided critical assistance to Athens during a period of severe grain shortage (the 'spanositia'). He sold grain at a subsidized price (5 drachmas per medimnos) and made significant financial contributions to the grain fund (sitonia). The decrees grant him the status of proxenos and euergetes, the right to own property (enktesis), and the right to serve in the Athenian military. One decree also addresses a diplomatic incident where his grain was seized by the city of Herakleia Pontica, leading Athens to send an envoy (Theobagenes) to demand restitution. The text is a prime example of the 'euergetism' system in Hellenistic-era Athens.
