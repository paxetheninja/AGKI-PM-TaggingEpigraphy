AI Analysis Summary

This is a well-preserved Athenian decree from 332/1 BC, found at the Amphiareion in Oropos. It follows the standard Athenian formulaic structure for decrees, including an invocation, dating by archon and prytany, identification of the secretary and proposer, and the formal resolution. The decree honors the local hero-god Amphiaraus for his benevolence toward the Athenians and visitors to his sanctuary, ordering a golden crown and the erection of a stone stele to commemorate the honor. It provides valuable insight into Athenian administration of the sanctuary during a period of Athenian control over Oropos.
