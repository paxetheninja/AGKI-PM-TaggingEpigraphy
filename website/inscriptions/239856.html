AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Amphictyonic decree from Delphi dating to the late 3rd century BC (c. 217-212 BC). It honors Polyarchides, son of Hermarchos, a citizen of Chios who served as a hieromnamon (sacred delegate). The text details his exemplary performance in religious duties (sacrifices, care of altars) and administrative/legal matters (resolving disputes). In recognition, the Council grants him a golden crown, bronze statues to be erected in Delphi and Chios, and prestigious rights including asylia (inviolability) and ateleia (tax exemption). The decree was to be set up on two steles, one in the sanctuary at Delphi and one in Chios.
