This inscription is a well-known public register of land sales from the island of Chios, dating to the 4th century BC. It provides detailed information on the sale of various plots of land and houses, including the names of the buyers, the prices in staters, and the topographical boundaries marked by 'horoi'. The text also outlines the legal procedures involving the city council (boule), heralds (kerykes), and boundary guards (orophylakes), as well as penalties for removing boundary stones. The mention of the 'basileus' refers to a specific magistrate in Chios.
