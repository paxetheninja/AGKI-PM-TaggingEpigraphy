This inscription is a classic Athenian ephebic decree from 94/3 BCE (archonship of Kallias). It provides a comprehensive record of the ephebes' year of training, detailing their religious participation (sacrifices to Artemis Agrotera, the Eleusinian Mysteries, and the Mother of the Gods), military drills (hoplomachia, akontismos), and intellectual pursuits (attending lectures in the Ptolemaion library). The decree serves as a public commendation of the youths' transition into full citizenship, rewarding them with golden crowns for their 'eutaxia' (good order) and 'eusebeia' (piety).
