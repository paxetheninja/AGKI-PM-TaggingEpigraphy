AI Analysis Summary

This Hellenistic decree from Samos honors Aristolaos, son of Amyntas, a Macedonian general (strategos) stationed in Caria. The text acknowledges his services to the city and his alignment with the king's policies. As a reward, the Samian people grant him citizenship, the title of proxenos, the right of proedria (front-row seating at festivals), and priority access to the council and assembly. The decree was to be inscribed on a stone stele and erected in the Heraion, the sanctuary of Hera on Samos. The list of names at the end likely refers to the witnesses or the committee responsible for the decree's implementation.
