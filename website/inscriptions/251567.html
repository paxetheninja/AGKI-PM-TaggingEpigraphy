This inscription (IEry 114) is a Hellenistic honorific decree from the city of Erythrai in Ionia. It honors Kallikrates, son of Leagoros, for his diligent and just service as a judge (dikastes) appointed by the people. The decree outlines the administrative process for its publication, including the role of the prytaneis, the secretary of the council, and the appointment of overseers (epistatai) to ensure the decree and a crown are engraved on a stone stele. The stele was to be placed in the Agora near an existing monument of Agathokles. The text also mentions financial arrangements involving the hieropoios Chrysogonos.
