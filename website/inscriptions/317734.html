AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Mieza (Leukadia) in Macedonia dates to the late 3rd century BC and contains a series of land sale contracts. The primary buyer in these transactions is Zopyros, son of Gorgias. The documents are highly structured, following a legal protocol that includes the eponymous priest of Asklepios (Nikanor), the tagoi (Epolemos), and the nomarch (Pistotos). Each entry details the seller, the location and size of the land (measured in plethra), the price in drachmae, the legal guarantor (bebaiotes), and a list of witnesses (martyres). The presence of individuals from Skydra and Neapolis highlights the regional administrative and economic integration of the Bottiaia district under the Macedonian monarchy.
