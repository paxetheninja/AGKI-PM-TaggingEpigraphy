AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a posthumous honorific decree from the city of Olbia for Neikeratos, son of Pappios, dating to the early 1st century BC. The text details his extensive services to the city, including his role in resolving internal conflicts, his military leadership during continuous wars, and his eventual death in a night ambush by enemies. In recognition of his 'virtue and benevolence,' the city granted him a public funeral, a golden crown, and an equestrian statue. Furthermore, it established an annual proclamation of his honors during the games of Achilles. The decree was to be inscribed on a white stone stele and placed in a prominent location chosen by his relatives.
