This inscription contains a series of sacral manumission records from Naupaktos, dating to the mid-2nd century BC. The documents record the fictitious sale of slaves to the god Dionysos, a legal mechanism to grant them freedom (en eleutheriai). The text specifies the eponymous official (the secretary of the thearos), the names and origins of the slaves (including Galatian, Thracian, and Arabian individuals), the purchase price in silver drachmas, and the names of guarantors (proapodotai) and witnesses. Some sections include paramone clauses, requiring the manumitted person to remain with their former owner for a specified duration or until death.
