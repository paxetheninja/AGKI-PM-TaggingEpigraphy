This inscription is a fragmentary copy of a Ptolemaic priestly decree, likely the Decree of Canopus (238 BC) or a similar synodal decree from the reign of Ptolemy III Euergetes. The text mentions the 'Benefactor Gods' (Ptolemy III and Berenice II), the 'Brother-and-Sister Gods' (Ptolemy II and Arsinoe II), and Alexander the Great. It records the decision of the high priests and prophets to establish annual festivals and honors for the royal family. The dating formula includes the Egyptian month Phaophi and mentions the Canephore of Arsinoe Philadelphus, a standard feature of Ptolemaic official dating. The provenance from Elephantine suggests this was a local copy of a national decree intended for the temple of Khnum.
