This inscription belongs to the series of 'Poletai' records from the Athenian Agora, dating to the mid-4th century BC (specifically between 350 and 342 BC based on the mentioned archons Pythodemos and Aristodemos). It documents the leasing of silver mines in the Laurion district. The text follows a strict administrative formula: it identifies the mine by name (e.g., Dionysiakon, Artemisiakon), its classification (old, newly opened, or workable), its precise location using topographical markers (boundaries, workshops, or neighboring properties), and the name and demotic of the individual who leased it (the 'epigraphamenos'). These documents are crucial for understanding the economic administration of the Athenian state and the legal framework of its mining industry.
