This inscription is part of the extensive series of financial accounts from Tauromenion (Taormina), dating to the Hellenistic period (likely 2nd century BC). It records the monthly financial activities of the city's magistrates, specifically the hieromnamones (sacred treasurers), tamiai (general treasurers), and officials overseeing the grain supply (sitophylakes and sitonai). The text is organized by the months of the local calendar (Pellaios, Karneios, Apollonios, Lanitropos) and lists transactions in talents and litrai, as well as grain quantities in medimnoi. The presence of specific names like Soteles and Phronios allows for prosopographical links to other known Tauromenian inscriptions.
