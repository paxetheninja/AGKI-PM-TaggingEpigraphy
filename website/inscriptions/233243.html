This inscription (IG IIÂ² 937) is a fragmentary Athenian decree dating to approximately 135 BC. It honors Zenodoros, son of Eumenes of the deme Trinemeia, for his diplomatic services and goodwill towards the Athenian people. Zenodoros served as an ambassador to the Seleucid King Antiochos VII Sidetes and provided assistance to Athenian citizens visiting Antioch. In recognition of his 'arete' (virtue) and 'eunoia' (goodwill), the city awarded him a gold crown, a bronze statue to be erected in the Agora near the statue of the king, and the privilege of 'sitesis' (dining in the Prytaneion). The decree was to be inscribed on a stone stele and set up in the Agora.
