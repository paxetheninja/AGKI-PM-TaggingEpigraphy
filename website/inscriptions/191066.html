This inscription, dated to 43 BC from Lindos, Rhodes, is a formal administrative and religious record. It lists various boards of officials who served the sanctuary and the city, specifically the Hierothytai (responsible for sacrifices), Archierothytai, and the Mastroi (a Rhodian board of magistrates). It also includes those who served as priests of Dionysus. The document concludes with a formulaic honorific statement common in Rhodian decrees, emphasizing the 'eusebeia' (piety) towards the gods and 'arete' (virtue) and 'eunoia' (goodwill) towards the 'plethos' (the body of citizens) of the Lindians. The top of the stone is missing, but the functional parts of the list and the concluding decree are well-preserved.
