This Athenian decree, dated to the archonship of Symmachos (188/7 BC), honors a citizen of Eretria who was a friend of King Ptolemy V. The text follows the standard Hellenistic Athenian format for citizenship grants: it includes a dating formula by archon and prytany, the name of the secretary, the location of the assembly (the Theater), and the proposer's name. The decree grants the honorand Athenian citizenship (politeia), contingent on a judicial review (dokimasia), and allows him to choose his tribe, deme, and phratry. It concludes with instructions for the publication of the decree on a stone stele to be placed on the Akropolis, with costs covered by the military treasurer.
