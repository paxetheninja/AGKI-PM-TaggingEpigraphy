This inscription, known as the 'Law of Ilion against Tyranny and Oligarchy' (IK Ilion 25), dates to approximately 281 BCE. It is a crucial document for understanding Hellenistic political ideology and the defense of democratic institutions. The law incentivizes the removal of anti-democratic leaders by offering a talent of silver, a bronze statue, and maintenance in the prytaneion to the tyrannicide. It also addresses the status of foreigners and slaves who participate in restoring democracy, granting them citizenship or freedom. Furthermore, it mandates a form of damnatio memoriae, requiring the removal of the names of tyrants from all public records and the destruction of their statues or dedications.
