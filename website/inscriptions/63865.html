This inscription is a fragment of a Delian inventory, likely dating to the period of the Second Athenian Domination (post-167 BC). It records a variety of sacred objects (phialai, crowns, tripods, and statues) held in the temple treasuries. The text is significant for its mention of several Hellenistic monarchs, including the Seleucids (Demetrius, Seleucus), the Antigonids (Antigonus), and Prusias of Bithynia, whose offerings remained part of the temple's wealth for generations. It also reflects the increasing Roman presence on the island through the mention of individuals like Quintus. The inventory meticulously notes the weight of each item in drachmae and occasionally describes their physical condition (e.g., 'tethrausmena' or broken).
