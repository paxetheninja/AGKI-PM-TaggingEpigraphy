The text follows the standard diplomatic formula for such agreements, including clauses for mutual defense, the prohibition of aiding enemies (non-assistance), and the requirement to publish the treaty on bronze tablets in the Capitol at Rome and in local sanctuaries (Athena and Asclepius) in Astypalaea.
