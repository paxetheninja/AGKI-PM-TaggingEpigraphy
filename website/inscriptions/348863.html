AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a civic decree from Maionia in Lydia, dated to 17/16 BC during the early Roman Principate. It is dated by the high priesthood of the Goddess Roma and Augustus, as well as the local stephanephoros. The decree honors Kleandros, son of Mogetos, for his exceptional character and public service, emphasizing his 'arete' (virtue), 'dikaiosyne' (justice), and 'philanthropia' (benevolence). The text highlights that his personal efforts for the fatherland exceed even the high standards set by his ancestors. The document was formally proposed by the board of strategoi and the secretary of the people, Thrasymachos.
