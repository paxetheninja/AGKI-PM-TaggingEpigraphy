This inscription (I.Stratonikeia 1101) is a significant civic decree from Stratonikeia in Caria, dating to the Roman Imperial period. It concerns the organization of a choir of thirty boys who were to sing daily hymns to the city's main deities, Zeus Panamaros and Hekate, for the preservation of the city and the Roman Empire. The text provides detailed administrative instructions regarding the selection of the boys by the paidonomos and paidophylakes, their attire (white clothing and wreaths), and the musical accompaniment. It also specifies the locations for the public display of the decree: the Bouleuterion and the Serapeion, highlighting the integration of civic and religious life.
