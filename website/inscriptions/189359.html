This inscription is a significant example of a circus defixio from Roman Carthage, dating to the 2nd or 3rd century AD. It targets the Blue faction (Veneti), specifically naming charioteers like Victorikos and Secundinus, and their horses. The text is rich in 'voces magicae' (magical names) and syncretic religious elements, invoking the God of the Jews (Iao, Sabaoth, Adonai) and referencing the Cherubim. The curse seeks to physically bind the limbs and hearts of the competitors to prevent them from winning in the circus. The transcription shows several lacunae and reconstructed letters, indicating a fragmentary state typical of lead tablets recovered from archaeological contexts like the Carthage circus.
