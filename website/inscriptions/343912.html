This inscription is a Samian decree from the late 4th century BC, found in the Asklepieion of Kos. It honors Koan judges and proxenoi who had traveled to Samos to arbitrate legal disputes. The decree grants them gold crowns, public announcements of their honors at the Dionysia, asylia (inviolability), proedria (priority seating), and Samian citizenship (politeia). The text specifies that two copies of the decree be inscribed on stone stelae: one for the Heraion in Samos and one for Kos. The mention of Queen Phila (likely Phila I, wife of Demetrius Poliorcetes) provides a secure Hellenistic date.
