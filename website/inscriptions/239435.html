This inscription is the 'Second Delphic Hymn' (CID 3.2), composed by Limenios, son of Thoinos, an Athenian, in 128 BCE. It was inscribed on the southern external wall of the Athenian Treasury at Delphi. The text is a paean and prosodion accompanied by musical notation (not fully represented in this transcription). It recounts the birth of Apollo on Delos, his journey to Attica and Delphi, and his victory over the Python. The text is fragmentary with significant lacunae restored by epigraphists.
