🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dating to the mid-3rd century AD (reign of Philip the Arab), represents a significant administrative document from the Temple of Hibis in the El Kharga Oasis. It provides an exhaustive list of 'hydreumata' (man-made water sources or wells) and 'pegai' (natural springs), which were the lifeblood of the oasis economy. The text mentions high-ranking Roman officials like the 'katholikos' (rationalis) Claudius Marcellus and the 'epitropos' (procurator) Marcus Salutarius, indicating central imperial oversight of water rights and land distribution ('koitastheisa diathesis'). The topographical descriptions use cardinal directions (north, south, east/apeliotes, west/lips) and mention 'peristasis' (the city's surrounding area) to define the boundaries of each water source.
