AI Analysis Summary

This extensive inscription from the mid-3rd century AD (reign of Philip the Arab) serves as a detailed administrative register of water sources, known as 'hydreumata', in the Kharga Oasis (Hibis). The text is structured as a catalogue, listing each well or cistern alongside its neighbors (geitones) and cardinal orientations (north, south, east, west), often referencing the 'perimeter of the city' (peristasis tes poleos). The involvement of the 'katholikos' (the Roman rationalis of Egypt) Claudius Marcellus and the 'epitropos' (procurator) Marcus Salutarius underscores the vital importance of water management and taxation in the desert regions of Roman Egypt. The document provides invaluable data for the historical geography and onomastics of the Great Oasis.
