AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a collection of manumission acts from the sanctuary of Asclepius at Pythoion in Perrhaibia. The records follow a strict formula: dating by the federal strategos of the Thessalian League and the local priest of Asclepius, followed by the name of the manumittor and the slaves being freed 'according to the law'. The presence of 'xenodokoi' (guarantors) is a characteristic legal feature of Thessalian manumissions during the 2nd century BC. The strategoi Kleon of Larissa and Pausanias of Pherai allow for a precise dating to the late 2nd century BC.
