AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, found at Aphrodisias and dating to the reign of Commodus (180-192 AD), is an official document regulating the local festival calendar. It synchronizes various contests (agones) funded by private bequests, such as those of Claudius Adrastus and Kallikrates, with the broader regional circuit (including Herakleia). The text specifies the financial management of the endowments (in denarii), the roles of officials like the priest of Aphrodite and the first archon, and lists specific competitive categories for tragic actors (tragoedoi).
