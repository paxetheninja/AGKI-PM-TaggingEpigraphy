AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Athenian prytany decree from the late 2nd century BC (131/0 BC). It records the honors bestowed upon the prytaneis of the tribe Attalis for their successful administration and religious observances. The text follows a highly standardized formula: dating by archon and secretary, recording the assembly's location (the Theater), the proposer's name, and the list of deities to whom sacrifices were made (Apollo, Artemis, Phosphoros). It concludes with instructions for the publication of the decree on a stone stele and the allocation of funds for its creation by the military treasurer.
