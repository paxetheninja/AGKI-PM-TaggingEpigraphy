This inscription is a royal letter from a Spartocid king (likely Paerisades II of the Bosporan Kingdom) to the city of Kos, dated to 242 BC. It is part of a large dossier of 'Asylieurkunden' (asylia documents) found at the Asklepieion of Kos. The king responds to an embassy from Kos, recognizing the sanctuary's right of asylia (inviolability) and the panhellenic status of the festival. The text emphasizes the traditional kinship (syngeneia) and friendship (philia) between the Bosporan dynasty and the Koans, referencing the king's father and brother. The letter follows standard Hellenistic diplomatic protocols, including the mention of theoria (sacred embassies) and the closing valediction 'errosthe'.
