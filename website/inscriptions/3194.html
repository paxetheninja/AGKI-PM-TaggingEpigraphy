AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian honorary decree dating to the archonship of Herakleitos (137/6 BCE). It honors Nikokrates of Phlya for his service as the priest of Asclepius and Hygieia. The decree details his religious activities, including the performance of sacrifices at the Asclepieia, Epidauria, and Heroia festivals, as well as his daughter's participation as an arrephoros. It also mentions his care for the temple (Asklepieion) and the organization of all-night vigils (pannychides). The text follows the standard formulaic structure of Hellenistic Athenian decrees, including the dating by archon and prytany, the identification of the secretary and proposer, and the location of the assembly in Piraeus.
