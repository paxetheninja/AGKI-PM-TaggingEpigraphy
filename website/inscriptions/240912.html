AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic manumission through fictive sale to Apollo Pythios, dated to the early 1st century AD (Archonship of Nikanor). It documents the release of two women, Sotericha and Epktesis, by their owners Straton and Orthagora. Sotericha is freed immediately and unconditionally, while Epktesis is bound by a 'paramone' clause to serve Stratonike for the remainder of her life. The text specifies the price of 3 silver minae per person and names several guarantors (bebaioteres) and witnesses from Delphi and surrounding regions like Amphissa and Aetolia, illustrating the legal and social networks of the period.
