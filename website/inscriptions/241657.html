AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Delphic manumission record dating to the late 2nd century BC. It documents the manumission of a female slave named Zopyra through the legal fiction of a sale to the god Apollo Pythios. The sellers are Aristion and Polykrateia, and the act is guaranteed by Aristoboulos. The text specifies that Zopyra is to be free (eleuthera) and protected from any future claims of ownership (anephaptos). The document is dated by the archonship of Kallikrates at Delphi and Kleon at Erineos, and it includes a list of witnesses and officials, which is typical for the administrative and legal records found on the Polygonal Wall at Delphi.
