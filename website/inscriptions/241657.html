AI Analysis Summary

This is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated by the archonship of Callicrates in Delphi and Cleon in Erineos. It follows the standard 'sale to the god' formula where Ariston and Polykrateia sell a home-born slave named Zopyris to Pythian Apollo for four silver minae. The text specifies that Zopyris is to be free (eleuthera) and protected from any attempt at re-enslavement (anephaptos). It includes a guarantor (bebaioter) and a list of witnesses including priests and other officials. The presence of square brackets indicates some textual loss and restoration.
