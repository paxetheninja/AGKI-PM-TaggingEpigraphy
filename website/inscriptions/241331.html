AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Delphic manumission record dating to the mid-2nd century BC. It documents the fictitious sale of two slaves, Komikos and Onas, by their owner Amaraos to the god Apollo. The price is set at four silver minae. The manumission includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring the former slaves to remain in service to Amaraos until his death. The text is authenticated by the Delphic archon (Emmenidas), the priests of Apollo (Mantas and Tarantinos), and a guarantor (Aristion). The presence of numerous lacunae and missing vowels in the provided transcription indicates a fragmentary state.
