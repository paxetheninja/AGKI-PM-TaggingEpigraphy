AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree of the Athenian residents (cleruchs) on Delos, dating to the archonship of Epikrates (146/5 BC). It honors Gorgias, son of Asklepiades of the deme Oion, for his significant contributions to the community, including the performance of sacrifices, the provision of prizes for contests, and the expenditure of personal funds for public benefit. The decree follows standard Athenian diplomatic and administrative formulas, including the appointment of ambassadors to Athens to confirm the honors. It concludes with instructions to erect a stone stele in the gymnasium, a central institution for the Athenian community on the island.
