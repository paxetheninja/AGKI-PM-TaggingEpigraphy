AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian honorary decree dating to approximately 330 BC, during the Lycurgan period. It was passed by the Council (Boule) and the People (Demos) to honor several religious officials: the priest of Dionysus (Meixigenes), the priest of Poseidon Pelagios, the priest of Zeus Soter, and the priest of Ammon, as well as a board of ten hieropoioi. They are commended for their piety and the successful performance of sacrifices for the welfare of the Athenian state, its women, and children. The decree awards each official a golden crown and mandates that the text be inscribed on a stone stele and erected in the Theater of Dionysus. The document concludes with a list of the honored hieropoioi identified by their names and demes.
