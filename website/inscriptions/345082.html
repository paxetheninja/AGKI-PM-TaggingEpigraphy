This 2nd-century BC inscription is a decree of the city of Smyrna honoring the city of Kaunos and a panel of foreign judges (Antigenes, Aineas, and Timouchos) sent to Smyrna to adjudicate legal disputes. The use of foreign judges (xenodikai) was a common Hellenistic diplomatic and legal practice to ensure impartiality in local courts. The decree grants the judges gold crowns, public announcements of their virtue at the Dionysia festival, and various privileges (proedria, ephodos). The text was to be inscribed on a white marble stele and set up in a prominent location. The presence of brackets in the transcription indicates that the stone is partially damaged, though the majority of the text is reconstructible.
