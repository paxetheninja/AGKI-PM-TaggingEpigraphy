This inscription, dated to 135 BC, is a composite document from Ombos (Kom Ombo) in Upper Egypt. It records a decree passed by the neaniskoi (young men) associated with the local gymnasium. The decree mandates the engraving of royal letters received from the joint sovereigns Ptolemy VIII Euergetes II, his sister Cleopatra II, and his wife Cleopatra III. These letters were addressed to Boethos, a high-ranking official (strategos and kinsman of the king), and to the members of the gymnasium, in response to a petition (enteuxis) submitted by Alkimachos and Themistokles. The inscription illustrates the administrative procedures of the Ptolemaic court and the significant social status of the gymnasium as a civic institution capable of direct interaction with the monarchy.
