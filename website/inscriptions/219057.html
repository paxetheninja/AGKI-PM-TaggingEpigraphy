This inscription (SEG 26.1813) is a primary historical source for the Aksumite Empire under King Ezana in the 4th century AD. It marks a pivotal moment in East African history: the official adoption of Christianity by the monarchy, evidenced by the Trinitarian formula at the start. The narrative describes a defensive military expedition against the Noba (Nubians) who had attacked Aksumite allies. The use of Greek in this context demonstrates Aksum's integration into the late antique Mediterranean cultural and diplomatic sphere.
