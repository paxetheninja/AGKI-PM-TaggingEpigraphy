AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Azoros in Perrhaibia dates to the 13th and 14th years of Augustus (15-13 BC). It records a series of manumissions where slaves were granted freedom and subsequently paid a fee (the 'geinomena denaria') to the city treasury. The records follow a strict administrative formula: dating by the year of Augustus and the strategos of the Thessalian League, naming the local treasurer (tamias), identifying the freed person and their former owner, and confirming the financial transaction. The mention of individuals from Larissa and Doliche indicates regional mobility and the legal interconnectedness of the Perrhaibian and Thessalian cities during the early Principate.
