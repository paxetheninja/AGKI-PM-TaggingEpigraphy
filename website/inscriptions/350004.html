This inscription from the Hellenistic period (ca. 200-180 BC) records a public subscription (epidosis) for the construction or renovation of the Aphrodision (Temple of Aphrodite) in the deme of Antimachia on Kos. The text begins with a reference to a decree proposed by Damokrates, son of Damokles, which authorized the collection of funds. The list is notable for including family units, where male citizens contribute not only for themselves but also on behalf of their wives and children (e.g., Aristion contributing for his wife Platthis, son Laistratos, and daughter Kleito).
