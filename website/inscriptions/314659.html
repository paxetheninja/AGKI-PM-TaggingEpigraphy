This inscription, dated to 21 AD, is a royal letter from the Parthian King Artabanus II (referred to by the dynastic name Arsakes) to the Greek city of Susa (Seleucia on the Eulaeus). The king intervenes in a local administrative dispute concerning Hestiaios, son of Asios, who had been elected treasurer for a second time. A local law required a three-year gap between terms, but the king confirms the election as valid ('kyrian'), citing Hestiaios's exceptional service, his refusal to take expenses from the city, and his successful embassies. The text is a crucial witness to the survival of Greek civic institutions and legal frameworks within the Parthian Empire and the direct involvement of the Arsacid crown in the internal affairs of its Greek poleis.
