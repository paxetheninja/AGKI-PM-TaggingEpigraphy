AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IMyl 634) is a Hellenistic honorific decree concerning a foreign judge. The city of Mylasa sent a judge named Theodoros (son of Theodoros, adopted by Isidoros) and a secretary named Menekrates to another city (the issuer of the decree) to settle legal disputes. The decree praises their conduct during their stay (epidemia) and awards Theodoros a gold crown and Menekrates an olive crown. It also grants Theodoros the right of proedria (front-row seating at festivals) and an invitation to the prytaneion. The honors were to be announced during the Dionysia festival. The decree was to be inscribed on a stone stele and erected in a conspicuous location to demonstrate the gratitude of the issuing city towards the people of Mylasa, whom they describe as kinsmen and friends.
