This inscription, identified as CID 2.75, dates to the autumn of 336 BC during the archonship of Peithon at Delphi. It records the financial accounts of the Delphic Amphictyony, specifically the 'logos' of the treasurers (tamiai) for the Pylaia meeting. The text details the receipt and distribution of funds for the reconstruction of the Temple of Apollo, involving the naopoioi (temple builders). It is historically significant for its list of Hieromnemones, which reflects the political composition of the Amphictyonic League shortly after the Battle of Chaeronea and the rise of Macedonian influence, as evidenced by the mention of a Macedonian delegate.
