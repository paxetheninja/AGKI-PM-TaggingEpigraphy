AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree of the Delphic Amphictyony dating to 193 BC, during the archonship of Kephalos. It honors Pixodaros, son of Kratias, from the Carian city of Mylasa. The decree is particularly significant because it references Pixodaros' illustrious ancestors, the Hekatomnid dynasts Mausolos and Hekatomnos, emphasizing their historical benefactions to the sanctuary. The Amphictyons grant Pixodaros and his descendants several high-status privileges: asylia (personal and property inviolability), ateleia (exemption from taxes), and prodikia (the right to have legal cases heard first). The text was to be inscribed and displayed in the sanctuary of Apollo at Delphi, serving as a permanent record of the diplomatic ties between the Delphic league and the Carian elite.
