AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a typical Athenian prytany decree dating to approximately 170 BC. It honors the prytaneis of the tribe Kekropis for their successful administration and religious duties (sacrifices) during their term. The text includes the standard publication clause, directing the secretary to set up a stone stele in the Prytanikon. It names several Athenian citizens, including the secretary Asklepiades of Sypalettos, providing valuable prosopographical data for the mid-2nd century BC.
