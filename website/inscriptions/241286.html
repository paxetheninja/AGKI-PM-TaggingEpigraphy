AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a manumission record from Delphi, dated to the mid-2nd century BC. It follows the standard Delphic formula of a 'sale to the god' (Apollo Pythios). Kleunika, daughter of Archelous, sells her slave Praxinika to the god for three silver minae. The manumission is conditional: Praxinika must remain (paramone) with Kleunika for the rest of Kleunika's life, serving her blamelessly. Additionally, Praxinika is tasked with the religious duty of crowning Kleunika's tomb annually after her death. The document lists the archons and priests of Apollo as witnesses and guarantors of the freedom granted.
