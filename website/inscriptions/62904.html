AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of a Delian temple inventory, likely from the period of Delian independence. It records the annual handover of sacred treasures between the outgoing and incoming boards of Hieropoioi and Tamiai. The text mentions specific types of vessels (phialai, skaphia), jewelry (rings, bracelets), and a gold crown dedicated by King Attalos I of Pergamon. The mention of the 'Eutycheon Philadelpheon' likely refers to a specific group of phialai associated with a fund or a specific donor group. The weights are recorded in drachmae and minae, and the text is dated by the eponymous archons of Delos.
