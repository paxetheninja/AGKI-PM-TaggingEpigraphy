🤖 AI Analysis Summary

IG II² 1666 is a major 4th-century BC inscription from Eleusis containing the building specifications (syngraphai) for the Philonian Portico of the Telesterion. The text provides an exhaustive list of architectural members—including triglyphs (τριγλύφους), metopes (μετώπια), cornices (γεῖσα), and stylobates (στυλοβάτας)—along with their precise dimensions in feet and palms. It specifies the use of Pentelic marble, Aeginetan stone, and local Eleusinian stone. The mention of the architect Philargos and the detailed instructions for dressing (ἐκπελεκῆσαι), dowelling (γομφῶσαι), and leading (μόλυβδον περιχέαι) the stones make it a primary source for understanding Greek monumental architecture and public procurement.
