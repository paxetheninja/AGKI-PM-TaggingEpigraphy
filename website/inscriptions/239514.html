This inscription is a manumission record from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Kallidemos (ca. 124 BC). It documents the fictitious sale of a female slave named Pistis to the god Apollo Pythios by Kleunika and her son Kleunikos for four silver minae. The document guarantees Pistis's freedom (eleutheria) and status as untouchable (anaphaptos) for the rest of her life. Sosixenos son of Kephalos serves as the legal guarantor (bebaioter). The text is characteristic of the thousands of manumissions found at Delphi, providing a rich list of contemporary officials and witnesses, including the priests of Apollo and the city archons.
