AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dating to the joint reign of Ptolemy VI Philometor and Cleopatra II (c. 152-145 BC), records a dedication by a priestly association (synodos) of the five tribes from the First Cataract region. It honors the royal family and Boethos, son of Nikostratos, a prominent official who served as the strategos of the Triakontaschoinos and founder of cities in that region. The text syncretizes Greek and Egyptian religious elements, identifying local deities like Chnoubis (Khnum), Hera (Satis), and Hestia (Anukis) alongside Hellenistic gods. It establishes annual festivals and honors for Boethos, reflecting the complex socio-political and religious landscape of the southern Egyptian frontier during the Ptolemaic period.
