AI Analysis Summary

This mid-3rd century BC inscription from Crete represents a diplomatic and legal treaty between the cities of Lyttos and Malla. It outlines regulations for military cooperation, property rights, and the prevention of illegal seizures (syle). The text mandates that the chief magistrates (kosmoi) of both cities enforce fines in staters for violations and ensures the treaty's longevity by requiring an annual public reading of the stele in both jurisdictions. The mention of 'the gods' (restored) follows standard formulaic language for such agreements.
