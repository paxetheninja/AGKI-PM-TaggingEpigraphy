AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to the archonship of Babylos (147/6 BC), documents a complex interstate arbitration involving the Amphictyonic Council. The dispute concerns the Euboean cities of Eretria, Karystos, and Chalcis. The Amphictyons delegated the judicial decision to the city of Hypata, which was tasked with forming a jury of thirty men to resolve the conflict. The text preserves parts of the official correspondence (epistolai) and references the council's records (hypomnematismoi), providing significant insight into the diplomatic and legal mechanisms of the mid-2nd century BC in Central Greece.
