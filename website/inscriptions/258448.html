AI Analysis Summary

This series of inscriptions (SEG 40.962-966) honors the family of Gaius Julius Theupompus of Knidos, a prominent figure and friend of Julius Caesar. Theupompus is credited with securing the freedom of Knidos from Caesar after the Battle of Pharsalus. The texts celebrate the family's piety (eusebeia), virtue (arete), and their role in restoring the city's 'ancestral freedom' and 'democracy'. The inscriptions likely served as bases for statues of the family members, erected by the Demos of Knidos.
