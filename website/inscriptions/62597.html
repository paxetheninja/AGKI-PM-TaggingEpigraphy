AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a typical financial account of the Delian hieropoioi from the period of Delian independence. It details the revenues of the Sanctuary of Apollo, specifically the rents from sacred estates (temene) and the interest on loans. The text also serves as an inventory handover, recording precious metal objects dedicated by prominent figures, including a Macedonian named Myntas and an unnamed King (likely one of the Diadochi, such as Antigonus I or Demetrius Poliorcetes). The use of both Attic and Alexandrian coinage reflects the economic transition of the early Hellenistic period.
