This inscription (Milet I 3, 149) is a comprehensive treaty of sympoliteia between Miletus and Pidasa, likely dating to c. 188/7 BC. It details the absorption of the Pidasian community into the Milesian state. The text covers citizenship rights, the transfer of the Pidasian archives to the Milesian bouleuterion, military arrangements (including a Milesian phrourarchos in Pidasa), and fiscal regulations such as tax exemptions for a five-year transitional period. It also addresses land ownership in the territory of Euromos and concludes with the sacred oath to be sworn by the magistrates and citizens of both cities to ensure the permanence of the union.
