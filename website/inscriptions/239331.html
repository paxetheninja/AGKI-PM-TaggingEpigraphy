AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant Delphic decree dating to 138 BC (SIG 698A). It honors the Athenian branch of the 'Artists of Dionysus' (technitai) for their participation in the Pythais, a sacred procession from Athens to Delphi. The text provides a comprehensive list of the participants, categorized by their roles: religious ambassadors (theoroi), teachers (didaskaloi), singers of the paean, musicians (flute players, kitharists), and actors (comic and tragic). The decree grants the guild and its members various privileges, including a laurel crown, 'promanteia' (priority in consulting the oracle), and 'asylia' (inviolability). Historically, it is a key document for understanding the organization of professional performers' guilds and the religious diplomacy between Athens and Delphi in the 2nd century BC. The decree was specifically ordered to be inscribed on the walls of the Athenian Treasury at Delphi.
