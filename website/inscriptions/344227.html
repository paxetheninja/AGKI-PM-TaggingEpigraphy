This inscription, found at the Heraion of Samos and dating to approximately 250 BC, records a decree and a list of personnel. It honors the trierarch Dionysios, son of Metrodoros, who led a contingent on a 'kataphraktos' (decked warship) sent by the Samian people to Iasos to fulfill a military alliance (symmachia). Dionysios is praised for his 'arete' (virtue) and 'dikaiosyne' (justice) and was awarded golden crowns and statues. The text serves as both an honorific monument and a public record of the citizens who participated in the naval expedition.
