AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from 170 AD is a monumental dedication by the city to Emperor Marcus Aurelius. It is remarkable for its prosopographical detail, listing a large network of elite individuals who held multiple civic and religious offices (gymnasiarchy, agoranomia, strategy, and high priesthoods of the imperial cult) across various Egyptian nomes. It highlights the interconnectedness of the Romano-Egyptian administration and the prominence of the imperial cult, specifically mentioning the Hadrianeion and the cult of the deified Antoninus Pius.
