AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Kos (ca. 220 BC) is a sacred law (diagraphÄ“) regulating the sale of the priesthood of Dionysos Thyllophoros. It outlines the administrative and financial procedures for the auction, conducted by the poletai, and specifies that the purchase price must be paid in three installments during the months of Karneios, Gerastios, and Alseios. The text provides detailed instructions on the priestess's rights to sacrificial portions (gera), such as legs and skins from sheep, pigs, and oxen. It also includes penal clauses for late payment and mandates the publication of the law on a stone stele in the sanctuary of the Twelve Gods. The document is dated by the monarchos Charmidas and mentions several Koan citizens involved in the drafting and proposal of the regulation.
