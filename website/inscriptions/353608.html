AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, found at Lagina in Caria and dating to the early 2nd century BC, records a decree of the Chrysaorean League (Koinon). It honors Aristonidas, son of Aristeides, a citizen of Stratonikeia. The text details his extensive services during a period of conflict, likely the war between Rhodes and Philip V or the subsequent Roman-Seleucid tensions. Aristonidas is praised for his military participation, his role as an ambassador (presbeutes), and his significant financial contribution of 3,000 drachmas to the city. As a reward, the League grants him a gold crown, a bronze statue to be erected in the sanctuary of Zeus Chrysaoreos, and the right of proedria (front-row seating) at public games. The mention of Alabanda and Mylasa highlights the regional political network of the Chrysaorean League.
