AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a decree from the Ionian city of Teos, dating to the early Hellenistic period (c. 303 BC), concerning the fortress of Kyrbissos. It provides a detailed set of regulations for the garrisoning of the site, including the selection of a phrourarchos (garrison commander), the recruitment of twenty citizen guards, and their financial compensation in 'Alexander drachmas'. The text is particularly notable for the inclusion of the specific oaths required of the garrison and the inhabitants of Kyrbissos, invoking a pentad of deities (Zeus, Helios, Poseidon, Apollo, and Athena) to ensure loyalty and prevent the betrayal of the fort. The decree also mentions the use of guard dogs and the publication of the text on stone stelae in the agora and the bouleuterion.
