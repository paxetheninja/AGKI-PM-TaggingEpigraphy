AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission document from Delphi, dated to 138 BC (archonship of Timokrates). It records the fictitious sale of a Syrian slave named Parmena to the god Apollo Pythios by her owner Kleodamos, with the consent of his mother. The document specifies the price of three silver minae and guarantees her status as 'eleuthera' (free) and 'anaphaptos' (untouchable/immune from seizure). It includes a comprehensive list of civic and religious officials, including prytaneis, a secretary, a priest, and a neokoros, serving as witnesses and guarantors to ensure the legality of the manumission.
