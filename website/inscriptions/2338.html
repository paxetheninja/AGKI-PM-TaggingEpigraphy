This inscription (IG IIÂ² 120) is a mid-4th century BCE Athenian decree concerning the reorganization and inventorying of the Chalkotheke (Bronze Storehouse) on the Acropolis. The decree specifies the involvement of military officials (strategoi, hipparchoi, taxiarchoi) and the treasurers of the Goddess. It mandates a thorough examination of the bronze items, their counting, and the recording of the results on a stone stele. The preserved text includes a detailed inventory list of bronze items, such as shields (aspides), cauldrons (lebetes), mixing bowls (kraters), and even a bronze horse, many of which are noted as being in poor condition ('ouch hygies').
