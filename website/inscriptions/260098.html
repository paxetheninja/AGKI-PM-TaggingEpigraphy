AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (ILabr 69) is a significant administrative document from the sanctuary of Zeus Labraundos in Caria. It serves as a land register or inventory of sacred estates (hiera ge) dedicated to Zeus, Apollo, and Artemis. The text meticulously records the names of various plots (e.g., Syistan, Kollinirea), their physical characteristics (trees, vines), and their boundaries by identifying neighboring landowners (homoroi) and natural or man-made landmarks like rivers (potamos) and public roads (hodos demosia). The presence of Carian names like Maussollos and the specific epithet of Zeus Labraundos firmly place this in the context of the Hecatomnid or early Hellenistic administration of the sanctuary.
