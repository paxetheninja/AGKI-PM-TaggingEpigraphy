AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is the 'Fortification Decree' of Skotoussa, a highly detailed administrative and legal document from the early 2nd century BC. It regulates the city's defensive perimeter by defining the public land (demosios topos) adjacent to the walls. The text provides a meticulous survey of the fortifications, including towers (pyrgoi), curtain walls (mesapyrgia), and gates (pylai), specifying their dimensions in feet (podes). It is dated by the priest of Asklepios and a board of tagoi (Thessalian magistrates). The document is a primary source for Hellenistic military architecture, urban planning, and the Thessalian dialect.
