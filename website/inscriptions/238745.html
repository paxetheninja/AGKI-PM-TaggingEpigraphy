AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal honorific decree from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Dexandas (c. 150/149 BC). It honors Biayos, son of Aristodemos, a citizen of Naupaktos, for his service as a theorodokos (host of sacred envoys) for the Pythian and Soteria festivals. The decree grants him and his descendants numerous privileges, including proxeny, euergetism, asylia (inviolability), proedria (priority seating), and the right to own land and property in Delphi. The text also lists the members of the council (bouleutai) for the second semester of the year.
