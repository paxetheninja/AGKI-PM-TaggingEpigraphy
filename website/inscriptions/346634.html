This inscription is a set of building specifications (syngraphai) dating to approximately 330 BC, during the Lycurgan period in Athens. It details the technical requirements for the construction and repair of fortifications and religious structures at Eleusis. Key features mentioned include the fabrication of wooden gates from ash wood (melinos), the installation of bronze hinges (strofeis) and bolts (balanoi), and the use of the local dark Eleusinian limestone for a stylobate. The text also references a corner tower near the sea and a propylon, reflecting a significant phase of public works intended to strengthen the sanctuary's defenses and infrastructure.
