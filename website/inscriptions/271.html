AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is identified as List 2 of the Athenian Tribute Lists (ATL), dating to 453/2 BCE. It is part of the first great stele (Lapis Primus) that recorded the quotas (aparchai) of one-sixtieth of the tribute paid by the allies of the Delian League to the treasury of Athena Polias. The text is a classic example of Athenian imperial administration, listing ethnonyms of cities from various districts (Ionian, Hellespontine, Thracian, Carian, and Island) alongside numerical notations of their payments. The mention of the 'secretary to the thirty' refers to the board of auditors (logistai) who oversaw the financial accounts.
