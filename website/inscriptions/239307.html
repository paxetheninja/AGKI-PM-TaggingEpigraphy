This inscription is a catalogue of Athenian ephebes who participated in the Pythais of 106/5 BC. The Pythais was a major religious procession from Athens to the sanctuary of Apollo at Delphi. The text lists the Athenian archon (Agathokles), the kosmetes (supervisor of the ephebes), and the names of the young men (ephebes) organized by their fathers' names. It was inscribed on the walls of the Athenian Treasury at Delphi to commemorate their participation in this sacred embassy. The text is fragmentary in parts, particularly in the lower sections of the name list.
