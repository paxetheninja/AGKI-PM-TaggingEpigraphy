AI Analysis Summary

This 2nd-century BC inscription from Demetrias (Makrinitsa) records a decree by the 'apostoloi' (likely a religious or professional association/guild) honoring Kraton, son of Kraton. Kraton served as the priest of Sarapis and demonstrated significant generosity (philotimia) by donating 1,000 silver drachmas to the association. The decree specifies that the interest from this capital should fund annual honors and sacrifices. In recognition of his piety and virtue, the association awarded him a golden crown and a painted portrait to be displayed in the most prominent part of the Serapeion. The text is fragmentary but largely restorable through formulaic parallels.
