This inscription (IC I viii 13) preserves a treaty (syntheke) between the Cretan cities of Knossos and Hierapytna, dating to the 2nd century BC. The text outlines the reciprocal recognition of religious festivals, the exchange of diplomatic missions (presbeiai), and the publication of the treaty on stone stelae in the Temple of Apollo Delphinios at Knossos and the Dodekatheon at Hierapytna. It also includes penal clauses, such as fines of 100 Attic staters for magistrates (kosmoi) who fail to uphold the public reading of the treaty. The heavy use of brackets indicates significant epigraphic reconstruction.
