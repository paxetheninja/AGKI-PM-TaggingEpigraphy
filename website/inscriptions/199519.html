This inscription is a decree from the Cretan city of Malla, dating to the period after 170 BC. It records the city's response to an embassy from Teos in Ionia. The Teians, represented by Herodotos, Menekles, and Dionysios, requested that Malla recognize Teos and its territory as sacred and inviolable (asylia), citing oracles from Apollo at Delphi and Didyma. Malla grants this request, acknowledging ancestral kinship (syngeneia) and previous ties. Furthermore, the decree grants the Teians isopoliteia (equal citizenship) and ateleia (tax exemption), and promises military assistance if Teian territory is threatened. The document was to be inscribed on a stone stele and dedicated in the sanctuary of Zeus Monnitios.
