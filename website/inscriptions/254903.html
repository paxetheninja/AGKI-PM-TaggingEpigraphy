AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dating to approximately 243 BC, is a pivotal Hellenistic document recording the 'homologia' (agreement) between the city of Smyrna and the military settlers at Magnesia-by-Sipylus. Following Smyrna's steadfast loyalty to Seleucus II during the Third Syrian War, the city incorporated the settlers into its own citizen body. The text outlines the administrative procedures for this sympoliteia, including the registration of new citizens into tribes, the mutual oaths taken by both parties, and the religious sanctions involving a wide array of local and dynastic deities. It also specifies the locations where stelae recording the treaty were to be erected, including major sanctuaries in the region.
