The law mandates that olive growers must sell a fixed portion of their crop (usually one-third, or one-eighth for estates formerly owned by Hipparchos) to the city at a set price to ensure a stable supply for the gymnasium and public use. The text details the administrative process of 'apographe' (declaration), the roles of the 'elaionai' (oil buyers/officials), and the judicial involvement of the Council (Boule), the Assembly (Ecclesia), and the Roman Proconsul in cases of disputes or smuggling.
