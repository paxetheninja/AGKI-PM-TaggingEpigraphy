AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian honorary decree dated to the archonship of Apolexis (46/45 BCE). It records the honors bestowed upon Sokrates, son of Sokrates of the deme Kephisia, who served as the treasurer (tamias) for the prytaneis of the tribe Erechtheis. The decree acknowledges his pious performance of all required sacrifices for the welfare of the Council, the People, and their families, as well as his diligent administration. The Council grants permission for the prytaneis to set up a painted portrait (eikon) of him and crowns him with a foliage crown (thallou stephanos), a traditional honor for virtuous citizens. The text concludes with a partial list of the prytaneis.
