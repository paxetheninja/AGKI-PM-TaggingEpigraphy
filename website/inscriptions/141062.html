This inscription, dated to 174 AD (during the consulship of Gallus and Flaccus Cornelianus), is a dossier containing a letter from the Tyrian merchants residing in Puteoli to their home city of Tyre. They petition for financial relief, explaining that their numbers have decreased and they can no longer afford the rent (misthos) for their commercial/religious station (statio) or the costs of sacrifices to their ancestral gods. They compare their situation to the Tyrian station in Rome, which receives support. The document includes the minutes of the Tyrian council meeting where Philokles son of Diodoros successfully argues in favor of the petition to preserve the city's interests and tradition.
