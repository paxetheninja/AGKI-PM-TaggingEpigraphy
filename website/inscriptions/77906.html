This inscription from Syros, dated to 251 AD, is a significant record of civic euergetism during the joint reign of Trajan Decius and Herennius Etruscus. It documents the generosity of the local official Apollonides, who funded a multi-day festival involving sacrifices and the distribution of food and money. The text is particularly valuable for its social detail, listing specific recipient groups: the gerousia (council of elders), the general citizenry (both resident and visiting), free women, virgins, and even the female servants (therapainides) of the stephanephoroi. The mention of 'nomoi' (likely denarii) alongside specific weights of bread and pork illustrates the logistics of Roman-era public banquets and the maintenance of social hierarchies through targeted benefaction.
