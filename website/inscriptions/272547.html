AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Akmonia, dated to 85 AD during the reign of Domitian, records a civic decree confirming a private foundation established by a citizen named Praxias. The endowment provides for a perpetual distribution (dianome) of money to the city councilors (bouleutai) and freedmen, specifically linked to the maintenance and protection of Praxias' funerary monument. The text includes legal safeguards against the alienation or alteration of the monument and the endowment, invoking the Roman hegemony and several local and panhellenic deities (Zeus Stodmenos, Asclepius, Artemis of Ephesus) as guardians of the decree. It concludes with the names of the officials and the public scribe who recorded the document.
