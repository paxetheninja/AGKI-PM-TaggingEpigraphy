This inscription (SIG 629) is a decree of the Aetolian League dating to 183/2 BC, found at Delphi. It records the formal acceptance by the Aetolians of the Nikephoria festival established by Eumenes II of Pergamon. The decree grants asylia (inviolability) to the sanctuary of Athena Nikephoros and equates the musical contests of the Nikephoria with the Pythian games, and the gymnastic/hippic contests with the Olympic games. It also honors the Attalid royal family (Eumenes, his brothers Attalus, Philetaerus, and Athenaeus, and their mother Apollonis) with golden statues and provides for the appointment of theorodokoi in Aetolian cities to receive the festival's sacred messengers. The text specifies that copies of the decree be set up in the pan-Aetolian sanctuary at Thermon and the pan-Hellenic sanctuary at Delphi.
