This inscription is a decree of the Syrnioi (a community in the Rhodian Peraia) concerning a significant endowment by an individual named Kleusthenes. He donated 500 drachmas and a piece of property (ktema) adjacent to the sanctuary of Asclepius. The decree stipulates that the income from this property and the donated funds are to be used for annual sacrifices to Asclepius and other associated deities. It also mentions the involvement of religious officials, specifically hierothytai and hieromnemones, in the administration of the gift and the performance of the rites. The text is characteristic of Hellenistic civic and religious administration in the Rhodian sphere of influence.
