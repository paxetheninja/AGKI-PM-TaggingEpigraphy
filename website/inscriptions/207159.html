This inscription contains four distinct manumission records from the city of Chyretiai in Perrhaibia, Thessaly. Records A, B, and C date to the late Hellenistic period (ca. 100-90 BC), while record D dates to the Roman Imperial period. Each entry follows a standard legal formula: it identifies the city treasurer (tamias) and the federal strategos of the Thessalian League to establish the date, names the individual being manumitted and their former owner, and confirms the payment of the statutory 15 silver staters to the city treasury. The text is heavily restored in parts but the formulaic nature of Thessalian manumissions allows for high confidence in the interpretation.
