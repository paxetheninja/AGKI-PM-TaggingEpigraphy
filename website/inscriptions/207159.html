AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Chyretiai in Perrhaibia (Thessaly) contains a series of manumission records dating from the late 2nd century BC to the Roman Imperial period. It follows the standard Thessalian formula where manumitted slaves pay a fee of 15 silver staters to the city treasury. The records are dated by the local treasurer (tamias) and the federal strategos of the Thessalian League. The text mentions several individuals, including the freedmen/women (Phane, Sosila, Euphrosyne, Nikephoros) and their former owners, as well as the officials overseeing the process.
