This inscription, found at the Asklepieion of Kos, is a Greek translation of a Roman legal document dating to the triumviral period (c. 39 BC). It names Marcus Antonius (Mark Antony) as 'autokrator' and one of the 'three men for the regulation of public affairs' (triumvir). The text outlines specific legal privileges, including tax exemptions (immunitas) and protection from prosecution, granted to certain individuals and their descendants (ekgonoi). It demonstrates the integration of Roman administrative law into the local epigraphic landscape of the Greek East during the transition from the Republic to the Empire.
