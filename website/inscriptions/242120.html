🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a version of the famous 'Sacred Law' (Nomos) of Antiochus I of Commagene (r. 70–31 BC). It was found at Arsameia on the Nymphaios and details the establishment of the royal cult. The text prescribes the duties of the priests, the organization of monthly festivals on the king's birthday, and the use of sacred lands and revenues to support the sanctuary. It also contains protective clauses and curses against anyone who would violate the sanctity of the 'hierothesion' or the royal decrees. The language reflects the syncretic Persian-Greek religious program of Antiochus I.
