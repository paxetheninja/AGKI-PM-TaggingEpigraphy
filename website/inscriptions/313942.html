AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant example of an astragalomanteion (dice oracle) from Perge, dating to the late 2nd or early 3rd century AD. It provides a series of oracular responses corresponding to the results of throwing five knuckle-bones (astragaloi). Each response is attributed to a specific deity (e.g., Athena, Zeus Soter, Apollo Delphikos) and offers practical advice on matters such as travel, business ventures, health, and legal disputes. The text is highly structured, listing the dice values followed by a poetic interpretation. The presence of Egyptian deities like Isis and Serapis alongside traditional Greek gods reflects the religious syncretism of the Roman Imperial period in Asia Minor.
