AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a lengthy administrative and honorific record of the Xystic Synod (an international athletic guild) based in Rome. It dates to the reign of Constantine the Great and details the financial endowments provided by high-ranking members of the guild, the commissioning of statues (andriantes), and the legal regulations governing the guild's funds and archives. It mentions the emperor Constantine as 'our master' and provides a rich list of the guild's hierarchy.
