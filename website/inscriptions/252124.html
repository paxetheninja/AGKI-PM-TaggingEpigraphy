This inscription is a Hellenistic decree issued by the city of Seleukeia (the name of Tralles during the Seleucid period) in honor of the Milesians. It emphasizes the 'syngeneia' (kinship) and 'philia' (friendship) between the two communities. The decree grants Milesians the right of 'politeia' (citizenship) in Seleukeia, including the right to hold office and 'proedria' (front-row seating) at festivals. It also mandates religious observances, specifically sacrifices to Apollo Didymeus (the patron of Miletus) and Zeus Larasios (the local deity of Tralles). The text was to be inscribed on a stone stele and set up in the sanctuary of Zeus Larasios.
