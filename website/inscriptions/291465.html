This inscription is an Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Ekudemos (244/3 BC). It honors the ephebes who served during the previous year (archonship of Philoneos, 246/5 BC) and their various military and physical instructors, including the paidotribes Hermodoros and the akontistes Lysikles. The text follows the standard formula for Athenian ephebic honors, including a publication clause for a stone stele to be placed in the Agora and a detailed roster of the young men listed by their tribes. The state of the text is fragmentary, with significant portions reconstructed from multiple joining pieces.
