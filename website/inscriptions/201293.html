AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IC IV 174) from Gortyna, Crete, dating to the late 3rd century BC, records a tripartite treaty between Gortyna, Hierapytna, and Priansos. The document includes a symmachy (military alliance), detailed territorial boundaries (horoi) for Priansos, and legal provisions for inter-city judicial cooperation. It concludes with a comprehensive religious oath invoking a variety of local and panhellenic deities, which serves to sanctify the agreement and establish the consequences of perjury. The text is a vital source for Cretan topography, dialect, and religious practice during the Hellenistic period.
