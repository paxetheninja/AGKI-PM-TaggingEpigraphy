AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a standard Delphic manumission record dating to the mid-2nd century BC. It documents the 'sale' of a Thracian slave woman named Nikata by her owner Mnasi-xenos to the god Apollo Pythios for the price of three minae. The act grants her freedom ('eleuthera'), though it includes a 'paramone' clause requiring her to remain with and serve Mnasi-xenos for the duration of his life and raise two children. The document is dated by the local archons and witnessed by the priests of Apollo and other citizens, including a resident of Amphissa.
