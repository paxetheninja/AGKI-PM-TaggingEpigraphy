AI Analysis Summary

This is a well-preserved example of a Delphic manumission by 'sale to the god'. It includes the standard dating by local archons (Antimachos in Delphi and Andronikos in Phocis), the identification of the seller (Mnasixenos) and the slave (Nikata, a Thracian), the price of three silver minae, and a 'paramone' clause requiring the slave to remain with the seller for a period and raise two children. It concludes with a list of religious and civic officials acting as witnesses and names Eukleidas as the scribe.
