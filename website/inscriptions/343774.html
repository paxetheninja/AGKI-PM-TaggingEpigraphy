AI Analysis Summary

This honorific decree from Samos (Pythagoreion), dating to the 2nd century BC, commemorates the career and public works of Aristomenes, son of Aristippos, during his tenure as agoranomos. The text highlights his religious piety through the dedication of statues of Demeter and Dionysos in the agoranomion, and his civic contribution through the construction of a stoa and a white marble gateway. A unique feature is the mention of public timekeeping devices (horologia) and a bronze dolphin used to indicate months and hours, emphasizing the agoranomos's role in regulating the market and public life.
