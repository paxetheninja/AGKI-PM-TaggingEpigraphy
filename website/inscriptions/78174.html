AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a 2nd-century BC honorific decree from the island of Tenos. It honors Apollonios, son of Hierokles, a physician from Miletos, for his professional excellence and his conduct during his residence in the city. The decree grants him the status of proxenos and benefactor (euergetes), awards him a crown of sacred olive from the sanctuary of Poseidon and Amphitrite, and grants him the right of proedria (front-row seating) at festivals. The text specifies that the decree should be inscribed on a stone stele and set up in the sanctuary of Poseidon and Amphitrite, a major religious center on Tenos, and that a copy should be sent to Miletos.
