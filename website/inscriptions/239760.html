AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Patron (ca. 100 BC). It documents the sale of a male slave of Sarmatian origin, named Sosipolis, to Apollo Pythios by Straton and Lysimachos, sons of Nikanor. The sale price is five silver minae. The document includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring the manumitted individual to remain in the service of his former masters for six years. It also specifies the legal protections for his freedom (asylia) and lists several witnesses, including priests of Apollo and other local officials. The text contains several lacunae but the legal formula is well-preserved and typical of the Delphic corpus.
