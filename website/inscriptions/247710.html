🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Ephesian Debt Law' (IEph 4), dates to approximately 297 BC, shortly after Lysimachus took control of Ephesus. It addresses a severe economic crisis caused by the 'Common War' (likely the wars between the Diadochi). The law provides a sophisticated mechanism for debt relief, distinguishing between different types of loans and property (georgia vs. tokismos). It mandates the use of arbitrators (diaitetai) and foreign judges (xenikon dikasterion) to resolve disputes, ensuring that the city's agricultural base remained viable while protecting the rights of creditors to a degree. The text is a crucial source for Hellenistic economic history and legal procedure.
