AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a legal record of manumission from Delphi, dated to the mid-1st century AD (47-66 AD) by the archonship of Kritolaos. It follows the standard Delphic formula where a slave (Thespis) is sold to Apollo Pythios by her owner (Menodoros) for a sum of 800 denarii to ensure her status as a free person (eleuthera). The text includes a 'paramone' clause requiring her to perform duties for a specified time and names Hermogenes as the guarantor (bebaioter). It was officially recorded by the city secretary Kallistratos and witnessed by priests and the strategos Lammenes.
