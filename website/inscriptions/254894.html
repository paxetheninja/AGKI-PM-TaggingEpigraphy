This inscription is a decree of the Ionian League (Koinon of the Ionians) dating to the early 3rd century BC. It honors Hippostratos of Miletos, a prominent general and 'philos' (friend) of King Lysimachos. The decree praises his fair and philanthropic administration of the Ionian cities under his command. As a reward, the League grants him a bronze equestrian statue to be set up at the Panionion, the central sanctuary of the League. The text specifies that the decree should be inscribed on the statue's base and on stone stelae in each member city to ensure public awareness of how the Ionians reward merit. The findspot in Smyrna (ISmyrn 577) represents one of these local copies or the original from the Panionion moved later.
