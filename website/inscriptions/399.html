AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of an Athenian financial inventory, specifically belonging to the 'Treasurers of the Other Gods' (Tamiai ton Allon Theon). Dated to 407/6 BCE, it lists sacred treasures and public assets stored in key locations such as the Opisthodomos of the Parthenon, the Eleusinion in the City, and the Bouleuterion. The items listed include Kyzicene staters, silver and gold vessels (phialai, cups), and even construction materials like tiles and ladders. The mention of Salaminokles as a dedicant and Smikythos as an official provides prosopographical links to the Athenian administration of the late 5th century BCE.
