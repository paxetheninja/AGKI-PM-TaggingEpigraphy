AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to the archonship of Anthesterios (158/7 BCE), is a major document concerning the Theseia festival in Athens. It honors Demetrios of Agryle for his successful management of the games as agonothetes. The text is a hybrid of a public decree and a victory list (fasti), recording winners in a wide array of events including the torch race (lampas), long-distance running (dolichos), wrestling (pale), boxing (pygme), and specialized military/equestrian events like hoplomachia and the anthippasia. It provides critical data on Athenian tribal organization and the participation of different age classes (paides, epheboi, andres) in the mid-Hellenistic period.
