AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to 216 BC and found at Olympia, records a significant political and religious agreement between the Acarnanian League and the city of Anactorium. Due to the financial exhaustion caused by wars, Anactorium was unable to maintain the sanctuary of Apollo at Actium. The decree transfers the administration of the sanctuary and the organization of the Actian games to the Acarnanian League. It details the appointment of joint officials (tax collectors, market overseers), the division of tax revenues (specifically the pentekoste), and the preservation of existing sacred laws. The text was to be displayed on two stelae: one at the sanctuary of Apollo Aktios and one at the panhellenic sanctuary of Olympia, explaining its findspot.
