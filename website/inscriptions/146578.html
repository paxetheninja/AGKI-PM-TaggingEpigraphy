AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Greek copy of the Edict of Diocletian on Maximum Prices, issued in 301 AD. It is a crucial economic document that lists maximum prices for various types of wine (Tiburtinum, Sabinum, Surrentinum, Falernum), honey, and meat, as well as daily wages for a wide array of craftsmen including stonemasons (lithourgoi), carpenters (tektones), mosaicists (mousiarioi), and painters (toichographoi). The wages are often specified as 'with maintenance' (trephetai) per day (meran). The values are recorded in denarii, here represented by the Greek talent/drachma notation common in Eastern copies of the edict.
