This inscription is a Samian honorific decree for Apollos, son of Sosigines, an Aeginetan. Dated shortly after 306 BC, it is historically significant for its mention of the festivals Antigoneia and Demetreia, which were established to honor the Diadochi Antigonos I Monophthalmos and Demetrios Poliorketes. Apollos is granted Samian citizenship (politeia), proxeny, and the right of entry and exit (eisplous and ekplous) in exchange for his services, including his cooperation with Samian ambassadors and his financial concessions regarding the festivals. The decree specifies the administrative process of enrollment into a tribe (phylÄ“), chiliastys, and hekatostys, and orders the stele to be erected in the sanctuary of Hera (Heraion).
