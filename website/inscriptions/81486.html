AI Analysis Summary

This inscription consists of three distinct honorific decrees from the city of Chalkis on Euboia, dating to the first half of the 2nd century BC. The first two decrees honor Sokrates of Sidon and Euboulos of Knidos for their general goodwill and contributions to the grain supply (sitonia). The third decree is particularly significant, honoring Kleon of Lacedaemon for providing a substantial interest-free loan of 13,500 drachmae to the city during a period of financial need for grain procurement. These texts illustrate the Hellenistic practice of 'euergetism', where cities relied on wealthy foreigners to manage food shortages and financial crises in exchange for civic honors, including proxeny, the right to own land (enktesis), and invitations to the Prytaneion.
