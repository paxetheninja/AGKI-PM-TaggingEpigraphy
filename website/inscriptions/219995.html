This inscription is a decree issued by the inhabitants of the village of Bousiris in the Letopolite nome (near the Giza Pyramids). It dates to the reign of Nero (55-59 AD). The villagers resolved to erect a stone stele dedicated to the god Helios Harmachis (the Great Sphinx) to honor the Prefect of Egypt, Tiberius Claudius Balbillus. The text praises Balbillus for his benevolence and specifically mentions his visit to the site, where he worshipped the Sphinx and cleared away the sand that had accumulated around it. The text is significant for its mention of the Sphinx (Harmachis) and the Roman administration's interaction with Egyptian cult sites.
