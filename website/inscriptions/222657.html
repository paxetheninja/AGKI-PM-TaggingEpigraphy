AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to 78 BC during the reign of Ptolemy XII Auletes and Cleopatra Tryphaena, is a collective dedication by a group of military settlers or 'foreigners' (xenoi) stationed in Hermopolis Magna. The group, which includes individuals from Apollonopolis and other 'co-citizens', dedicated a sanctuary (hieron), an enclosure (peribolos), and all appurtenances to Apollo and the 'associated gods' (synestioi theoi). The text is significant for its extensive prosopographical list, which provides insight into the ethnic and social composition of the Ptolemaic military administration in Middle Egypt during the late Hellenistic period.
