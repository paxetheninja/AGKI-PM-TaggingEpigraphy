AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian prytany list for the tribe Akamantis, dated to the archonship of Tineius Ponticus (168/9 AD). It follows the standard Roman-period format for such documents, beginning with an invocation to Good Fortune (Agathe Tyche) and the names of the eponymous archon and the hoplite general. The text lists the secretary of the councilors, Philoumenos of Kephale, who commissioned the inscription to honor his colleagues. The body of the text consists of a catalogue of councilors (prytaneis) grouped by their demes (Sphettioi, Thorikioi, etc.), followed by a list of 'aisitoi', which includes high-ranking religious officials like the Hierophant and the Dadouchos of the Eleusinian Mysteries, as well as administrative staff like the secretary of the Council and People and the clerk of the Skias.
