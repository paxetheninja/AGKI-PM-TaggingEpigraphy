AI Analysis Summary

This is a significant Athenian decree from the early 3rd century AD honoring Marcus Ulpius Eubiotus, a prominent benefactor and consular. The text highlights his extraordinary generosity during a grain shortage (sitoneia), for which he and his sons received unprecedented honors, including bronze statues in the Synedrion and Prytaneion, and special seating at the Panathenaic and Dionysiac games. It reflects the social and political structure of Roman Athens, mentioning the Areopagus, the Boule, and various religious officials like the Hierophant, Exegetai, and Manteis.
