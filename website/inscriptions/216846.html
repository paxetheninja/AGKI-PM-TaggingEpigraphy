AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to 48 AD, contains an edict from Gnaeus Vergilius Capito, the Roman Prefect of Egypt. It is a significant administrative document aimed at curbing corruption and the abuse of power by Roman soldiers and local officials, specifically regarding the illegal requisitioning of transport and supplies (angareia). The prefect orders that the edict be published in both the metropolis and individual villages to ensure the local population is aware of their rights and the penalties for those who extort them. It also details the process for financial auditing and reporting through the imperial freedman Basilides.
