AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dated to 162 AD or shortly after, is a significant honorific monument for T. Flavius Hierophantes of the deme Paiania. It provides a detailed cursus honorum, listing his service as Eponymous Archon, Panegyriarch, Keryx of the Areopagus, Hoplite General, and Gymnasiarch. Crucially, it records his religious prestige as the Hierophant at Eleusis, noting that he initiated the Emperor Lucius Verus into the Mysteries and received the 'strophion' (priestly headband) from Emperor Antoninus Pius. The text emphasizes his lineage, tracing his descent from other prominent Athenian officials like Flavius Alcibiades and Flavius Leosthenes, and highlights his piety and generosity toward the city.
