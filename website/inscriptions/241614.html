AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Delphi (182/181 BC) records a complex testamentary foundation by Alkesippos of Calydon. He dedicates a substantial sum of gold and silver to Apollo and the city to establish an annual festival called the Alkesippeia. The document also functions as a will, providing for the manumission of his slave Theutiman, giving instructions for his own burial, and naming guardians and witnesses for the legal act. The list of witnesses includes high-ranking officials such as a Strategos and a Polemarch.
