This inscription is a decree from the mid-2nd century BC (153/52 BC) found on the island of Thera. It was issued by the 'aleiphomenoi' (the association of those who use the gymnasium) to honor Baton, son of Philon, for his exceptional service as gymnasiarch. Baton served in this capacity five times, demonstrating significant 'philotimia' (love of honor/generosity) by maintaining order, funding prizes for athletic games dedicated to Hermes, Herakles, and the Ptolemaic King, and providing for the gymnasium's needs. The decree grants him golden crowns and a painted portrait (eikon), and orders the text to be inscribed on a stone stele and placed in the most conspicuous part of the gymnasium. The transcription provided shows significant character loss, particularly vowels, which is typical of weathered stone or specific lacunae in the original find.
