AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated between 124-116 BC. It documents the fictive sale of a female slave named Ages, born in the household (oikogenes), by her owner Aristomeneia of Ambryssos to the god Apollo Pythios. The act is performed with the consent of Aristomeneia's mother, Xenokrateia. The text follows the standard Delphic formula, including the names of the Delphic archon (Timokrates), the Phokian strategos (Eupalidas), and members of the council (bouleutai). It specifies the price paid and guarantees the slave's future status as free (eleuthera) and untouchable (anaphaptos) by any former masters.
