AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, identified as IDelos 509, is a significant Hellenistic law from Delos regulating the trade of fuel (wood, charcoal, and brushwood). It aims to prevent monopolies and price gouging by requiring importers to declare their stock and prices to the 'pentekostologoi' (collectors of the 2% tax) and 'agoranomoi' (market officials). It details the 'eisangelia' (denunciation) process for violators, the role of the 'thirty' (dikastai), and the distribution of fines between the accuser and the public treasury. The text provides crucial insight into the economic administration of the Delian emporion.
