AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Eretria, dating to approximately 100 BC. It records the extraordinary generosity of Theopompos, son of Archedemos, who donated 40,000 drachmas to create a permanent endowment for the provision of oil (elaiochrestion) in the city's gymnasium. The decree outlines the honors bestowed upon him, including golden crowns and bronze statues to be placed in the sanctuary of Artemis Amarysia and the gymnasium. It also includes a 'sanctio' or legal clause protecting the fund from being diverted to other uses, with heavy fines payable to Artemis for any official who proposes a change to the fund's purpose.
