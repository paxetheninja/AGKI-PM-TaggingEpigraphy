AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from the sanctuary of Hekate at Lagina (part of the territory of Stratonikeia) honors Diomedes, son of Sosandros, for his extensive religious and civic contributions. The text details his service as a priest (hiereus), his performance of the stephanephoria, and his funding of public banquets (deipna) and wine distributions (oinou diapompas) during the Panamareia festival. It notably mentions a divine manifestation (energeia) of Hekate involving a bull that led the priest to the bouleuterion. Diomedes is also praised for his hospitality toward Roman officials, including a quaestor (tamias) and senators, as well as athletes and performers. The inscription concludes by listing his family members who shared in his liturgical expenses.
