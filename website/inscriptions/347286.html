🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, IG II² 244, is a major Athenian law (nomos) dating to approximately 337/6 BC, shortly after the Battle of Chaeronea. It mandates the repair and strengthening of the fortifications of Athens, specifically the Long Walls and the defenses of Piraeus and Mounichia. The text is remarkable for its technical detail, including the 'syngraphai' (specifications) for masonry work, stone cutting, and the construction of towers and stairs. It also outlines the administrative and financial oversight by the nomothetai, the teichopoioi (wall-builders), and the treasurers, reflecting the urgent defensive measures taken by Athens in the face of Macedonian hegemony.
