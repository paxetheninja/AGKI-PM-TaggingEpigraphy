AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant civic decree from Aigai in Aeolis, dating to approximately 281 BC, shortly after the Battle of Corupedium. It documents the city's formal establishment of the ruler cult for Seleucus I Nicator and his son Antiochus I Soter. The decree is highly detailed, ordering the creation of two new civic tribes (Seleukis and Antiochis), the renaming of the Prytaneion and Strategion to the Seleukeion and Antiocheion respectively, and the erection of statues and altars. It also regulates religious honors, including a monthly sacrifice and a new priesthood. The text concludes with a list of citizens appointed to oversee the implementation of these honors.
