AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a standard Delphic manumission record dating likely to the 2nd century BC (based on the archon Sosipitros). It follows the 'sale to the god' formula where Damosthenes, with his daughter Kleas's consent, sells the slave Zopyra to Apollo Pythios for 5 minae. The text specifies that Zopyra is to be free (eleuthera) and protected from seizure (anaphaptos). It includes a list of witnesses, including the priest Thambos and the neokoros Eukles, as well as members of the council (boule). The presence of a guarantor (bebaioter), Damophanes, ensures the legality of the transaction against future claims of ownership.
