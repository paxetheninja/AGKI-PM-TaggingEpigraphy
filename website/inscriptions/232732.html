AI Analysis Summary

This is an Athenian honorary decree dating to the late 5th century BC, likely during the Peloponnesian War. It honors Antiochus and Phanosthenes (likely Phanosthenes of Andros) for their services to the city, specifically regarding the supply of naval materials like oars. The text involves several Athenian administrative bodies and officials, including the Council (Boule), the Assembly (Demos), the Generals (Strategoi), the Trireme-makers (Triereopoioi), and the Treasurers of the Greeks (Hellenotamiai). It follows the standard procedure for public honors, including the granting of the title 'euergetes' (benefactor) and the publication of the decree on a marble stele.
