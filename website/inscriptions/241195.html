This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Soion (c. 150-140 BC). It documents the 'fictitious sale' of a Syrian slave named Kastalia to Apollo Pythios by Polia, daughter of Philon, with the consent of her family. The price is set at four silver minae. The text includes standard legal protections for the manumitted person, such as the 'anaphaptos' clause (freedom from seizure), and lists a large number of witnesses, including priests of Apollo and local officials from Delphi, Lilaia, and Daulis.
