AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major honorific decree from the Attic deme of Rhamnous, dating to the period of the Chremonidean War (c. 268-262 BC). It honors Epichares, son of Laches, for his exemplary service as hipparch and later as strategos. The text provides detailed historical evidence of military life and defense strategies during the war, including the protection of the harvest (grain and grapes), the construction of 'phylakteria' (guard posts) and towers, the management of Ptolemaic allied troops under Patroclus, and the ransoming of prisoners captured by pirates. It reflects the local autonomy and military responsibilities of the demesmen in the border fortresses of Attica.
