AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Amphictyonic decree from Delphi dating to 184/183 BC, during the archonship of Kraton. It honors Nikostratos, son of Anaxippos, a Thessalian from Larissa, for his significant diplomatic contributions. Nikostratos served as a hieromnemon and was part of an embassy to Rome, where he addressed the Senate (synkletos), generals (strategoi), and tribunes (demarchoi) to promote the interests of the Amphictyonic League and the Greeks. The decree grants him a crown of the god and a bronze statue to be erected in the sanctuary of Apollo Pythios. The text reflects the complex geopolitical landscape of the early 2nd century BC, highlighting the interaction between Greek federal bodies and the rising power of Rome.
