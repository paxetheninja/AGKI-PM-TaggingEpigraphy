This 2nd-century BC inscription from Larisa records a series of testimonies (martyriai) regarding a territorial dispute between the cities of Kondaea and Mopsion. The witnesses describe specific geographical boundaries, including the confluence of the Peneios and Europos rivers, the 'Dionyseion' peak, and various valleys (pharangos). The text is a crucial source for the historical geography of the Pelasgiotis region and the legal procedures of inter-city arbitration in Hellenistic Thessaly. It was intended to be displayed in the sanctuary of Apollo Kerdoios, the patron deity of the Thessalian League.
