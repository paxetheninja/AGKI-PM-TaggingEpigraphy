AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to 173 BC during the archonship of Aiakidas. It documents the fictitious sale of a Thracian slave named Soterichos to Apollo Pythios by his owner Mantas, with the consent of Mantas' sister, Sosa. The text includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring the freedman to remain in service to Mantas for a specified duration. It also details legal protections for the freedman against seizure and establishes a panel of three men (Diodoros, Mnasi-theos, and Kleudamos) to arbitrate potential disputes. The presence of numerous witnesses and a guarantor (Damanes) confirms its status as a public legal document.
