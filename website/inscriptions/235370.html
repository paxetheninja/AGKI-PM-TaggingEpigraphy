This inscription is a significant Athenian decree from 196/5 BC honoring the statesman Kephisodoros. It details his thirty years of service, including his role as military treasurer (tamias ton stratiotikon) and his diplomatic efforts to secure alliances and grain supplies for the city. The decree is a primary source for Athenian resistance against Philip V of Macedon. It grants Kephisodoros high honors: two bronze statues (one in the Agora and one in the Piraeus emporion), sitesis (dining in the Prytaneion), and proedria (front-row seating at festivals).
