AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of a temple inventory from the island of Delos, dating to approximately 156/5 BC during the Athenian Cleruchy. The text meticulously records the weight (in mnae) and physical condition of sacred items, such as statues (agalmata), vessels (kadiskos), and furniture (thrones). It specifically mentions items located in the Dodekatheon (Sanctuary of the Twelve Gods) and the Thesmophorion (Sanctuary of Demeter and Persephone). The mention of gilded (epichrysa) and wooden (xylinas) elements provides insight into the material culture of Hellenistic religious dedications.
