AI Analysis Summary

This Athenian honorary decree (181-170 BC) honors Nikoratos, son of Nikoratos, an Alexandrian. He is recognized for his goodwill (eunoia) and assistance to Athenian citizens visiting Cyprus, where he served under the Ptolemaic governor (strategos). The decree grants him the status of Proxenos and Euergetes (benefactor) and awards him a foliage crown. It mandates the publication of the text on a stone stele to be placed in the sanctuary of the Demos and the Graces (Charites) in Athens.
