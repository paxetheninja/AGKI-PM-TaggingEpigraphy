This inscription is an Athenian honorary decree dating to the period 181-170 BCE. It honors Nikoratos, son of Nikoratos, an Alexandrian who was in the service of Ptolemaios (likely Ptolemy, son of Polycrates), the Ptolemaic governor of Cyprus. Nikoratos is praised for his assistance to Athenian citizens visiting Cyprus. The decree grants him the status of proxenos and euergetes (benefactor) and orders the publication of the decree on a stone stele to be placed in the sanctuary of Demos and the Graces (Charites). The text follows standard Hellenistic Athenian diplomatic and administrative formulas.
