AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the Athenian 'Mining Leases' (poletai records) found in the Agora. It dates to approximately 350/49 BCE, though it references earlier archonships (Thoudemos 353/2 and Agathokles 357/6). The text provides a detailed administrative catalogue of silver mines in the Laurion region, identifying them by name (e.g., Artemisiakon, Phaistiakon), location (demes like Sunion and Thorikos), and the individuals who registered the leases. It also specifies the physical markers (stelae) that defined the boundaries of the mining concessions.
