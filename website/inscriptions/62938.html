This inscription represents a significant administrative and financial document from the sanctuary of Delos, specifically the accounts of the treasurers (tamiai) during the archonship of Periandros (c. 172-170 BC). The text meticulously lists monthly expenditures for religious maintenance, including the purchase of wood for altars (xyla epi bomous), sacrificial animals for purification (choiros to hieron katharsasthai), and funds allocated for the grain supply (sitonia). It provides insight into the complex economic and religious administration of the Delian sanctuary during the Hellenistic period.
