AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a typical Delphic manumission record dating to the archonship of Pyrrhos. It documents the sale of a female slave named Euameris, of Boeotian origin, to Pythian Apollo by her owners Kleonika and Bouthora. The sale is a legal fiction to grant the slave her freedom (eleutheria) and inviolability (anephaptos). The document includes a 'paramone' clause requiring Euameris to stay with Kleonika and perform her duties as long as Kleonika lives. It also lists the price paid, a guarantor (Dorotheos), and several witnesses, including the priests of Apollo.
