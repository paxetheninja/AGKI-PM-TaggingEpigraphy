This inscription is a comprehensive ephebic catalogue from Athens, dated to the archonship of G. Albidius Secundus (192/3 AD). It follows the standard late Roman Athenian format, listing the kosmetes (superintendent), the antikosmetes, and the technical staff (paidotribes, grammateus, etc.), followed by the sophronistai (moral overseers) and the ephebes themselves, categorized by their ancestral tribes (Erechtheis, Aegeis, etc.). The text also records the ephebes' participation in naval battles (naumachia) at Mounichia and their victories in imperial and local games (Antinoeia, Hadrianeia, Theseia). It includes a specific mention of funds provided for sacrifices for the health of the Emperor (Commodus), highlighting the intersection of civic education and the imperial cult.
