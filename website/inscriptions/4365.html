This inscription is a classic Athenian ephebic catalogue from the Roman period (192/3 AD), specifically the archonship of Galvidius Secundus. It serves as an official record of the young men (ephebes) who completed their civic and military training, as well as the staff (cosmetes, sophronistai, didaskaloi) who managed them. The text is significant for its mention of the 'Sebastophorika' funds, which highlights the financial relationship between the Roman imperial administration and the Athenian ephebeia. It also provides a detailed list of religious and athletic festivals (Antinoeia, Adrianeia, Theseia, etc.) that formed the core of ephebic life, and mentions the Diogeneion, the primary gymnasium for ephebic education in Athens.
