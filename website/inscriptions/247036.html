AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal administrative record (paradosis) from the sanctuary of Apollo at Didyma, dating to the Hellenistic period. It documents the transfer of sacred objects and funds between outgoing and incoming boards of treasurers (tamiai). The text lists various votive offerings, including a silver tripod from the Milesian people, phialai from the Cyzicenes and private individuals like Euboulos of Iasos, and gold items dedicated to Artemis. The use of 'Alexandrian drachmas' as a weight standard is characteristic of the post-Alexander era in Ionia. The inscription serves both as a religious record of piety and a legal document ensuring financial accountability within the temple administration.
