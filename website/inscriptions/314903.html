AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree from the city of Thespiai in Boiotia, dating to the late 3rd century BC (ca. 217-212 BC). It records the city's decision to honor foreign judges sent from Delphi to adjudicate local matters. The text follows standard Boiotian dialectal forms and administrative formulas, including the archon dating (Theogonos) and the month (Panamos, reconstructed). It details the virtuous conduct of the judges, their adherence to justice, and the city's reciprocal honors, including the publication of the decree on stone steles in a prominent location. The text is fragmentary, with significant reconstructions in the latter half regarding the administrative costs and the names of officials.
