AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a standard Athenian honorific decree from 213/2 BC, during the archonship of Herakleitos. It honors the prytaneis of the tribe Antigonis for their religious and administrative services, specifically mentioning sacrifices to Apollo Prostaterios and Artemis Boulaia. A significant historical feature is the erasure of the tribe name 'Antigonis' in lines 5 and 16, which reflects the political changes in 200 BC when Athens abolished the Macedonian tribes. The decree was passed in the Theater and was to be set up in the Prytanikon.
