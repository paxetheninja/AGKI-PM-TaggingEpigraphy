AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Hellenistic decree from the deme of Halasarna on the island of Kos, dating to the late 3rd century BC (monarchia of Philotas). It addresses the urgent need to complete the Temple of Apollo, specifically focusing on the roof and doors to prevent the deterioration of existing work. The decree mandates the election of four men to oversee the project and establishes a subscription (epangelia) system where citizens and residents contribute at least 30 drachmas, payable in three installments. The text provides significant insight into local religious administration and public financing in the Hellenistic period.
