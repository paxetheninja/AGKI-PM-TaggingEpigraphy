AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant administrative document from Halicarnassos, Caria, dating to the mid-5th century BCE. It records the sale of lands and houses that had become the property of the gods Apollo and Athena, likely through confiscation or debt default. The text lists the names of the purchasers, the location of the properties (frequently in the district of Salmakis), and the prices paid in drachmae. It provides invaluable evidence for the prosopography of Halicarnassos, showing a mix of Greek and Carian names (such as Panyassis and Sswllos), and illustrates the economic role of the city's sanctuaries and the neopoiai (temple wardens) in managing sacred assets.
