AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a standard Athenian prytany decree dating to 190/89 BC, during the archonship of Hippias. It honors the prytaneis of the tribe Akamantis for their religious and administrative duties. The text follows a rigid formula: it mentions the performance of sacrifices (thysiai), praises the treasurer and secretary, and orders the publication of the decree on a stone stele in the Prytanikon. The lower part of the stele preserves a list of the individual councilors (prytaneis) by name, which is a crucial source for Athenian prosopography and deme organization in the Hellenistic period.
