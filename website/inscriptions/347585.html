It honors the ephebes of that year and their kosmetes, Theoboulos, for their exemplary behavior, piety towards the gods, and discipline. The text details their participation in major festivals such as the Dionysia, Panathenaia, and Ptolemaia. It also lists the professional military instructors (teachers) who trained them in various skills: hoplomachia (heavy infantry combat), archery (toxotes), javelin (akontistes), and the use of the catapult (katapeltaphetes). The decree orders the publication of the honors on a stone stele in the Agora and the dedication of a monument in the Lyceum.
