AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Delphi (dated to the archonship of Thrasykles, c. 150-140 BC) records the manumission of two slaves, Prostateris (of Koroneian origin) and Palles, by Philoxenos. The legal mechanism used is the 'fictive sale' to Apollo Pythios for a price of five silver minae. The document includes standard Delphic manumission clauses: the 'paramone' (service requirement), the 'bebaiosis' (guarantee by Hippon and Ammendas), and the 'asylia' (protection against re-enslavement). The text concludes with a list of witnesses, including the priests of Apollo and the local archons.
