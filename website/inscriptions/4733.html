This inscription (IG IIÂ² 2492) is a legal contract (misthosis) issued by the Attic deme of Axone in 345/4 BCE, during the archonship of Euboulos. It records the 40-year lease of the 'Phelleis' estate to Autokles and Auteas for an annual rent of 152 drachmas. The text details agricultural obligations, including the planting of vines and the maintenance of olive trees, and provides legal safeguards for the lessees against war damage or administrative changes. A supplementary decree moved by Eteokles concerns the sale of olive wood and the adjustment of rent based on the interest from the sale proceeds. The document was to be published on two stone steles, one in the sanctuary of Hebe and another in the local lesche.
