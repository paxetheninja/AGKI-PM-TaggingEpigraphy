AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Miletus (Milet I 9, 368) is an honorific record for a group of gymnasiarchs (Dionysios, Artemon, and Apollonios) serving during the eponymous year of Pristanor. The text details their liturgical services, specifically their funding and organization of sacrifices to Hermes and Herakles in the month of Thargelion, and an 'enkausis' ritual in the month of Posideon. It highlights their generosity toward the gerousia (council of elders), including meat distributions and the provision of oil for the gymnasium, emphasizing that they acted in accordance with the city's decrees and maintained the dignity of their office.
