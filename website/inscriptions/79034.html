AI Analysis Summary

This 3rd-century BC decree from Aegiale on Amorgos records a pirate raid where over thirty people (including women, girls, free citizens, and slaves) were captured. Two brothers, Hegesippos and Antipappos, who were also taken captive, successfully negotiated with the pirate leader Sokleidas to ransom the captives. For their bravery and 'philotimia' (ambition/generosity), the city honors them with crowns to be announced at the Dionysia festival and sets up a stele in the temple of Athena Polias.
