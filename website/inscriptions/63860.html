AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of a Delian inventory, specifically recording the sacred property and votive offerings held in the Letoon (Sanctuary of Leto) or transferred between administrators. It dates to the period of the Athenian Cleruchy (post-167 BC). The text meticulously lists statues (agalmata), vessels (phialai, skyphoi), and furniture (tables, chests), often specifying the donor (including members of the Ptolemaic dynasty and local officials like architheoroi) and the weight of precious metal items. The mention of the 'Deliades' (chorus of Delian maidens) and various royal dedications highlights the international and ritual significance of the sanctuary.
