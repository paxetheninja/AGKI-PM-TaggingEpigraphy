AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major historical document from the Aksumite Empire, written in Greek. It records the military campaigns of King Ezana against the Bougaites. It follows a standard royal Aksumite pattern: a list of titles, a narrative of the campaign and the humane treatment/resettlement of the captives, a dedication of statues and a stele to the god Ares (the Greek equivalent of the Aksumite war god Mahrem), and a closing curse to protect the monument. The use of Greek reflects the international status of Aksum in Late Antiquity.
