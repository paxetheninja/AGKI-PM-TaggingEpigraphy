🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant part of the Athenian Naval Catalogues (specifically IG II² 1628), dating to the archonship of Kephisodoros (326/5 BCE). It documents the meticulous administrative oversight of the Athenian navy, listing the handover of triremes and quadriremes (tetrereis) between boards of dockyard curators. It details the 'wooden equipment' (skeue xylina) and 'hanging equipment' (skeue kremasta) for each vessel, identifies the trierarchs responsible for them, and records financial settlements related to the grain fund (sitonikon). The mention of the orator Demades and the trierarch Konon (descendant of the famous admiral) provides vital prosopographical data for late 4th-century Athens.
