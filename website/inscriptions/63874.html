AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of a larger inventory from the sanctuary of Apollo on Delos, dating to the period of the Second Athenian Domination (166–140/39 BC). It meticulously records the transfer of sacred objects between temple officials (hieropoioi). The text lists various precious items, including silver bowls (phialai), golden crowns (stephanoi), rings (daktylioi), and even military equipment like a helmet (perikephalaia). Many items are associated with Hellenistic monarchs (Seleukos, Antigonos, Demetrios, Ptolemaios), reflecting the international prestige of the Delian sanctuary. The presence of Roman names like Publius and Popillius indicates the growing Roman influence on the island during this period.
