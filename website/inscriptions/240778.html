AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to approximately 20 AD. It follows the traditional Delphic formula of a 'fictive sale' to Apollo Pythios. Philon, son of Philon, an Elatean, manumits a female slave named Phelima for the price of three silver minae. The text specifies that she is to be free (eleuthera) and untouchable (anepaphos) by any claimant. The document is dated by the archonships of Xenokrates and Neikandros and lists several witnesses, including the priest of Apollo and the secretary of the city. The presence of a guarantor (bebaioter) and the deposit of the document in the public archives are also noted.
