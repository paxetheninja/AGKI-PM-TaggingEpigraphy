AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to the Roman period (20-46 AD). It documents the sale of a home-bred slave named Epktesis by her owner Neikas to the god Apollo Pythios for three minae of silver. This 'fictive sale' was the standard mechanism in Delphi to grant a slave legal freedom. The text specifies that Epktesis is to be free and untouchable (anaphaptos), provided she fulfills the 'paramone' condition of staying with Neikas for the remainder of her life. The document is dated by the archonship of Dorotheos and witnessed by priests of Apollo and other citizens, following the strict legal and religious protocols of the sanctuary.
