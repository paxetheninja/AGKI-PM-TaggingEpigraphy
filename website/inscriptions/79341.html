This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Samothrace dating to the late 3rd century BC (c. 228-225 BC). It honors Hippomedon, son of Agesilaus, a Spartan who served as a Ptolemaic governor (strategos) over the Hellespont and Thracian regions. The text highlights his military protection of the island during a period of instability, his piety in participating in the Samothracian Mysteries, and his financial generosity in providing loans for mercenary pay. It also touches upon administrative matters such as grain export privileges and the settlement of cleruchs (military colonists) to ensure the island's security and agricultural productivity.
