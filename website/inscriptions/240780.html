AI Analysis Summary

This is a typical Delphic manumission record dating to the Roman period, as evidenced by the presence of a Roman citizen (Publius Memmius) among the witnesses. It documents the sale of two slaves to Pythian Apollo by Sopatra, daughter of Dionysius, for 6 minae of silver. The text outlines the legal protections for the newly freed women, including their status as 'anepaphos' (untouchable by former owners) and a 'paramone' clause requiring them to remain with Euameris for a specified time. The inscription is dated by the archonship of Theoxenus and includes a full list of administrative and religious officials as witnesses.
