This inscription is a decree from the Sicilian city of Gela, found at the Asklepieion of Kos. It dates to 242 BC and is part of a large dossier of responses from various Greek cities to the Koan embassy requesting recognition of the panhellenic status of the Asklepieia games and the asylia (sacred inviolability) of the sanctuary. The text highlights the 'syngeneia' (kinship) between Gela and Kos, noting that the Koans were 'synoikistai' (co-founders) of Gela. It details the reception of the Koan ambassadors (theoroi), the acceptance of the sacrifice to Asklepios, and the provision of hospitality (xenia) and financial support for the sacrifice.
