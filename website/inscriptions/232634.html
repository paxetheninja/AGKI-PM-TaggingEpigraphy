AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the poletai (public auctioneers) records from Athens, dating to the archonship of Pythodoros (402/1 BCE). It documents the sale or lease of confiscated properties following the restoration of democracy after the rule of the Thirty Tyrants. Each entry typically lists the registrant (epigrapse), the property description with its neighbors (geitones), the buyer (priato), the guarantor (eggyetes), and the initial installment payment (katabole). The text mentions numerous Attic demes and individuals involved in these legal and financial transactions.
