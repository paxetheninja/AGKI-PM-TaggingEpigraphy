AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Protogenes Decree' (IosPE I² 32), is a cornerstone of Hellenistic epigraphy from the Northern Black Sea. It honors Protogenes for his extraordinary 'euergetism' during a period of severe crisis in Olbia. The text describes multiple instances of 'sitodeia' (grain shortage), threats from the Galatians and Scythian tribes (Saitai, Skiri), and the arrival of King Saitapharnes demanding tribute. Protogenes is praised for providing interest-free loans, donating large sums of gold, and personally overseeing the fortification of the city walls when the public treasury was empty. The decree provides invaluable data on the economic life, social structure, and precarious geopolitical situation of a Greek colony on the Scythian frontier.
