AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree (psephisma) of the Eleans dating to the Roman Imperial period (reign of Trajan or Hadrian). It honors the pancratiast Tiberius Claudius Rufus for his extraordinary performance at the Olympic Games. The text details how Rufus, after being matched against the athlete Kalleas, fought with such endurance that the match lasted until nightfall ('mechri nyktos'). The decree, initiated following a report by the Roman official Marcus Vettilenus Laetus, grants Rufus the right to set up a statue at Olympia. The text is a significant example of agonistic culture and the intersection of local civic honors with Roman administrative oversight in the 2nd century AD.
