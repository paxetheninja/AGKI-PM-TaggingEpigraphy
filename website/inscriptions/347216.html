AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal Athenian state decree dated to the archonship of Euthykritos (328/7 BC). It honors Dendrokles, son of Kleinias of the deme Kerameis, for his pious and diligent service as the priest of Asklepios. The decree specifically mentions his care for the sanctuary and the theater, as well as his successful performance of sacrifices. As a reward, he is granted a gold crown and public praise. The text provides significant administrative detail, including the names of the secretary, the presiding tribe (Antiochis), and the proposers of the motion. It concludes with instructions for the treasurer to fund the inscription and for the secretary to set up the stele in the Asklepieion.
