This inscription is an Athenian honorific decree from c. 200 BCE. It honors Aris, son of Kothon of Byzantium, along with several trierarchs and a treasurer from the same city. Aris is granted the status of proxenos and euergetes, as well as enktesis (the right to own land and a house in Attica). The decree follows standard diplomatic protocols, including an invitation to hospitality (xenia) in the Prytaneion and the requirement to set up a stone stele on the Acropolis. The text is fragmentary but the formulas are well-preserved enough to identify the specific honors and individuals involved.
