AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Damostratos (c. 150-140 BC). It documents the 'sale' of a female slave named Eutaxia and her son Parnassios to Apollo Pythios by their owner, Andronikos. This legal fiction ensured their status as free (eleutheroi) and protected them from future enslavement. The text includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring the manumitted individuals to remain in service to Andronikos until his death. It concludes with a list of witnesses, including the priest of Apollo and several local officials.
