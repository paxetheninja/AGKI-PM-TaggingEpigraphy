AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Pelops (165/4 BC). It honors Pamphilos, son of Archon, from the deme of Eleusis, for his exemplary service as demarch. The text details his religious contributions, including the performance of sacrifices to Demeter, Kore, and Dionysos, the organization of the Kalamaia festival, and the management of processions and theatrical contests. The decree specifies that it should be inscribed on a stone stele and erected in the courtyard of the sanctuary at Eleusis, with the cost covered by the military treasurer. The latter part of the text contains a second motion by Xenokrates reinforcing these honors.
