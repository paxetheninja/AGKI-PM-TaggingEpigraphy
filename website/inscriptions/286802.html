AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Hellenistic decree from Magarsos (Antiocheia in Cilicia) dating to approximately 140 BC. It formalizes diplomatic relations and kinship (syngeneia) with the neighboring city of Antiocheia-on-the-Kydnos (Tarsos). The text details the religious honors to be performed, including a procession from the Hestia Boulaia to the temple of Athena Magarsia, the sacrifice of a golden-horned heifer, and the participation of civic officials (prytaneis, demiurge, gymnasiarch) and youth (ephebes). It also establishes a festival truce (ekecheiria) and mandates the public reading of the decree and the erection of a marble stele.
