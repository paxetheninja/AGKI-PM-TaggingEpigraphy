AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IEph 2) dates to the 4th century BCE and records a mass death sentence passed by Ephesian authorities against 45 men from Sardis. The crime involved sacrilege (asebeia) and physical assault (hybris) against Ephesian sacred envoys (theoroi) who were traveling to the sanctuary of Artemis in Sardis to deliver ritual garments (chitons). The text is a crucial source for understanding international sacred law, the relationship between Ephesus and Sardis, and Lydian onomastics, as it provides a long list of names with complex patronymics.
