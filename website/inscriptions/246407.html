AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Thespiae (Boeotia), dating to the Hellenistic period (3rd century BC), records a public decree followed by a subscription list (epidosis). The decree establishes strict financial controls over funds collected for the completion of a temple (naos), threatening officials (prytaneis) with fines of 3,000 drachmas for misappropriation. It mandates that the decree and the names of those who promised and gave money be inscribed on the temple's doorpost (parastas). The subsequent list provides a valuable prosopographical record of the citizens of Thespiae, often noting contributions made on behalf of sons or brothers.
