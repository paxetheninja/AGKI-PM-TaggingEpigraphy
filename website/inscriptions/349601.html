This inscription is a Hellenistic decree from the island of Kos, dating to approximately the mid-3rd century BC. It honors the physician Xenotimos, son of Timoxenos, for his exceptional service during a period of widespread illness (epidemic). The text notes that while other public physicians were themselves incapacitated by the disease, Xenotimos volunteered his services to the citizens without distinction. For his 'eunoia' (goodwill) and professional skill ('iatrike techne'), the city awards him a gold crown and public praise to be announced during the festival of the Dionysia. The text is fragmentary, particularly at the beginning and end, with significant restorations required for the formulaic sections.
