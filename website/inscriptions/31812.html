This inscription from Thouria, dating to the late Hellenistic period (2nd/1st c. BC), is a regulatory document concerning the sitonia (public grain supply). It details the financial responsibilities of the polemarchoi, the oversight role of the synedroi (councilors), and the administrative duties of the grammateus (secretary). The text provides a complex set of instructions for the purchase (katagoraxis) and distribution (diadosis) of grain, ensuring that the process follows the 'diagramma' (regulation) and 'nomoi' (laws). It reflects the high level of bureaucratic organization in Messenian cities regarding food security and public finance.
