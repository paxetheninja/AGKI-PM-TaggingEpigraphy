This inscription is a decree from the fortress of Rhamnous, dated to approximately 225 BC (archonship of Niketes, implied by 'epi Niketou'). It honors Menandros, son of Teisandros of Eitea, who served as a trierarch. The text details his contributions, including spending his own funds for the ship's equipment, caring for the crew (providing oil and sacrifices), and ensuring the safety of those sailing with him. The decree authorizes a gold crown and the erection of the stele, overseen by a committee of three men: Timokrates of Oa, Archestratos (son of Aischines), and Diokles of Hamaxanteia. The text provided contains lacunae and missing characters typical of a transcription of a worn stone surface.
