AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the Athenian naval catalogues (tabulae curatorum navalium), specifically dating to the mid-4th century BC. It records the inventory of equipment for triremes, including various types of oars (zygia, thalamia), masts (histos), rudders (pedalia), and ladders (klimakides). The text lists the names of trierarchs and other officials responsible for the return or maintenance of this gear, often identified by their demotics (e.g., Myrrinousios, Lakiades). These records were essential for the administration of the Athenian navy and were typically displayed in the shipyards of the Piraeus.
