AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal manumission record from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Antiphilos. It documents the sale of two female slaves, Anasphoron and Sotera, to Apollo Pythios for the sum of 8 silver minae. The manumission is conditional upon a 'paramone' clause, requiring the freed women to remain with their former owner, Eurydika, for the duration of her life. The text lists various civic and religious officials, including the strategos Philon and the polemarch Damon, as well as several witnesses, providing a detailed snapshot of Delphic legal and social structures.
