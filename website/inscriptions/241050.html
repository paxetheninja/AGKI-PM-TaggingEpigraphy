This inscription is a manumission record from Delphi dating to the Roman Imperial period (after 66 AD). It follows the common Delphic formula of a 'sale to the god' (Apollo Pythios). Akoutos, son of Isidoros, sells two slaves, Drakon and Anasiphoros, to the god for 6 minae of silver. The act ensures their freedom (eleutheria) and protection from future claims of ownership. The document is dated by the archonship of Diodoros and includes a list of witnesses, including a member of the prominent Memmii family. The text mentions the recording of the act in the public archives and its inscription in the sanctuary/theater area.
