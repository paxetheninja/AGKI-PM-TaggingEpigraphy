This inscription is a Samian honorific decree dating to the late 4th century BC (306-301 BC). It honors Timophanes, son of Archepolis, a citizen of Phaselis, for his continued goodwill and services to the Samian people and individual citizens. The decree grants him proxeny, the title of benefactor, and Samian citizenship, including enrollment into the civic subdivisions (tribe, chiliastys, hekatostys). It also provides him with the right of access to the council and assembly, and the right of import/export (eisplous and ekplous) during both peace and war. The document concludes with instructions for the secretary of the council to oversee the inscription of the decree on a stone stele to be erected in the sanctuary of Hera (the Heraion).
