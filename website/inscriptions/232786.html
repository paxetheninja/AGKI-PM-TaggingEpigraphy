This inscription is a fragmentary Athenian decree dating to approximately 368 BC. It records a judicial treaty (symbolai) between Athens and the city of Stymphalos in Arcadia. The text outlines legal procedures for resolving disputes between citizens of the two states, mentioning the role of 'xenodikai' (judges for foreigners) and 'proxenoi'. It follows the standard administrative practice of being inscribed on a stone stele (stele lithine) for public display in Athens.
