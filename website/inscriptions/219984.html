This inscription, dated to 57 BC during the reign of Berenice IV, records a petition from the priests of the crocodile god Pnepheros in Theadelphia. The priests request the formal granting of 'asylia' (sanctuary status) for their temple and its surrounding precincts. The text defines the physical boundaries of this sacred space (mentioning the 'Boubasteion' to the north) and specifies that anyone violating the sanctuary shall be liable to the death penalty. The document concludes with an order to the strategos Dioskourides to ensure the grant is inscribed on a stele for public display. The text is heavily restored in parts, particularly the royal titles and the names of officials.
