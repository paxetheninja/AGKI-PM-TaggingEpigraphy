This inscription, IG IIÂ² 333, is a major Athenian law (nomos) dating to 335/4 BC during the archonship of Euainetos. It was proposed by the statesman Lykourgos of the deme Boutadai, a central figure in the financial and religious reorganization of Athens after the Battle of Chaeronea. The law concerns the 'kosmos' (sacred equipment and ornaments) used in religious processions, specifically the Panathenaia. It mandates the melting down of old, small, or damaged gold and silver dedications to create new, more impressive sacred vessels and ornaments for various deities, including Athena, Zeus Soter, and Artemis Brauronia. The text also mentions the 'dermatikon' (revenue from the sale of hides of sacrificed animals) as a source of funding and specifies penalties for officials who violate the regulations. It is a prime example of Lykourgan administrative reform aimed at restoring the splendor of Athenian cults.
