This inscription (IG IIÂ² 333) is a major Athenian law proposed by the statesman Lykourgos of Boutadai in 335/4 BC. It mandates the reorganization and manufacture of sacred ornaments (kosmos) for several major cults, including Athena, Zeus Soter, Amphiaraos, and Artemis Brauronia. The law specifies that old, small dedications should be melted down to create larger, more magnificent items, funded by sacred monies and the 'dermatikon' (revenue from animal hides). It reflects the religious and financial reforms of the Lykourgan era, aimed at restoring the splendor of Athenian festivals like the Panathenaia.
