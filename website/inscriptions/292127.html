AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, dating to the early 3rd century AD, represents a significant administrative and religious document from Roman Athens. It records a decree by the genos of the Kerykes (Heralds), one of the two families responsible for the Eleusinian Mysteries. The decree mandates the listing of its members, many of whom held the high office of Areopagite, on a stele to be erected in Eleusis. The names are meticulously organized by the traditional Athenian tribes (Erechtheis, Aegeis, Pandionis, Hadrianis, Kekropis), reflecting the continued importance of tribal identity and ancestral priesthoods in the Roman period.
