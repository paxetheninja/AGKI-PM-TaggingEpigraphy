This inscription, dated to the early 1st century AD (20-46 AD), is a manumission record from Delphi. It follows the traditional Delphic formula where a slave-owner (Dionysia) 'sells' slaves (Glykera and Agathe) to Apollo Pythios to grant them freedom. The text includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring the freedwomen to remain in service to Dionysia for the rest of her life. It also specifies financial penalties in denarii for breach of contract and names the archon Lamnos and various witnesses, including the priest of Apollo. The presence of denarii reflects the Roman economic influence in Phokis during this period.
