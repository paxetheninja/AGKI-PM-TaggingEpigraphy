AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Kos (ca. 100-50 BC) is a detailed regulation for the sale of the priesthood of Dionysos Thyllophoros. It was drafted by the prostatai and the secretary of the council. The text specifies that the priesthood is for life, outlines the payment schedule in two installments (in the months of Badromios and Gerastios), and defines the sacrificial perquisites (gere) the priestess is entitled to receive, such as legs, skins, and specific monetary amounts (triobol, drachma) depending on the type of animal sacrificed. It also mentions the public display of the stele in the sanctuary of the Twelve Gods.
