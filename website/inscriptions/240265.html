AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a proxeny decree from the city of Lilaia in Phokis, found at the sanctuary of Delphi. Dated to approximately 208 BC, it records the city's gratitude toward a garrison of soldiers and their commanders sent by King Attalos I of Pergamon to defend Lilaia during the First Macedonian War. The decree grants Thrasymachos, Athenodoros, and the listed soldiers the status of proxenoi and benefactors, along with rights of isopoliteia (equal citizenship), asylia (inviolability), and security in both war and peace. The text is a vital historical source for the military and diplomatic relations between the Attalid dynasty and the cities of Central Greece.
