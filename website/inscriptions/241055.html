AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a typical example of a Delphic manumission by 'sale to the god' (sacral manumission). Dated to the archonship of Kritolaos (c. 75-80 AD), it records Neikas selling a female slave named Sympherousa to Pythian Apollo for five silver minae. The document includes the standard legal protections for the manumitted person (aphon), the names of the guarantors, and the witnesses, including the priests of Apollo. The text mentions the deposition of the record in both the public archives and the temple of Apollo.
