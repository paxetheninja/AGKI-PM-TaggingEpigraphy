AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant posthumous honorific decree from Olbia for Theokles, son of Satyrus, dating to the late 2nd or early 3rd century AD. Theokles is praised for his extensive civic service, including holding the office of archon four times and serving as strategos and gymnasiarch. The text is remarkable for its long list of cities from the Black Sea and Asia Minor that also honored him with crowns, indicating his high status and perhaps his involvement in diplomatic or commercial networks. The decree mentions his death was caused by a 'malevolent spirit' (baskanos daimon) before he could complete his term. It orders the erection of an armored statue in the gymnasium and the inscription of the decree on a white stone stele to serve as an example of virtue for others.
