AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Salutaris Foundation' (104 AD), is one of the most significant documents from Roman Ephesus. It details a massive private endowment by the Roman knight Gaius Vibius Salutaris. The text outlines the donation of 29 gold and silver statues and 20,000 denarii. It establishes a complex system of financial distributions (dianomai) for the Boule, Gerousia, and other civic groups, and mandates a specific processional route for the statues through the city, linking the sanctuary of Artemis with the urban center and the theater. The text is reconstructed from multiple fragments found in the Great Theater.
