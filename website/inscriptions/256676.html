This inscription from Teos (Ionia) is a posthumous honorific decree for Pyrrhakos, son of Philodoxos. It details his extensive diplomatic career, specifically his multiple embassies to Rome and the Roman Senate, as well as to an unnamed king. He is credited with securing tax exemptions (aphorologia) for the city and contributing personal funds for the city's safety. The decree provides for a bronze statue, a golden crown, and the public proclamation of these honors at athletic and musical festivals. Pyrrhakos died while serving on an embassy, making these honors particularly significant as a form of public memorialization.
