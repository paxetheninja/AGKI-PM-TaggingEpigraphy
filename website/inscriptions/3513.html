AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IG II² 1299) is a significant honorific decree from the late 3rd century BC (post 236/5 BC). It was passed by the Athenian soldiers and foreign mercenaries stationed at the strategic frontier forts of Eleusis, Panakton, and Phyle. They honor the general Aristophanes of Leukonoe for his diligent command, his financial support of the troops, and his performance of sacrifices to Demeter, Kore, and other gods for the welfare of the Athenian people and the Macedonian royal family (King Demetrius II and Queen Phthia). The text provides a rare glimpse into the military administration and the presence of foreign mercenaries (Phocians, Macedonians, Cretans, etc.) in Attica during the Hellenistic period.
