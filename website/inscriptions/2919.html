This inscription is an Athenian honorific decree dating to the archonship of Thymochares (c. 257/6 BC). It honors the ephebes who served during the previous archonship of Telokles. The text emphasizes the ephebes' obedience (peitharchia) to the laws and their kosmetes. It follows the standard bureaucratic structure of the Athenian democracy, including the prytany, secretary, and the formal motion to praise and crown the ephebes with a foliage wreath (thallou stephanos). The latter part of the inscription lists the professional staff of the gymnasium, including the paidotribes (trainer) and konistes (attendant), which is characteristic of ephebic documents.
