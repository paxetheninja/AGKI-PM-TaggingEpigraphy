AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant Athenian ephebic decree from 116/5 BCE, during the archonship of Sarapion. It honors the kosmetes Demetrios of Alopeke for his exemplary leadership of the ephebes. The text provides a detailed account of the ephebes' religious duties, including sacrifices to Demeter, Kore, and the Mother of the Gods, and their educational activities, such as the donation of books to the library. The second half of the stele serves as a demographic record, listing the ephebes by their tribal affiliations (Erechtheis, Aegeis, etc.) and including a section for foreign ephebes (xenoi), reflecting the international character of the Athenian ephebeia in the late Hellenistic period.
