This inscription preserves a letter from the Emperor Augustus (6 BC) to the city of Knidos, though found on Astypalaea. It concerns a criminal case involving a home invasion. Augustus details how he commissioned Asinius Gallus to interrogate slaves under torture (basanoi), revealing that the defendants (Euboulos and Tryphera) were acting in self-defense against Philinos and another Euboulos who had besieged their house for three nights. Augustus exonerates the defendants, noting that the death of the aggressor occurred when a slave dropped a chamber pot (gastran) on him during the struggle. The letter serves as a rescript to be entered into the public records.
