This inscription is a significant portion of the Coan sacrificial calendar (LSCG 151B). It dates to the mid-4th century BC and outlines the cultic obligations for the month of Panamos. It specifically details the sacrifices to Zeus Machaneus and Zeus Polieus, involving the selection of oxen and heifers. It also mentions the Phyleomachidai, a local kinship group (genos), and their role in the distribution of sacrificial meat. The text provides insights into the intersection of civic administration and religious practice in Hellenistic Kos, particularly regarding the perquisites of the priest (hiereus) and the basileus.
