AI Analysis Summary

This is a significant Hellenistic decree from the city of Ilion (Troy) honoring King Antiochus (likely Antiochus I Soter). The text details the king's efforts to restore peace and order to the cities of Seleucis after a period of difficulty. In recognition of his military success and piety, the city decrees various honors, including public prayers, sacrifices to Athena Ilias and Apollo (the ancestor of the Seleucid dynasty), and the erection of a golden equestrian statue in the sanctuary of Athena. The decree also mentions the king's sister/queen and his children, emphasizing the dynastic nature of the honors.
