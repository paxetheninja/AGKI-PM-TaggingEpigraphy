AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Delphic manumission record dating to the late 2nd century BC. It documents the fictitious sale of a home-born slave named Lamia by her owner Nikas to the god Apollo Pythios for five silver minae. The contract includes a 'paramone' clause, requiring Lamia to remain in service to Nikas for the duration of the latter's life. The document specifies that Lamia shall be free and untouchable (anephaptos) after Nikas's death, and names Cleon as the legal guarantor (bebaioter) to protect her status against future claims of enslavement.
