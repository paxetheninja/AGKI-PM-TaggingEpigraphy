This inscription is a complete honorific decree from the deme of Halasarna on Kos, dating to the mid-2nd century BC. It honors the physician Onasandros, son of Onesimos, for his exceptional medical skill (iatrike techne) and his philanthropic dedication to the citizens. The text highlights that he studied under Antipatros, served the community without charging fees (misthos) even during difficult times, and maintained a high moral character. The decree concludes with the voting results (248 in favor, 0 against) and instructions for the napoiai (temple wardens) to engrave the decree on a stone stele and place it in the sanctuary of Apollo, next to the stele of his teacher Antipatros.
