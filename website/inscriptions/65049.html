AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Delos, dated to the archonship of Theaitetos (144/3 BC), records the gymnasiarchy of Leonides. It provides a detailed list of sacred officials (hieropoioi) and ephebes who participated in religious rites, specifically the carrying of the sacred basket (kanoun) for Heracles and Zeus. The list is notable for its inclusion of both Athenian citizens (identified by demes like Melite and Marathon) and foreigners (ethnics from Smyrna, Berytus, and even Roman names like Gaius and Spurius), illustrating the international character of Delian society in the mid-2nd century BC.
