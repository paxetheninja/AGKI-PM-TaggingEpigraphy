AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Naupaktos in West Lokris dates to the mid-2nd century BC and contains several distinct manumission records. These documents utilize the legal fiction of a 'sale to the god' Asklepios (referred to as 'Asklapios' in the text) to grant freedom to slaves. The text details the names of the sellers (Nikokrates, Meinokrates, Lykios), the slaves (Menis, Leukios, Simika, Alexandros), and the prices paid. It also includes 'paramone' clauses requiring the manumitted individuals to remain with their former owners for a specified period, and lists the official witnesses and guarantors (bebaioteres) required by law.
