AI Analysis Summary

This 2nd-century BC inscription from Tenos records a decree by the tribe of the Hyakinthioi. It honors Medeios and Sounides for their civic and religious contributions, including grain distributions to citizens and foreigners and the performance of sacrifices to Asklepios and Apollo. The text highlights a marriage alliance between the two families and grants hereditary rights within the tribe. It specifies the erection of two steles in major local sanctuaries (Asklepios and Poseidon/Amphitrite), funded by tribal revenues.
