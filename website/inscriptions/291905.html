This inscription (IG IIÂ² 1040 + 1025) is a significant Athenian decree from the late Hellenistic period (46/5 BC). It honors the ephebes of the year of the archon Polyxidos and their kosmetes, Sosistratos of Halai. The text provides a detailed account of the ephebic curriculum, including religious duties (sacrifices to Artemis Agrotera, participation in the Dionysia and Eleusinian Mysteries), military training (hoplite and cavalry exercises), and intellectual pursuits (attending lectures by philosophers and grammarians in the Ptolemaion). The decree orders the setting up of a painted image of the kosmetes in the Stoa of Attalos and the crowning of the ephebes with golden wreaths.
