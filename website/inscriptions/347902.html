AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal honorary decree of the Athenian state, dated to the archonship of Antimachos (256/5 BC). It records the honors bestowed upon a citizen of Pergamon who provided significant aid to Athens, specifically in the form of grain (krithon) and money (argyriou drachmas) for the city's defense (phylaken). The decree grants the honoree 'isoteleia' (tax equality) and 'enktesis' (the right to own property in Attica). The text follows the standard Athenian bureaucratic format, including the dating by archon and prytany, the names of the secretary and proposer, and the final instruction to set up the stele on the Akropolis at the expense of the administration.
