This inscription, likely from Rome and dated between 198 and 209 AD, is a dedication for the welfare of the Severan imperial family. It was set up by Lucius Septimius Tryphon, an Alexandrian philosopher and high priest of the Dionysiac synod. The text is a rich source for the history of professional guilds (synodoi) of performers, listing various 'paradoxoi' and 'periodoneikai' (victors in the great games) such as kitharodes, tragedians, and comedians. The erasure of Geta's name provides a terminus post quem for the modification of the stone following his damnatio memoriae in 211 AD.
