AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, identified as SEG 47.1667 and found at Kilisse-Tepe near Alexandreia Troas, dates to the transition between the 4th and 3rd centuries BC. It is a technical document detailing architectural specifications (syngraphai) for a building project. The text specifies measurements in cubits (pecheis) and palms (palaistoi), describes the leveling of foundations (themeliou) using a level (diabaten), the placement of orthostates, and the method of joining stones with dowels (gomphosei). Such documents are crucial for understanding Hellenistic engineering, construction management, and the legal framework of public works.
