AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree (dogma) issued by the Delphic Amphictyony around 48 BC. It honors Kallistos, son of Epigonos, from Knidos, for his distinguished conduct and diplomatic mediation between the Greek cities and the Roman 'autokrator' (likely Julius Caesar) and his legates. The decree grants Kallistos the titles of proxenos and euergetes, a gold crown, and a bronze statue to be erected in the sanctuary of Apollo at Delphi. It also awards him proedria at the Pythian Games, reflecting the intersection of political honors and religious festivals in the late Hellenistic/early Roman period.
