This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic sacral manumission dating to the late 2nd century BC. It follows the standard formula: dating by the local archon (Diodoros), the sale of the slave (Theodosios, a Galatian) to Apollo Pythios for a price of 3 minae, the declaration of freedom (eleutheria), and the inclusion of a paramone clause requiring the freedman to stay with the manumittors (Apollonios and Phila) as long as they live. It concludes with a list of witnesses, including priests of Apollo and members of the council (bouleutai).
