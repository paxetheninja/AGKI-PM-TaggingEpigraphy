AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic manumission by sale to Apollo Pythios. It dates to the mid-2nd century BC and follows the standard legal formula: dating by the local archon (Eucleos), naming the seller (Estia) and the slave (Lyso), stating the price (3 minae), and detailing the 'paramone' clause which requires the freedwoman to remain in service to her former owner until the latter's death. It also includes legal protections against re-enslavement and a comprehensive list of religious and civil witnesses.
