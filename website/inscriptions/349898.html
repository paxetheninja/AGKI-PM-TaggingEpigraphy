AI Analysis Summary

This 1st-century BC inscription from Kos is a detailed sacred law (diagraphÄ“) regulating the sale of the priesthood of Zeus Alseios. It was drafted by the board of prostatai and the council secretary. The text specifies the physical requirements for the priest (healthy and whole), his liturgical exemptions, and his ceremonial honors, such as wearing a purple chiton and a gold-leafed crown during festivals like the Dionysia and Alseia. It also mandates specific sacrifices to be performed by the monarchos, hieropoioi, and gymnasiarchos, providing the monetary value of the animals (bulls and sheep). The document concludes with administrative instructions for the poletai (sellers) and the requirement to display the law on a stone stele near the altar of Zeus Alseios.
