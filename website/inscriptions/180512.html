AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from the Amphiareion of Oropos, dating to the mid-2nd century BC, is a composite administrative document. It begins with a decree regulating the conduct of the hierarchai (sacred officials) and the publication of the record on a stone stele. The majority of the text consists of a detailed inventory of sacred vessels (mastoi, skyphoi, kyathoi, chutridia) dedicated to the god Amphiaraos. It records the names of donors, including a Milesian named Hellenikon, and the weights of the objects. Such inventories were typically produced during the reorganization of temple treasures or when items were being melted down (synchoneuthenta) to create new sacred equipment.
