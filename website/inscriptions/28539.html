This inscription is a detailed financial record (building account) from the sanctuary of Asclepius at Epidauros, dating to the early 3rd century BC. It documents the expenses incurred for various construction projects, including the 'skanomata' (temporary shelters or booths), the 'hippostasis' (stables), and the 'balaneion' (bathhouse). The text lists contractors (ergonai), the specific tasks performed (e.g., stone cutting, wood transport, encaustic painting), the agreed price, the deduction of the 'epidekaton' (a 10% tax or retention fee), and the final payment in drachmae and obols. It also mentions the 'hieromnemones' (sacred officials) overseeing the works and the 'boule' (council) involved in the administration.
