AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a long honorific decree from Gytheion dating to the early 1st century BC. It honors two Roman brothers, Nemerios and Marcus Cloatius, who were prominent 'negotiatores' (businessmen) in the region. The decree highlights their role as intermediaries between the city and Roman authorities, their financial generosity in providing and restructuring loans during a period of economic crisis, and their hospitality toward Roman officials and Athenian judges. It provides crucial evidence for the integration of Roman elites into the social and political fabric of Greek cities in the late Hellenistic period. The text specifies that the decree be inscribed on a stone stele and placed in the sanctuary of Apollo.
