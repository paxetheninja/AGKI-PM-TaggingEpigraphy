AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of the inventories of the Brauronion on the Athenian Acropolis, dating to approximately 341/0 BC. It records the transfer of sacred treasures (jewelry like rings and various textiles like chitons and tarantina) between boards of overseers (epistatai). The text is significant for its prosopographical data, listing numerous Athenian women and their male relatives as dedicators of specific garments and gold items.
