🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant building contract from Delos, dated to the mid-4th century BC. It records the 'syngraphai' (specifications) for the quarrying, transport, and finishing of stone blocks for a public building, likely a temple. The text is highly technical, listing precise measurements in feet (podes), palms (palaistai), and fingers (daktyloi). It names the contractor, Conon of Thespiae, and several Athenian guarantors from various demes, reflecting the international nature of labor and finance in the Delian sanctuary during this period. The legal clauses include oversight by the 'naopoioi' (temple commissioners) and the 'architekton' (architect), as well as penalties for non-compliance with the timeline.
