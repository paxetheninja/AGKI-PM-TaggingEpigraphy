This inscription, dated to the second half of the 4th century BC in Attica, represents a complex building account and set of specifications (syngraphai) for works in a sanctuary, likely that of Zeus Soter in Piraeus. The text is written in the stoichedon style and details the financial administration of public works, including the transport of stone (likely Karystian marble, given the contractor Archestratos is from Karystos), the construction of a propylon, and the installation of architectural features like the geison and krepis. It mentions various officials (epistatai, grammateus) and contractors (misthotai), providing a window into the economic and legal procedures of Athenian public building projects.
