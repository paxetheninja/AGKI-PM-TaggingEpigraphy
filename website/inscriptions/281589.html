AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a characteristic communal record from the Ormeleis region in Pisidia. It lists various individuals who 'honored the ochlos' (the local community or association). The honors often involved 'crowning' and the provision of specific quantities of oil (measured in sextarii) or other goods. The text is highly formulaic, repeating the names of donors and their fathers, many of which are typical for the Phrygian-Pisidian borderlands, such as Menis and Attalus.
