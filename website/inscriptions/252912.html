AI Analysis Summary

This is a significant honorific decree from the city of Priene, dating to the late Hellenistic or early Roman period (1st century BC/AD). It honors Aulus Amelius Sextus Zosimus for his exemplary service as gymnasiarch and secretary. The text provides detailed insights into the duties of a gymnasiarch, including the provision of oil (aleimma), the organization of contests (skillomachia, boxing), and the maintenance of the gymnasium facilities. It also mentions his role in religious festivals like the Panathenaia and his dedication of statues of Hermes. The decree specifies the honors granted: a golden crown and four types of statues (painted, bronze, gold, and marble) to be placed in the most prominent part of the city.
