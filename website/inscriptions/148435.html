AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an honorary decree from the city of Larisa in Thessaly, dating to the late 2nd century BC. It honors an individual named Hegesipolis, likely a citizen of Skotoussa, for his consistent goodwill (eunoia) and services to the people of Larisa. The decree grants him several high-status privileges: the title of proxenos (diplomatic representative), the right to own property (enktesis), and tax equality (isoteleia). It also mandates the crowning of the individual with an olive wreath for his virtue (arete). The text highlights the diplomatic relationship between Larisa and Skotoussa, describing them as 'kinsmen and friends' (syngene kai oikeion kai philon), and orders the dispatch of ambassadors to Skotoussa to formally announce these honors.
