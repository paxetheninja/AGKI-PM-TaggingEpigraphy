🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from the deme of Isthmos on Kos (late 3rd/early 2nd century BC) is a significant administrative document. It records the registration of citizens eligible for the 'monarchy' (the eponymous priesthood of Apollo) and naturalized citizens (damopoietoi). The text is highly characteristic of Koan epigraphy, utilizing a specific filiation formula that includes the father's name, the mother's name, and the maternal grandfather's name. This detailed genealogical recording was likely linked to verifying tribal or cultic eligibility. The mention of the damarchos Damoxenos provides a chronological anchor for the decree that mandated this registration.
