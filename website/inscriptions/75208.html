🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription (IG XII, 2 526) is a dossier of documents from Eresos, Lesbos, dating to the early Hellenistic period. It records the legal proceedings against the former tyrants Agonippos and Eurysilaos, who had seized power with Persian support. The text includes a decree of the Eresian assembly, a letter from King Antigonos I Monophthalmus, and references to the 'diagramma' (edict) of Alexander the Great. It details the tyrants' crimes, such as the destruction of altars and the extortion of money, and the subsequent judicial decisions regarding their execution and the banishment of their descendants. The inscription is a vital source for the study of Hellenistic kingship, the restoration of democracy in Greek cities, and the legal treatment of political enemies.
