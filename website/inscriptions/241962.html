AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, found at Delphi but concerning the city of Tegea, is a copy of the famous 'Exiles Decree' (diagramma) issued by Alexander the Great in 324 BC. It provides detailed instructions on how the city should handle the return of political exiles, specifically focusing on the restitution of paternal and maternal properties (patroia and matroia). It establishes a 'foreign court' (xenikon dikasterion) to adjudicate disputes and mandates a civic oath of reconciliation (amnestia) to prevent future civil strife (mnesikakia). The text is a crucial witness to the political intervention of Macedon in the internal affairs of Greek poleis during the late 4th century BC.
