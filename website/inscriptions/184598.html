AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Chersonesos is a fragmentary administrative and financial record, likely part of the 'Register of Sales'. It documents sales (prasin) and financial totals (kephalaia) that were carried out under the authority of a decree (psephisma). The text lists numerous citizens and officials, including a prytanis and elected supervisors (epimeletai), who were responsible for overseeing these transactions. The repetitive structure of names followed by patronymics is characteristic of official lists or registers of property sales in the Hellenistic period, specifically involving vineyards and underground cellars (katoruges).
