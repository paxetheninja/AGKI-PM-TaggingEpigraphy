AI Analysis Summary

This inscription contains two related decrees from the city of Eresos on the island of Lesbos. The first decree (A) concerns the arrival and maintenance of foreign judges from Miletos, sent to resolve legal disputes according to a treaty. The second decree (B) is an honorific resolution passed after the judges completed their work. It praises the Milesian people and the specific judges, Exochos and Antigonos, for their impartiality and justice. They are awarded golden crowns and citizenship in Eresos. The text provides significant insight into the Hellenistic practice of 'foreign judges' (dikastai xenikoi) used to settle internal civic strife or backlogged litigation through inter-city cooperation.
