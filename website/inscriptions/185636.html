AI Analysis Summary

This 4th-century BC inscription from Kos is a major sacred law (lex sacra) regulating the public priesthoods (hierosynai). It details the ritual requirements for sacrifices to Zeus Polieus, Athena Polias, and the Twelve Gods. The text is particularly valuable for its description of 'gera' (honorary portions of meat) distributed to various civic and religious actors, including the monarchos, heralds, and even craftsmen like potters. It also establishes strict purity rules for the priest, including prohibitions on contact with death or childbirth, and sets heavy fines (1000 drachmae) for officials who fail to enforce these regulations. The mention of the Nestoridai suggests the involvement of specific kinship groups in the sacrificial process.
