This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic manumission record dated to approximately 130 BC (Archonship of Ariston). It utilizes the 'sale to Apollo' formula, a common legal mechanism in Delphi where a slave (Herais) is sold to the god to achieve the status of 'eleuthera' (free) and 'anephaptos' (untouchable/protected from seizure). The text is highly structured, beginning with a dual dating formula (Delphic and Ambrossian archons), followed by the sale terms, the price of 5 minae, and a detailed list of witnesses including priests of Apollo and citizens from both involved cities. The presence of a 'bebaioter' (guarantor) and penalties for anyone attempting to re-enslave Herais underscores the document's legal rigor.
