This inscription is a 'paramone' manumission from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Kleodamos (c. 188 BC). Kallikrateia, daughter of Aristagoras, sells her slave Sostrata to Apollo Pythios for three silver minae. The sale guarantees Sostrata's freedom relative to the outside world, but she is contractually obligated to remain ('paramone') with Kallikrateia for the duration of Kallikrateia's life, doing what is ordered of her. The text lists guarantors (Aristagoras and Euthydamos) and witnesses (priests and private citizens).
