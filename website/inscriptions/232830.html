This inscription, dated to c. 337/6 BC during the archonship of Phrynichos, is a law passed by the Athenian nomothetai. It regulates the financing and organization of the 'Little Panathenaia'. The law establishes that revenue from the 'Nea' (newly acquired land, likely in Oropos) should be used to fund the festival. It details the duties of the hieropoioi (sacrificial officials), the poletai (public sellers/leasers), and the distribution of sacrificial meat (kreanomia) to various magistrates and the general citizenry in the Kerameikos. The proposer, Aristonikos of Marathon, was a prominent politician of the Lycurgan era, a period noted for its focus on religious and financial reform.
