AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a decree from the city of Spalauthra in Magnesia, dating to the late 2nd century BC (ca. 130-126 BC). It honors Lysias, son of Epitelos, a citizen of Demetrias who served as the general (strategos) of the Magnesian League. The text praises Lysias for his 'kalokagathia' (noble character), his benevolence toward the people of Spalauthra, and his helpfulness to individual citizens. The decree mandates that he be crowned with a foliage wreath, that the resolution be inscribed on a stone stele and placed in the agora near the sanctuary of Artemis Soteira, and that envoys be sent to deliver a copy of the decree to him. It also mentions administrative details such as the treasurer Attalos providing funds for the stele's construction.
