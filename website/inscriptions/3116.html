AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Athenian honorific decree from the early 2nd century BC (c. 186/5 BC). It records two related decrees: the first honors Zopyros of Melite for his daughter Timothea's service as a kanephoros (basket-bearer) in a religious procession; the second honors the epimeletai (supervisors) of the same procession. The text provides significant prosopographical data for the Athenian elite and details regarding the administration of religious festivals, specifically the Dionysia, as indicated by the location of the stele in the sanctuary of Dionysos. The mention of the military treasurer (tamias ton stratiotikon) for funding the inscription is typical for this period.
