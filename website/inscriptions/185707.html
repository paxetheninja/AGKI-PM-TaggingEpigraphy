AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a 3rd-century BC honorific decree found at the Asklepieion of Kos. It records the honors bestowed upon Hermias, son of Emmenidas, a physician from Halikarnassos. The text highlights his professional excellence and his willingness to treat both citizens and visitors, including those without means, free of charge. The decree specifies that he be awarded a crown, to be announced during the Dionysia and Asklepieia festivals, and that the resolution be inscribed on a stone stele in the sanctuary of Apollo. It also mentions the diplomatic interaction between Kos and Halikarnassos, involving the envoy Samides, son of Menekrates.
