This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Kyzikos dating to the early 1st century AD. It was issued jointly by the local Greek demos and the resident Roman business community (conventus civium Romanorum). The text honors Apollonis, daughter of Prokles, for her exceptional virtue (sophrosyne), her devotion to her husband (philandria), and her religious service as a priestess of Artemis. The decree outlines elaborate posthumous honors, including a city-wide period of mourning, a public funeral procession involving all citizens (men, women, ephebes, and maidens), and the erection of multiple statues in prominent civic locations like the Tetragonos Agora and the Charitesion. It provides valuable insight into the social status of elite women in Roman Asia Minor and the administrative cooperation between Greeks and Romans in Kyzikos.
