This inscription is an honorific decree from the city of Megara dating to the late 2nd or early 1st century BC. It honors a local citizen (whose name is partially lost, ending in -ron) for his 'philothimia' (ambition/generosity) and 'kalokagathia' (nobility). The decree mentions the approval of the Roman Senate and the Roman praetor Marcus Calidius (likely M. Calidius, praetor in 77 BC), reflecting the geopolitical reality of Roman hegemony in Greece during this period. The honors include a golden crown and the erection of a statue (eikon) in a prominent place in the city. The decree was to be announced at major Panhellenic games (Pythian, Isthmian, Nemean) and the local Asclepieia, serving as an incentive for other citizens to act beneficially toward the demos.
