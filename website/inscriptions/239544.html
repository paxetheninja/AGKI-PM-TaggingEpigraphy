This inscription is a manumission record from Delphi, dated to the archonship of Kallidemos (c. 124 BC). It follows the standard Delphic formula of a 'sale to the god' (sacral manumission). Agathon, son of Euarchos, sells a home-born slave (oikogenes) named Sosixenos to Apollo Pythios for four silver minae. The god ensures the slave's status as 'eleutheros' (free) and 'anephaptos' (inviolable). The text includes a 'bebaioter' (guarantor), Dexandas, and a list of witnesses including the priests of Apollo and city archons, providing a rich snapshot of Delphic legal and religious administration in the late 2nd century BC.
