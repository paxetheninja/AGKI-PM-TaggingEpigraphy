AI Analysis Summary

This inscription represents the financial accounts of the Athenian Amphictyons on Delos between 372 and 367 BC. It records the administration of the sanctuary of Apollo, specifically focusing on the collection of interest from loans made to various Greek cities and private individuals. It also details expenditures for religious festivals, including the purchase of a crown and tripods, and mentions the transport of sacred offerings from the Hyperboreans. The text is a crucial source for understanding the economic and administrative control Athens exercised over the Delian sanctuary during the Second Athenian League.
