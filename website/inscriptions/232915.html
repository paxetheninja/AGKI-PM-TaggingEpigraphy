AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragmentary Athenian decree dating to 303 BC, concerning a treaty and alliance between Athens and Sicyon. It follows the standard administrative procedures of the period, including the swearing of oaths, the dispatch of envoys, and the awarding of honors (golden crowns). The text also contains the typical publication clause, directing the treasurer to pay for the inscription on a stone stele from the fund for decrees. The mention of 'The Saviors' (Soteres) refers to the Antigonid kings, Antigonus and Demetrius, who were worshipped in Athens at this time.
