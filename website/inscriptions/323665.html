AI Analysis Summary

This inscription represents a diplomatic dossier from the Hellenistic period, likely the 2nd century BCE. It consists of a cover letter from the city of Lampsacus to Thasos, followed by two decrees passed in the months of Areion and Apatourion. The decrees honor Dionysodorus, son of Pempides, a Thasian, for his assistance to Lampsacene citizens. He is granted the title of proxenos and euergetes, along with rights of asylia (inviolability), the right to enter and leave the harbor during peace and war, and the privilege of priority access to the council and assembly (proedria/ephodos). The text is a prime example of inter-city relations and the administrative procedures of the Lampsacene boule and demos.
