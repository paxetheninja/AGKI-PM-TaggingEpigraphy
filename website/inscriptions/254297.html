🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant inventory of the Heraion of Samos dating to the period of the Athenian cleruchy (c. 322/1 BC). It records the transfer of sacred property ('kosmos tes theou') from the outgoing treasurers under the archonship of Theokles to the incoming ones under Peisileos. The text provides a rich catalogue of 4th-century BC textiles (Lydian chitons, mitrai, sindones) and temple furniture (diphroi, lebetes). The presence of Athenian demotics (e.g., Phrearrhios, Thorikios) confirms the Athenian administration of the island during this era.
