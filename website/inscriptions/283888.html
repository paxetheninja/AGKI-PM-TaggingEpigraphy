This inscription is a significant Hellenistic decree from Telmessos dated to 240 BC, during the reign of Ptolemy III Euergetes. It honors Ptolemy, son of Lysimachus (a local dynast and relative of the Ptolemaic house), for his administrative and economic relief provided to the city after a period of war. The text details extensive tax exemptions on various agricultural products (grain, pulses, sesame) and mandates the establishment of a cult for Zeus Soter, including an annual sacrifice and a procession. The decree was to be set up in the sanctuary of Artemis.
