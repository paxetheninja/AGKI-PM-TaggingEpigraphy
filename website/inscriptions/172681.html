AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major historical document from Histria (Istros) dating to the late 3rd or early 2nd century BC. It honors Agathokles, son of Antiphilos, for his repeated diplomatic and military interventions. He served as a commander of archers (toxarches) and a general with full powers (strategos autokrator), and undertook multiple embassies to Thracian and Scythian leaders. He negotiated with the Thracian chieftain Zoltes and the king Rhemaxos to protect the city from raids, secure the harvest, and recover stolen livestock. The text provides crucial evidence for the political instability in the Dobruja region and the complex relationships between Greek cities and local 'barbarian' rulers.
