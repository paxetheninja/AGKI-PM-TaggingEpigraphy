AI Analysis Summary

This is a typical Delphic manumission record from the late 2nd century BC. It documents the sale of a female slave of Syrian origin, Zopyra, to Apollo Pythios by her owner Eunous. The text follows the standard formula: dating by the local archon and council, the terms of the sale (four silver minae and five staters), the guarantee of freedom (eleuthera), and a list of witnesses including religious officials like the neokoros and priests.
