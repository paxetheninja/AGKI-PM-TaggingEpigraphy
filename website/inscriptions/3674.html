AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragment of an Athenian treasure inventory from 340/39 BC. It documents the transfer of sacred property from the outgoing treasurers of Athena to the incoming board. The text describes various luxury items, including gilded wood, silver and gold rings, seals, and shields, and mentions specific locations within the Hekatompedon (part of the Parthenon) where these items were kept. It also references small statues or representations of Dionysus and Apollo.
