This inscription is a public decree from the island of Kos, dating to the early 2nd century BC. It records a substantial donation by Phanomachos, a Thessalian resident, who dedicated land and houses to Zeus and the Demos of Kos. The text is highly significant for understanding civic and religious life, as it details the boundaries of the donated property (naming several local neighbors), the organization of a religious procession (pompe) involving priests, magistrates (prostatai), ephebes, and children, and the performance of hymns. It also provides administrative instructions for the construction of the sanctuary and the publication of the decree on a white marble stele to be placed near the altar.
