AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the Foundation of Gaius Vibius Salutaris (104 AD), is one of the most significant epigraphic finds from Ephesus. It records a massive donation by a Roman equestrian, including 29 statues (representing the Emperor, the Senate, the Equestrian order, and the tribes of Ephesus) and a financial endowment. The text regulates the 'diataxis' (arrangement) of a ritual procession where these statues were carried from the Temple of Artemis through the Magnesian Gate to the Theater and back. It reflects the intersection of Roman imperial cult, local civic identity, and the religious primacy of Artemis Ephesia.
