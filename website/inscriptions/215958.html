AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal petition addressed to Queen Berenice IV (57 BC) by the priests of the crocodile god Pnepheros in Theadelphia. The priests request the confirmation of the right of asylum (asylia) for their temple and its surrounding grounds. The text includes a cover letter from the local epistates and instructions to the strategos Dioscorides to ensure the boundaries are marked and the decree is engraved on a stele. It highlights the intersection of royal authority, local administration, and temple privileges in late Ptolemaic Egypt.
