This inscription, dating to the late 3rd or early 2nd century BC from the region of Halicarnassus, records the foundation of a private family cult by Poseidonios, son of Atrokles. The document is a complex mix of a private legal act (foundation/hypotheke), an oracular response (chresmos) from Apollo of Telmessos, and a sacred law (lex sacra). It prescribes the ritual honors for Apollo Telmessos, the Mother of the Gods, and the Agathos Daimon of the founder's parents. It also establishes the financial and administrative framework for the cult's maintenance, including the management of a garden and the distribution of sacrificial meat among family members and participants.
