This inscription from Kyzikos (Mysia) dates to the Hadrianic period (117-138 AD). It serves as a detailed administrative and religious record of the city's prytaneis. The text is organized chronologically by the months of the Kyzikene calendar (Apatureon, Poseideon, Lenaion, Anthesterion) and genealogically/politically by the four traditional Ionian tribes (Geleontes, Argadeis, Bores, and Aigikoreis). The mention of 'mystai' (initiates) among the officials suggests that these civic roles were integrated into the local mystery cults, likely those of Dionysos or Apollo, which were prominent in Kyzikos. The names reflect the high degree of Romanization in the local elite, with many individuals bearing Roman tria nomina (e.g., Ulpius, Claudius, Flavius).
