AI Analysis Summary

The Tabulae Heracleenses (IG XIV 645) are two famous bronze tablets from Herakleia in Lucania. They contain detailed regulations for the survey, boundary marking, and leasing of sacred lands (temenos) belonging to Dionysos and Athena Polias. The text is a primary source for ancient Greek land management, agricultural requirements (such as the planting of vines and olives), and the Doric dialect. It records the recovery of sacred lands from private encroachment and their subsequent division into lots for lease, with rents paid in grain to the city's granaries. The tablets mention various local officials, including Ephors and Polianomoi, and provide precise measurements in schoinos and oregmata.
