This inscription is a significant honorific decree from the deme of Rhamnous, dated to the archonship of Kephisophon (235/234 BC). It honors Dikaiarchos of Thria, a military official who served under the Macedonian kings Antigonos II Gonatas and Demetrios II. The text details his career: guarding the fortress at Rhamnous, serving at Eleusis and Panakton, and his diplomatic intervention in Eretria to save Athenian citizens from execution. It also highlights his piety in funding the Nemesia festival sacrifices during wartime. The decree provides crucial evidence for the Macedonian presence in Attica and the administrative life of the frontier demes.
