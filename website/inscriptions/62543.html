This inscription (IDelos 104-12) records the administrative and financial activities of the Athenian Amphictyons on Delos for the year 353/2-352/1 BC. It is divided into two primary sections: a record of leases (misthoseis) for sacred properties such as temene, houses (oikiai), and gardens on Delos and Syros, and a meticulous inventory of the treasures held in the Temple of the Athenians. The inventory lists numerous votive offerings, including gold crowns, silver phialai, and bronze kraters, often specifying the weight and the donor's name. The presence of Athenian donors and items from as far as Sicily (Tauromenion) reflects the pan-Hellenic importance of the sanctuary under Athenian control during the mid-4th century BC.
