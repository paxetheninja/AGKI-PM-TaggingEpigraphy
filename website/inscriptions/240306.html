AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a decree of the Delphic Amphictyonic Council dating to the archonship of Philaitolos (c. 200 BC). It records the recognition of the city of Antioch (Alabanda in Caria) and its territory as sacred and inviolable (asylos). The decree honors the city, King Antiochus III (the Great), and the ambassador Pausimachos, son of Atrokles. It specifies that the decree should be inscribed on the base of a bronze statue of King Antiochus located in the sanctuary of Pythian Apollo at Delphi. The text includes a long list of hieromnemones (representatives) from various Greek regions including Aetolia, Argos, Thebes, and Chios.
