AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant Hellenistic decree from Miletus, dated to approximately 300/299 BC. It records the honors bestowed upon Antiochus (the future Antiochus I Soter), son of Seleucus I Nicator, in recognition of his promise to build a large stoa at the sanctuary of Apollo in Didyma. The text is a classic example of the relationship between Hellenistic monarchs and Greek cities, involving reciprocal benefaction (euergetism) and religious piety. It mentions specific civic offices like treasurers (tamiai) and prytaneis, and religious festivals like the Dionysia and Didymeia.
