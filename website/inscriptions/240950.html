This inscription is a standard Delphic manumission record dating to the Roman period (20-46 AD), evidenced by the use of 'denarii' as currency. It documents the fictitious sale of the slave Kalliteime to Pythian Apollo by Euphrosynos and Soteris. The document specifies the price of 200 denarii and guarantees her status as 'eleuthera' (free) and 'anepaphos' (untouchable/inviolable). The text mentions the archonship of Laidas and the involvement of the city secretary, Leon, ensuring the document was deposited in the public archives and inscribed on the sanctuary walls as per local law.
