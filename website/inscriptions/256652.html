AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a version of the 'Teian Curses' (Dirae Teiae) found at Abdera, a colony of Teos. The text dates to the early 5th century BC and records public imprecations pronounced by the city's magistrates (timouchoi) against various offenses, including poisoning (pharmaka), hindering the grain supply, and political subversion. It is famous for its reference to 'Phoenician letters' (phoinikographia), which likely refers to the alphabet or the act of writing. The text mentions several festivals (Anthesteria, Herakleia) and officials like the aisymnetes and tamiai.
