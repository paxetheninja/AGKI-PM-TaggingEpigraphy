AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Kremna is a significant example of a public dice oracle (astragalomanteia) dating to the reign of Hadrian. It consists of a series of 56 possible dice combinations (though only a portion is preserved here). Each entry begins with the dice values (1=Chios, 3=Trias, 4=Tetras, 6=Stes/Hexas), the total sum, the name of a presiding deity, and a hexameter or prose response. These oracles were popular in Southern Asia Minor (Pisidia, Lycia, Pamphylia) and served as a form of 'divine lottery' for citizens seeking guidance on travel, business, health, and marriage. The text is fragmentary, with many sections reconstructed based on parallel dice oracles found in other cities like Termessos or Adada.
