AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Hellenistic honorific decree from the island of Siphnos, dating to approximately 274-270 BC. It honors Perigenes, son of Leontiskos, an Alexandrian, for his role as a diplomatic intermediary who brought favorable news regarding the health and goodwill of King Ptolemy II Philadelphus and Queen Arsinoe II. The decree grants Perigenes a gold crown worth 2000 drachmas, the status of proxenos (public guest/representative), and various civic privileges including proedria (front-row seating at festivals) and asylia (inviolability). The text specifies that the decree be set up in the sanctuary of Dionysos, highlighting the close ties between the Cycladic islands and the Ptolemaic dynasty during this period.
