This inscription, found on Failaka Island (ancient Ikaros) in the Persian Gulf, is a critical document of Seleucid administration under Antiochus III. It consists of a cover letter from Anaxarchos to the islanders and a primary letter from Ikadion. The text details the king's interest in the island, specifically the relocation of the sanctuary of Artemis (Soteira), the establishment of gymnastic and musical competitions, and the regulation of land tenure and tax exemptions for settlers. It also includes an economic directive prohibiting the export of grain to ensure local stability. The inscription reflects the Hellenistic practice of 'synoikismos' and the integration of remote territories into the royal administrative and religious framework.
