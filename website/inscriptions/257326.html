AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Aphrodisias records the establishment of a family tomb by Aurelius Eumachus. It includes detailed legal provisions regarding burial rights for his wife Tatia, children Pelles and Eumachia, and other relatives. The text is notable for its strict prohibitions against unauthorized burials or the sale of the monument, backed by a significant fine of 10,000 denarii payable to the Roman treasury. The document concludes with a dating formula and a note that a copy was officially registered in the city's archives.
