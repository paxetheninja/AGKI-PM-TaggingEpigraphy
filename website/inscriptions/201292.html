This inscription (IC III iii 3,B) represents a significant diplomatic document from Hellenistic Crete, specifically a treaty of symmachy (alliance) between Hierapytna and Lyttos. Dated shortly after 205 BC, it reflects the complex inter-city politics of the island. The text outlines the duties of the 'kosmoi' (magistrates), the division of war booty, and the requirement for both cities to agree on peace or war. It concludes with a comprehensive religious oath invoking a long list of deities, including Zeus Ortrios, Athena Aleria, and Apollo Pythios, which is typical for Cretan international agreements. The mention of Gortyn as a location for a common stele suggests its role as a regional mediator or a central religious hub.
