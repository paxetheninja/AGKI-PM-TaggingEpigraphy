AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a decree from the city of Kaunos in Caria, dating to the 2nd century BC. It honors a panel of foreign judges (Antigenes, Aeneas, and Timouchos) and their secretary (Pythion) sent from the city of Smyrna. The judges are praised for their fairness and for restoring concord (homonoia) among the citizens. The decree specifies that they are to be awarded golden crowns, which are to be announced during the festival of the Dionysia and at musical contests. The text also includes instructions for the decree to be inscribed on a stone stele and placed in a conspicuous location, and for the ambassador from Smyrna to be invited to hospitality (xenia) at the Prytaneion.
