AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of the Delian temple accounts from the period of independence. It records the financial administration of the sanctuary of Apollo by the hieropoioi. The text specifically details the transfer of funds between successive boards of officials (under archons Soteles and Charilaos), payments for public services such as flute players (auletai), and significant loans (daneismata) granted by the sanctuary to the city (polis), the council (boule), and various magistrates. The mention of 'syngraphai' (contracts) held by private individuals like Pythokles indicates the legal framework of these public-private financial interactions.
