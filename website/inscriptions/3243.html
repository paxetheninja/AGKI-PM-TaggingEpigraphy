AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian honorific decree dating to the late 2nd century BC. It honors Sosandros, son of Philistos, from the deme of Sypalettos, for his exemplary service, piety, and financial contributions (liturgies) towards the Athenian state, specifically regarding the decoration and maintenance of gymnasia and temples. The decree mandates that he be awarded a golden crown and that the text be inscribed on a stone stele to be placed on the Acropolis near the temple of Athena. It also includes administrative instructions for the recording of the associated costs.
