This inscription is a series of manumission acts from Pythoion in Perrhaibia (Thessaly), dating to the late 1st century BC. It follows the standard Thessalian legal format where the act is dated by the federal strategos (Leon and Diotimos) and the local month. The text records several individuals (both men and women) freeing their slaves, often with the condition of 'paramone' (remaining with the master for a specified time). The presence of local officials like 'tagoi' and 'xenodokoi' (judges for foreigners) at the end of the text highlights the civic and legal oversight of these private acts.
