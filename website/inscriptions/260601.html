AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major record of international arbitration in the 2nd century BC. The city of Magnesia on the Maeander was chosen to arbitrate a long-standing territorial dispute between the Cretan cities of Itanos and Hierapytna over the island of Leuke and the sanctuary of Zeus Dictaeus. The text is highly complex, citing Roman senatorial decrees, royal correspondence from the Ptolemies, and ancient sacred laws to establish historical ownership and boundary lines. The document serves as a legal dossier, preserving multiple layers of diplomatic and religious history.
