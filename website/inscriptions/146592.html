AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of a religious subscription list (epidosis) from the Roman Imperial period in Lebadeia. It documents private contributions toward the 'sekos' (enclosure or precinct) of the oracle of Zeus Trophonios. The text lists names of citizens, often with patronymics, and the specific amount of drachmas they provided. The presence of Roman names like Licinnius and Markianos alongside traditional Greek names reflects the social integration of the 1st and 2nd centuries AD. The inscription is fragmentary, with several names and numerical values lost or partially restored.
