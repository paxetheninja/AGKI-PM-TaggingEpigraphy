AI Analysis Summary

The Rabatak inscription is a seminal Bactrian text written in the Greek script during the reign of Kanishka I. It provides a definitive genealogy of the early Kushan kings, linking Kanishka to his father Vima Kadphises, grandfather Vima Taktu, and great-grandfather Kujula Kadphises. The inscription commemorates the foundation of a 'bagolango' (temple) and the installation of statues of these kings alongside Iranian and local deities. Crucially, it mentions the expansion of Kushan power into the heart of northern India (mentioning Pataliputra and Kaushambi) and records Kanishka's decision to replace Greek with the 'Aryan' (Bactrian) language for official records.
