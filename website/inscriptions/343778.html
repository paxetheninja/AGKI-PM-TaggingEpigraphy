AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant historical decree from Samos, dated shortly after 322 BC, following the return of the Samian exiles. The decree honors Gorgos and Minnion, sons of Theodotos of Iasos, who were influential figures at the court of Alexander the Great. The text details how Gorgos used his proximity to Alexander to advocate for the restoration of Samos to its original inhabitants. It records Alexander's announcement in his camp regarding the return of the island. In gratitude, the Samian people granted Gorgos and Minnion citizenship, enrollment in the civic subdivisions (tribes, chiliastyes, and clans), and ordered the decree to be set up in the sanctuary of Hera. The mention of Alexander's camp and the specific political context of the 'Samian restoration' makes this a key document for Hellenistic history.
