AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a fragmentary decree from the island of Samos, dating to the Augustan period (late 1st century BC to early 1st century AD). It documents the city's diplomatic engagement with the Roman Empire, specifically recording the dispatch of an embassy to Augustus. The text mentions key figures of the Julio-Claudian era, including Gaius Caesar and Marcus Agrippa. It outlines religious honors, such as sacrifices to Jupiter Capitolinus, and administrative instructions for the publication of the decree on marble stelae, one of which was to be set up in the sanctuary of Hera (the Heraion). The document concludes with a list of the ambassadors and temple wardens (neopoioi) involved, providing valuable prosopographical data for Samos during the early Principate.
