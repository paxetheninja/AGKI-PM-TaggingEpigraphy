This inscription contains the regulations for the 'Italika kai Romaia Sebasta Olympia' (the Sebasta games of Naples), which were established in 2 BC to honor Augustus. Although found at Olympia, the text explicitly mentions the Neapolitans and the requirement for athletes to register in Naples 30 days before the festival. The text is a crucial source for understanding the organization of 'Isolympic' games in the Roman period, detailing age categories (boys from 17 to 20, then men), financial support for participants, and a comprehensive list of events including the stadion, pentathlon, boxing, and various musical and dramatic competitions (kitharists, actors, etc.). It also mentions the role of the agonothetai and the use of specific crowns (wheat/grain) as prizes.
