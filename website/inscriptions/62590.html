This inscription is a classic example of a Delian temple inventory and administrative record from the period of Delian independence. It combines three distinct administrative functions: recording the victors of the Dionysiac festivals (agonistic), cataloging the sacred treasures of the temple (inventory), and documenting the legal transfer of responsibility between outgoing and incoming magistrates (archons). The mention of King Philip V of Macedon's dedication provides a crucial chronological and political anchor, reflecting the sanctuary's importance to Hellenistic monarchs. The text is highly technical, using specific terminology for vessel types and weights.
