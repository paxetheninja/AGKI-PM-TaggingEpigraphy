AI Analysis Summary

This inscription preserves a letter from the Roman dictator Lucius Cornelius Sulla to the city of Kos, dated to approximately 81 BC. Sulla confirms the traditional privileges and immunities (such as exemption from taxes and military service) of the 'Technitai of Dionysus' (the guild of actors and musicians) of the Ionian and Hellespontine branch. The confirmation follows an embassy led by Alexander of Laodicea, a kitharist. The document reflects the Roman administration's continued support for Greek cultural institutions and the use of local cities as repositories for official decrees.
