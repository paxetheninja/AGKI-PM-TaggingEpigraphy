This inscription is a significant example of a 3rd-century AD lead curse tablet from Amathous, Cyprus. It belongs to a known corpus of 'silencing' spells (phimotika). The petitioner, a woman named Serapias, adjures chthonic deities and 'deaf demons' to bind her husband, Marion, specifically targeting his tongue, anger, and spirit to prevent him from opposing her. The text is notable for its extensive use of 'voces magicae' (e.g., 'masomamasimablaboio') and its invocation of a complex hierarchy of underworld powers, including the 'King of the Deaf Demons' and Hecate. The physical state is fragmentary, requiring extensive reconstruction of the formulaic expressions.
