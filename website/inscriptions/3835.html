🤖 AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is part of the Athenian Naval Inventories (IG II² 1613), specifically dating to the year 353/2 BCE (archonship of Thoudemos). It documents the administrative oversight of the Athenian fleet, recording the transfer of 'wooden gear' (skeue xylina) and 'hanging gear' (skeue kremasta) between outgoing and incoming boards of dockyard overseers (epimeletai ton neorion). The text lists specific triremes by name (e.g., Nikephoros, Thraseia, Eutychia), their builders, and the trierarchs responsible for the equipment. It also notes the condition of the gear using the term 'dokimos' (fit for service) and accounts for items lost or damaged at sea.
