This inscription (IMagn 8) from Magnesia on the Maeander is a public record of land sales. It is dated by the prytany of Aristos and the months of Anthesterion and Smision. The text meticulously lists several transactions where individuals purchased land in the countryside (agroikia). It specifies the area in 'schoinoi' (a land measure), the price per unit in drachmas, and the total sum paid to the city treasury. Notably, it includes a Semitic name, Bosthon, indicating the diverse population of the city. The final clause ensures that the buyers have shared rights of entry and exit (eisodous kai exodous) to their respective plots.
