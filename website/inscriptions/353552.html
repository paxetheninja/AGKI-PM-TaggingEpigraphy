AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Priene, dating to approximately 120 BC. It honors Herodes, son of Herodes, for his extensive civic and diplomatic contributions. Herodes served as the eponymous magistrate (stephanephoros) and represented Priene in crucial embassies to the Roman commander Marcus Perperna and King Ariarathes VI of Cappadocia. The text highlights his efforts to renew ancestral ties with Athens, the metropolis of Priene, and his generous funding of religious festivals, including the Panionia and sacrifices to Zeus Olympios, Hera, and Athena Polias. The honors bestowed include a gold crown, multiple statues (bronze, gold, and marble), front-row seating at games (proedria), and free meals in the Prytaneion (sitesis).
