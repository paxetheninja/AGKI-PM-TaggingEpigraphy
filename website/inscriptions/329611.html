AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Tauromenion (modern Taormina) provides a rare and detailed look at the municipal financial administration of a Sicilian city during the late Roman Republic (70-36 BC). The text records the monthly accounts of the 'tamiai' (treasurers), specifically tracking the 'eisodos' (income), 'exodos' (expenditure), and 'loipon' (remainder). The accounts are calculated in 'litrai', a traditional Sicilian unit of weight and currency. Notably, the text uses transliterated Roman month names (e.g., Quintilis, Sextilis, September), reflecting the deep integration of Roman administrative structures into Sicilian civic life following the Roman conquest. The mention of 'sitoniois' suggests funds related to the grain supply, a critical concern for Sicilian cities.
