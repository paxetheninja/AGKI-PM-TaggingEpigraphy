AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a major decree of the Nesiotic League (the League of the Islanders) dating to approximately 280 BC. It honors Sostratos of Knidos, a prominent figure in the early Ptolemaic court and the architect of the Pharos of Alexandria. The text highlights the diplomatic relationship between the League and the Ptolemaic dynasty. Sostratos is rewarded for his 'goodwill' (eunoia) and 'virtue' (arete) with a golden crown worth 3000 Alexandrian drachmas, isopoliteia (equal citizenship) across all member islands, and ateleia (tax exemption). The decree also mandates the public proclamation of these honors at the Ptolemaieia festival in Delos and the erection of the decree on a stone stele in the sanctuary of Apollo.
