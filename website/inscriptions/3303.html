This inscription is a significant decree issued by the Panhellenion, the league of Greek cities established by Emperor Hadrian in 131/2 AD. Found in Athens, the administrative center of the league, the text records honors and administrative decisions involving the city of Thyateira. It highlights Hadrian's role as a benefactor ('euergetes') and 'Olympian' deity, specifically mentioning his 'philodoria' (generosity) regarding grain supplies. The document concludes with the name of the famous sophist and politician Herodes Atticus (Ti. Claudius Atticus Herodes), who served as the high priest of the Imperial cult and archon of the Panhellenion during this period.
