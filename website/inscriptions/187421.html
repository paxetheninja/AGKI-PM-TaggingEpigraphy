AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a mid-3rd century BC honorific decree from the island of Kalymna. It honors Aratokritos, son of Aristeas, for his hereditary goodwill (eunoia) and his significant financial contributions to the city, including the ransoming of prisoners and the funding of public needs. Most notably, the decree records his offer to construct and decorate the 'skene' and 'proskenion' of the theater in the sanctuary of Apollo Dalios at his own expense. The council and people accept this offer, grant him the space in the theater, and order the decree to be inscribed on the building itself as a permanent record of his benefaction.
