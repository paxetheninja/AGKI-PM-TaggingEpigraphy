This inscription, found at Delphi and dated to 341/340 BC, represents the financial records of the Delphic Amphictyony during the archonships of Peithagoras and Aristonymos. It documents the 'argyrologia' (money collection) conducted during the Pylaia (the council's meeting). The text is a crucial source for understanding the financing of the reconstruction of the Temple of Apollo after the Third Sacred War. It lists contributors from across the Greek world, including the Cyclades (Keos), the Peloponnese (Sikyon, Pellana), Sicily (Syracuse), and Thessaly, recording payments in Aeginetan, Attic, and Corinthian drachmas, which highlights the panhellenic nature of the sanctuary's support and the economic diversity of the period.
