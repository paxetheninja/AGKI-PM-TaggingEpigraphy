This inscription is an Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Glaukippos (277/6 BC). It honors a group of epimeletai (supervisors) for their diligent management of sacrifices to Zeus Soter and Athena Soteira. A significant detail is the mention of the 'strosis tes klines' (laying of the couch) and 'kosmesis tes trapezes' (decoration of the table), which refers to the ritual of theoxenia (or lectisternium), where a meal was symbolically prepared for the gods. The decree follows standard Athenian diplomatic formulas, including the list of honored individuals with their demotics and the instruction to set up the stele in the Agora.
