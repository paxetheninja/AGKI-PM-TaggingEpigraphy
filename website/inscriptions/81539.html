This inscription is a victor list (agonistic catalogue) from the city of Chalkis, dating to the late 2nd century BC. It records the winners of various athletic events (stadion, dolichos, diaulos, pale, pygme, pankration, pentathlon) held during the festival of the Herakleia. The games were overseen by the epimeletes of the gymnasium, Demetrios son of Andromachos. The list is organized by age categories: panpaidas (all-boys), paidas (boys), ephebon (ephebes), ageneious (beardless youths), and andras (men). The inclusion of several Roman citizens and individuals from Antioch alongside local Chalkidians reflects the international prestige of the games and the cosmopolitan nature of Euboea in the late Hellenistic period.
