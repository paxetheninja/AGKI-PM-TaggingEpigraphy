AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of a Delphic sacral manumission dated to the archonship of Sosiptros (c. 136 BC). It records the fictitious sale of a female slave named Parmenousa to the god Apollo Pythios by her owner Kallistratos, with the consent of her husband Aristomachos. The text outlines the conditions of her freedom, specifically that she is to be 'eleuthera' (free) and 'anaphaptos' (immune from seizure) for all time. It concludes with a list of witnesses, including the priests of Apollo and local archons, which was standard practice to ensure the legal validity of the manumission.
