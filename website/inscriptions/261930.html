AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Stratonikeia in Caria honors Marcus Sempronius Theodotus and his family for their extensive civic and religious contributions. Dated to the reign of Maximinus Thrax (235-238 AD), the text highlights their roles as priests and asiarchs, their funding of oil distributions (laithesia) for citizens, foreigners, and soldiers, and their meticulous performance of local mysteries and festivals including the Panamarea, Dionysia, and Heraia. It also records the erection of the stele in a stoa built by their father and mentions ancestral figures like Sempronius Clemens.
