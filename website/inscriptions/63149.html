AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree from the island of Delos, dated to the late 3rd century BC. It honors Admetos, son of Bokros, a Macedonian proxenos, for his significant services to the sanctuary and the Delian community. The decree mandates the creation of two bronze statues of Admetos: one to be erected in the Delian sanctuary near the altar of Zeus Polieus, and the other to be sent to Thessaloniki. Boulon, son of Tynnon, who proposed the motion, was also selected as the envoy to travel to Thessaloniki to coordinate the placement of the statue there. The text provides valuable insight into the diplomatic relations between the Delian sanctuary and the Macedonian kingdom during the Hellenistic period.
