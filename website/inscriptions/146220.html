AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Akraiphia, Boiotia, dating to the reign of Caligula (after 37 AD). It honors Epameinondas, son of Epameinondas, for his extraordinary liturgies and diplomatic services. The text records his two gymnasiarchies, his funding of the Great Ptoia festival after a thirty-year lapse, and his leadership of a Boiotian embassy to the Emperor Gaius (Caligula). It also mentions his financial contributions to public banquets (demothoiniai), the distribution of grain and wine, and the repair of the stadium (konisis). The decree orders the erection of statues in the sanctuary of Apollo Ptoios and the agora, and the inscription of the decree in both locations.
