AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from the Asklepieion of Kos, dating to the late 2nd or early 1st century BC, is a significant example of a 'lex sacra' (sacred law). It regulates the financial and ritual administration of the sanctuary, specifically focusing on the perquisites (gera) of the priest from animal sacrifices (oxen, birds) and the mandatory contributions to the temple treasury (thesauros). It defines the roles of various officials, including the priest (hiereus), the prostatai, and the trapezitai (bankers/table-wardens), and establishes penalties (zamia) for violations of the sanctuary's rules, such as unauthorized grazing in the sacred grove (temenos).
