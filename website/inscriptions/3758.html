This inscription is an Athenian inventory (paradosis) recording the transfer of sacred objects and temple furniture between outgoing and incoming boards of treasurers (tamiai) and overseers (epistatai). It dates to the mid-4th century BCE, specifically mentioning the archonships of Charikleides (363/2) and Elpines (356/5). The items listed include bronze baskets (kana), censers (thymia), cups (kyathoi), and various pieces of furniture like couches (klinai) and tables (trapezai), some of which are noted as broken (kateagota). The text belongs to the records of the Treasurers of the Other Gods.
