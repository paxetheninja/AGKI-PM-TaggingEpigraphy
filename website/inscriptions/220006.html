AI Analysis Summary

This inscription preserves an imperial rescript issued by the Tetrarchy (Diocletian, Maximian, Constantius, and Galerius) around 298 AD. Addressed to the inhabitants of Elephantine and Syene (modern Aswan), the text deals with administrative and fiscal matters, specifically land ownership (gedia) and traditional privileges (pronomia). It reflects the intensive administrative reorganization of Egypt undertaken by Diocletian following the suppression of the revolt of Domitius Domitianus and Aurelius Achilleus in 297-298 AD. The presence of the 'hermeneia' (translation) suggests the original was in Latin, translated into Greek for the local population.
