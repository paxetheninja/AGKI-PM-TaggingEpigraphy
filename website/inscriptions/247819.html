This inscription (IEph 26) is a significant decree of the Ephesian gerousia dating to the reign of the Emperor Commodus (late 2nd century AD). It serves as a restoration of ancient religious and financial privileges. The text looks back to the city's Hellenistic refoundation by King Lysimachus, noting that while he had established the mysteries and sacrifices, funds had later dwindled. The decree honors the official Neikomedes for his 'philoteimia' (generosity/ambition) in identifying new revenues that allow the gerousia to return to its 'ancient custom' (palaion ethos) of celebrating sacrifices and banquets for Artemis and the Emperor. It includes specific instructions on the distribution of money for these feasts and threatens those who would violate the decree with charges of sacrilege (hierosylia).
