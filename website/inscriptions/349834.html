This inscription, found in the Asklepieion of Kos, records a territorial arbitration between the Ionian cities of Klazomenai and Kolophon. In the Hellenistic period, it was common for cities to invite 'foreign judges' (xenodikai) from a neutral third city, such as Kos, to resolve long-standing land disputes. The text follows a standard formula for such awards: it names the month (Karneios), the judges, the legal basis (diagramma), and then provides an exhaustive list of topographical markers (tombs, rivers like the Lagyros, quarries, and hills) that define the newly established border. The presence of Teian citizens suggests they may have served as witnesses or were involved in the adjudication process.
