This 3rd-century AD inscription from Attica preserves a series of sacred hymns (Paeans) dedicated to the healing gods Asclepius, Hygieia, and Telesphorus. It includes the famous Hymn to Hygieia, traditionally attributed to Ariphron of Sicyon, which asks the goddess to dwell with the speaker for the remainder of their life. The text also addresses Telesphorus, a minor healing deity often associated with the recovery phase of illness. The mention of the 'Kekropidai' (Athenians) and 'Epidaurioi' (Epidaurians) highlights the pan-Hellenic nature of the Asclepius cult and its specific local observance in Athens. The presence of lacunae and dashes indicates the fragmentary state of the stone.
