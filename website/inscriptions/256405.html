This inscription is a diplomatic decree from the city of Arcadia in Crete (identified by the mention of 'kosmoi' magistrates). It honors the city of Teos and its ambassadors, Herodotus and Menekles, by confirming the sacred status and asylia (inviolability) of Teos and its territory, which is dedicated to Dionysus. The decree also grants the ambassadors proxeny and civic rights such as isopoliteia and the right to own land and houses, while ordering the text to be displayed in the temple of Asclepius.
