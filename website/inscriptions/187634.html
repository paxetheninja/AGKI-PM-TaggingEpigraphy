AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a proxeny decree from the city of Akragas (Agrigentum) in Sicily, though found in Rome. It honors Demetrios of Syracuse for his goodwill and services to the Akragantine people. The text provides a detailed administrative snapshot, naming the prostates, the proagoros, the secretary, and the ambassadors who reported on Demetrios's merits. It follows the standard Doric dialect of Sicily and specifies that the decree should be recorded on two bronze tablets, one to be placed in the council chamber (bouleuterion) and the other given to the honoree. The mention of the month Karneios and the tribe in presidency (proedreuousas) reflects the local democratic institutions of Akragas in the Hellenistic period.
