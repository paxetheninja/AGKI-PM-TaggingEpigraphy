AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Athenian prytany decree from the late 1st century BC (c. 20 BC), specifically during the archonship of Demeas. It follows the standard formulaic structure: a preamble naming the archon and the proposer, a report by the prytaneis of the tribe Pandionis regarding the sacrifices performed by their treasurer (Philon of Paiania), a formal decree by the Boule to praise and crown the treasurer and the prytaneis, and finally a complete register of the fifty prytaneis arranged by their respective demes. The text also lists various administrative officials associated with the prytany, such as the strategos and the keryx.
