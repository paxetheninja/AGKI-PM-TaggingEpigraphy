AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant honorific decree from the city of Abdera in Thrace, dating to the 2nd century BC. It honors a Roman citizen, Gaius Postumius, son of Marcus, and his son Publius, for their goodwill (eunoia) and services to the city. The decree grants them the status of proxenos and euergetes, gold crowns to be announced at the Dionysia festival, and a gilded bronze statue to be erected in Thessaloniki. The text provides detailed administrative information, including the cost of the statue (3000 Alexandrian drachmas) and the involvement of local officials like the nomophylakes and the oikonomos Soterichos. It reflects the increasing Roman influence in the Aegean and the diplomatic efforts of Greek cities to maintain favorable relations with Roman individuals.
