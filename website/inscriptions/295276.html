This 2nd-century BC decree from Abdera honors Gaius Postumius, a Roman, and his son Publius. The text details Gaius's role in preserving the city's freedom and his virtuous conduct during his stays. Honors granted include a gold crown, proedria (front-row seating) at the Dionysia, and a bronze statue to be erected in Thessaloniki. The decree is notable for its specific financial instructions, mentioning a cost of 3000 Alexandrian drachmas to be handled by the city's nomophylakes and oikonomos. The mention of Thessaloniki as the site for the statue suggests the importance of that city as a regional Roman administrative hub during this period.
