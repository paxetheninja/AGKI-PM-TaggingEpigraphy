AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a temple inventory from Aegina, dating to the late 5th century BC. It provides a comprehensive list of ritual objects, furniture, and votive offerings belonging to the local cults of Mnia and Azesia, as well as Dionysus. The inventory specifies materials such as bronze, iron, and cypress wood, and lists items like incense burners, lampstands, kraters, statues, iron pins, shields, and thrones, some of which were kept in the opisthodomos (back room) of the temple. The text is fragmentary with several lacunae but the nature of the list is clear.
