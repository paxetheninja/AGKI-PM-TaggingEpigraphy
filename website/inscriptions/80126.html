AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Karystos, Euboia, dates to the reign of the Emperor Hadrian (117-138 AD). It serves as an official civic register of the bouleutai (councilors) who were chosen by lot to serve in the local council. The text is organized into groups, each headed by an 'archeproboulos' (chief of the probouloi). It also references an eponymous official who held the prestigious office of stephanephoros. The presence of Roman names like Flavius Modestinus reflects the integration of local elites into Roman imperial structures during the 2nd century AD.
