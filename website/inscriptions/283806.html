This inscription is a formal decree of the Lycian League (Koinon) dating to the early 3rd century AD. It honors Troilos, also known as Gnaeus Iulius, a prominent citizen of Balboura. The text highlights his religious role as a priest of Apollo Patroos and his civic generosity, specifically mentioning distributions (dianomai) of his own funds to the league's councilors and officials. The decree also emphasizes his distinguished family background, noting that his brothers, Kastor and Polydeukes, served as 'ethnikoi archontes' (federal magistrates). The honors bestowed include a gilded image and the erection of a statue, typical of the high-status civic culture in Roman Lycia.
