AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a building contract (syngraphe) from Delos, dated to approximately 280 BC. It provides detailed technical specifications for the construction of wooden elements, specifically 'klimakides' (steps or ladder-like supports) and 'phatnai' (coffers or ceiling panels). The text outlines the materials to be used (elm and cornel wood), the finishing techniques (using red ochre and scrapers), and the assembly methods (pegs and joints). The latter part of the inscription records the awarding of the contracts to Phanias and Peisiboulos for 300 drachmas each, listing their guarantors (Erembrotos and Antigonos) and a series of witnesses. The document was intended to be inscribed on a stone stele and displayed in the sanctuary, serving as a public record of the administration of temple funds and construction projects.
