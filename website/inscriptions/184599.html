This inscription, dated to 185/186 AD during the reign of Commodus, represents a significant legal dossier from Chersonesos. It contains a series of official communications regarding the 'pornikon telos' (tax on prostitution). The city had petitioned Roman authorities to uphold previous imperial and consular rulings that likely exempted certain groups or regulated the collection of this tax. The text mentions a 'chiliarch' (tribune) named Stilprimianus and Valerius Maximus. It was publicly displayed to prevent 'neoterismos' (illegal innovations or changes) and to ensure that the rights of the citizens were protected against abuses by soldiers or tax collectors. The document concludes with the names of the envoys (presbeuontes) who handled the petition.
