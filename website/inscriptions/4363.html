AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic Athenian ephebic catalogue dating to the late 2nd century AD (c. 190-200 AD). It lists the names of ephebes and their fathers, grouped by their respective tribes (Erechtheis, Aegeis, Pandionis, etc.). It also includes a section for 'epengraphoi' (foreigners or non-citizens enrolled in the ephebeia). The text mentions a 'kosmetes' (the official overseeing the ephebes) and a 'hiereus boutypos' (a priest involved in sacrificial rituals). The presence of brackets and dashes indicates significant surface damage and missing text.
