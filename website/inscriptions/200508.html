AI Analysis Summary

This text represents the 'Great Code' of Gortyn, the most significant surviving legal document from ancient Greece. Dating to the mid-5th century BC, it is written in the Dorian dialect and boustrophedon style. The code systematically addresses matters of private law, such as the status of free persons versus slaves (doloi), the rights of the 'patroiokos' (the heiress who must marry her next of kin to keep property within the family), divorce procedures, and the division of estates. It provides a rare glimpse into the social hierarchy and judicial administration of a Cretan polis during the transition from the Archaic to the Classical period.
