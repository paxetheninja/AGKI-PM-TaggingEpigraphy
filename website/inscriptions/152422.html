This inscription is a significant honorary decree from the city of Kalindoia in Macedonia, dated to 1 AD (the month of Daisios). It honors Apollonios, son of Apollonios, for his service as a priest of Zeus and Caesar Augustus. The text provides a detailed account of his 'euergetism' (benefaction), including the funding of sacrifices, public banquets (hestiasis), and games (agones). It specifically mentions the dedication of a statue of Augustus, which is a crucial piece of evidence for the early Imperial Cult in the region. The decree also mentions the 'politarchs', a characteristic Macedonian magistracy, and orders the erection of statues for Apollonios and his parents in the most prominent part of the agora.
