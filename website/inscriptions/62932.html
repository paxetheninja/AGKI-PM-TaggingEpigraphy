The text is divided into sections: the initial transfer of cash from the sacred chest (kibotos), followed by a meticulous inventory of votive offerings (stamnons, phialai, crowns, and jewelry) stored in the Temple of Apollo. It highlights the international prestige of the Delian sanctuary through mentions of royal dedications from the Ptolemaic, Antigonid, and Seleucid dynasties. The mention of 'technitai' refers to the Dionysiac artists, indicating the sanctuary's role in funding cultural and theatrical performances.
