This inscription is a mid-2nd century BC honorific decree from Oropos. It records the city's decision to honor a panel of judges and their secretaries who were sent to settle legal disputes between the Eretrians and the Thebans. The text details the diplomatic process, including the reception of ambassadors, the appointment of the judicial panel, and the subsequent honors (gold crowns and a bronze statue) awarded to the judges for their fairness and adherence to the 'symbolon' (judicial treaty). The names of the judges, such as Diodoros son of Krates, are preserved in the latter half of the fragment.
