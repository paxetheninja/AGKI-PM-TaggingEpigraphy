AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Apolexis (20/19 BC). It honors Themistokles, son of Theophrastos, of the deme Hagnous, who served as the Dadouchos (torch-bearer) in the Eleusinian Mysteries. The text is significant for its detailed prosopography of the genos of the Kerykes, one of the two families responsible for the Eleusinian cult. It traces Themistokles' lineage through several generations of high-ranking religious officials, including Hierophants and Altar-priests, emphasizing the hereditary nature of these roles and the family's long-standing piety and service to the state and the goddesses Demeter and Kore.
