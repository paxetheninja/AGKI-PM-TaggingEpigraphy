AI Analysis Summary

This 3rd-century BC decree from Histria (Istros) honors Dionysios, son of Strouthion, for his exceptional euergetism. During a financial crisis and grain shortage, Dionysios provided 1,000 gold pieces to the city to ensure the food supply. In recognition, the city awarded him the title of 'euergetes' (benefactor), commissioned a bronze statue of him to be placed in the agora, and granted him and his descendants the hereditary priesthood of the Samothracian Gods. The decree was to be inscribed on a white stone slab and set up near the altar of the Samothracian Gods, highlighting the religious and civic importance of the cult in Histria.
