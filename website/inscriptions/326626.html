AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, found at Pagai (modern Alepochori) and dating to approximately 192 BC, is a significant document of Hellenistic international law and arbitration. It records a decree by the city of Pagai honoring the Achaean League and the city of Sicyon for their mediation in a long-standing territorial dispute between Pagai and its neighbor Aigosthena. The dispute specifically concerned the harbor of Panormos. The text details the appointment of neutral judges from Thyrreion and Kassope, the dispatch of ambassadors, and the final resolution. The decree grants the mediators various honors, including proxeny and the right to have the decree inscribed on a stone stele. The presence of numerous Achaean names reflects the political dominance of the Achaean League in the region during this period.
