AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Nikias (266/5 BC). It honors the archon Nikias of Otryne, his assistants (paredroi), and the supervisors of the procession (epimeletai tes pompes) for their pious and diligent performance of religious duties, specifically the sacrifices and the procession in honor of Dionysos. The text mentions the safety of the Council, the People, and the crops of the land. It concludes with the administrative order to publish the decree on a stone stele in the sanctuary of Dionysos, with the cost to be covered by the administration.
