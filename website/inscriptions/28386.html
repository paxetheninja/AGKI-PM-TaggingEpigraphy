AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal honorific decree from the city of Methana, dated to 2 AD. It honors Lucius Licinius Antros, a Corinthian citizen, for his virtuous conduct and hospitality toward the citizens of Methana during his stay. The decree grants him the titles of proxenos and benefactor, along with the right to own land and pastures (enktesis). The text begins with a long list of local magistrates or witnesses (synarchai) and concludes with the formal approval of the assembly.
