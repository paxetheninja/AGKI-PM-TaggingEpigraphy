AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is part of the extensive series of gymnasiarchic accounts from Tauromenion (modern Taormina). It documents the annual administration of the gymnasium, listing the eponymous magistrates and the gymnasiarchs responsible for each year. The text is highly technical, recording the quantities of oil (elaion) and wine (oinos) used, measured in kadoi, prochoi, and kotylai. It also details complex financial transactions involving large sums of money in talents and litrae, including loans (daneizomenon) and remaining balances (loipon). These lists are a primary source for the prosopography and economic history of Hellenistic Sicily.
