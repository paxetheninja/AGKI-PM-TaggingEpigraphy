AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is an honorific decree issued by the Delphic Amphictyony in the late 3rd century BC to honor Dermokles (likely of Chios). The text details his piety (eusebeia) towards the gods and his goodwill (eunoia) towards the Aetolian League and the Amphictyons. The honors bestowed include a laurel crown, a gold crown, and the erection of two bronze statuesâ€”one in the sanctuary at Delphi and another in Chios. Additionally, Dermokles is granted several high-status privileges: asylia (inviolability), ateleia (tax exemption), prodikia (priority in legal trials), and proedria (reserved seating at games). The decree was to be published on two stone stelae, with the costs covered by the secretary of the Hieromnemones.
