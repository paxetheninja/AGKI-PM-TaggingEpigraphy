AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant honorific decree from Chersonesos Taurica, dated to 129/130 AD during the Roman period. It honors Demetrios, son of Herakleitos, granting him proxeny and citizenship (politeia), along with the right of entry and exit (eisploun kai ekploun) in peace and war. The text is dated by the 'reign' of the goddess Parthenos, the city's patron deity, and the secretaryship of Ariston, son of Attinas. The document concludes with a detailed list of magistrates (archons) and guardians of the law (nomophylakes) who sealed the decree, providing valuable prosopographical data for the region.
