This Hellenistic decree from Samos (ca. 280-246 BC) honors Aristolaos, a Macedonian general (strategos) stationed in Caria, likely under the service of the Ptolemaic kings. The decree grants him Samian citizenship (politeia), proxeny, and the right to hold front-row seats at games (proedria). It reflects the diplomatic relations between the Samian polis and the military administrators of the Hellenistic monarchs. The text provides detailed administrative instructions for the publication of the stele in the Sanctuary of Hera (Heraion) and lists the officials responsible for the costs and execution.
