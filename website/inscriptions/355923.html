AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a dossier of documents from the city of Cyme in Aeolis, dating to the early 3rd century BC. It records a diplomatic exchange between the city and Philetaerus, the founder of the Attalid dynasty of Pergamon. The city, facing a need for military defense, sent ambassadors (Menecrates and Asclepiodorus) to Philetaerus to request arms. Philetaerus responded with a letter granting 600 bronze-plated shields (peltai) as a gift. In return, the people of Cyme passed a decree to honor him with a golden crown and a statue to be announced at the festivals of Dionysia and Antiochia. The text also contains detailed regulations for the distribution of the shields among the tribes and strict prohibitions against their sale or pawning, including specific fines.
