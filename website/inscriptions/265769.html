AI Analysis Summary

This inscription from Neoclaudiopolis (often associated with the region of Neokaisareia) is a classic example of a funerary imprecation or curse. It addresses any future passerby or owner of the land, warning them under the threat of divine wrath not to disturb the body, the stone structure, the statues, or the inscription itself. The text lists a comprehensive pantheon of gods, particularly those associated with the underworld and justice (Pluto, Demeter, Kore, Hekate, Erinyes, Hermes Chthonios), to enforce the curse. The curse includes the total destruction of the violator's family, lineage, and property, while a blessing is promised to those who respect and maintain the tomb.
