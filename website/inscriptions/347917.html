This inscription is a formal Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Polyeuktos (250/49 BC). It honors Xenokrates of Chios for his extensive benefactions to the city of Athens. The text details his assistance to Athenian grain commissioners (sitonai) such as Pamphilos and Philippos, particularly during their missions to Syracuse. Xenokrates is also praised for helping recover runaway slaves, providing travel funds (ephodion) to citizens, and his general readiness to serve the Athenian state. The document is a classic example of Hellenistic euergetism, where foreign individuals were granted honors in exchange for diplomatic and economic support.
