This inscription is an Athenian honorific decree dating to the mid-3rd century BCE (archonship of Athenodoros). It honors the sitophylakes (grain commissioners) for their diligent and just administration of the grain supply. The decree follows the standard procedure: a proposal by the Boule (Council) to the Demos (People), the awarding of a gold crown for 'philotimia' (love of honor) and 'dikaiosyne' (justice), and the instruction for the secretary to publish the text on a stone stele to be placed near the Synedrion. The mention of King Antigonos (Antigonos II Gonatas) highlights the political reality of Macedonian hegemony in Athens during this period. The text is fragmentary but largely reconstructible through standard epigraphic formulas.
