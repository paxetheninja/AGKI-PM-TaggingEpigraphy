AI Analysis Summary

This is an Athenian honorific decree from the mid-3rd century BCE. It honors the 'sitophylakes' (grain commissioners) who served during the archonship of Athenodorus. The text follows the standard Athenian formula for such decrees: it mentions the Council and People, lists the names and demotics of the officials being honored, specifies the award of a golden crown for their 'philotimia' (ambition/love of honor) and 'dikaiosyne' (justice), and provides instructions for the publication of the decree on a stone stele. The military treasurer is tasked with paying for the inscription. Notably, King Antigonus (likely Antigonus II Gonatas) is also mentioned in the latter part of the fragment.
