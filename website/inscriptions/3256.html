AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant Athenian decree dating to the archonship of Demochares (78/7 BC). It honors the 'ergastinai', the young women from prominent Athenian families who were selected to weave the sacred peplos for the statue of Athena Polias. The decree acknowledges their successful completion of the task, their participation in the Panathenaic procession, and their dedication of a silver phiale. The text is historically valuable for its prosopographical data, as it lists the names of the maidens and their fathers, organized by their respective tribes (Erechtheis, Aegeis, Leontis, Ptolemais, etc.), providing insight into the social and religious life of late Hellenistic Athens.
