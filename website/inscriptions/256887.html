AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree of the 'Sacred Synod of the Artists of Dionysus' (Dionysiac Technitai) from the oikoumene, honoring Gaius Julius Longianus, a tragic poet. The text is dated to 127 AD by the Roman consuls Marcus Gavius Squilla Gallicanus and Titus Atilius Rufus Titianus. It highlights the poet's 'virtuous education' (enaretos paideia) and his contributions to the synod. Hadrian is notably referred to as the 'New Dionysus', a common title during his travels in the Greek East. The text is heavily restored but follows standard epigraphic formulas for honorific decrees by professional associations.
