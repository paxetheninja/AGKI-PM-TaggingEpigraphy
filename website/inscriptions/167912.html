This inscription is a decree from the city of Odessus (modern Varna) dating to the mid-1st century BC. It honors Menogenes, son of Asklepiades, who served as a strategos (general) for the Thracian King Sadalas II. The decree highlights Menogenes' diplomatic and practical assistance to the citizens of Odessus and his high standing at the royal court. In recognition of his 'kalokagathia' (nobility and goodness) and 'eunoia' (goodwill), the city grants him and his descendants proxeny, citizenship, the right to own property (enktesis), tax exemption (isoteleia), and front-row seating at games (proedria). The text concludes with instructions to inscribe the decree on a white stone stele and place it in the sanctuary of Apollo, the city's patron deity. The transcription shows significant lacunae and phonetic spelling variations typical of the period and region.
