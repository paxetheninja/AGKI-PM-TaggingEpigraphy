AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal Athenian decree dated to the archonship of Aristophantos (330/29 BC). It was proposed by the famous statesman Lykourgos of Boutadai. The decree honors Eudemos of Plataea, a resident alien or foreign benefactor, for his significant material contribution to the city. Eudemos provided 1,000 yokes of oxen for the construction of the Panathenaic Stadium and the theater. In return, he is granted the title of benefactor (euergetes), the right to own land and a house in Attica (enktesis), and the privilege of serving in the military and paying taxes alongside Athenian citizens. The text is a key document for the Lykourgan building program in 4th-century Athens.
