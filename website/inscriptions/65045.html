This inscription, found on Delos and dating to approximately 112 BC, contains a list of gymnasiarchs. It was commissioned by Phokion, son of Aristokrates of the deme Melite, who recorded the names of those who had held the office of gymnasiarch since the Athenian recovery of the island in 167/6 BC ('since the people through the Romans recovered the island'). The text is a significant prosopographical source for Athenian citizens residing in Delos during the Second Athenian Cleruchy, listing individuals by their full Athenian onomastic formula (name, patronymic, and demotic). The mention of the 'demos' and 'Romans' highlights the political context of Delos as a free port under Athenian administration and Roman protection.
