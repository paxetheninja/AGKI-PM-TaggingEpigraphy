This inscription is an honorific decree from the mid-2nd century BC (155-153 BC) issued by the 'ktoina' (a local administrative unit) of the Potidaians on the island of Karpathos. It honors Pamphilidas, son of Hieron, a citizen of Karpathopolis, for his exceptional leadership and bravery during a war against Cretan invaders. The text describes how he organized the defense of the island, provided equipment for the youth, and ensured the safety of the civilian population (women and children). The decree mandates the creation of two stelae of Lartian stone (a Rhodian limestone) to be placed in the sanctuaries of Poseidon and Athena Lindia, reflecting the island's integration into the Rhodian sphere of influence.
