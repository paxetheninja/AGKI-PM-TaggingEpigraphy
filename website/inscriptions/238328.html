This inscription (CID II 4) belongs to the series of financial accounts managed by the Naopoioi (temple-builders) at Delphi. It dates to the archonship of Pantichares (362/361 BC) and records the receipts from the 11th pylaia (Amphictyonic meeting). The text lists the 'eisphora' (contributions) from various Greek states and individuals for the rebuilding of the Temple of Apollo, which had been destroyed in 373 BC. It provides a valuable snapshot of the financial administration and the pan-Hellenic cooperation involved in the sanctuary's restoration.
