AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, known as the 'Lygdamis Inscription' (ML 32), is a seminal legal document from mid-5th century BCE Halicarnassus. It records a joint decree by the Halicarnassians, the Salmacitians, and the tyrant Lygdamis. The text regulates property disputes following a period of civil strife, establishing an 18-month statute of limitations for claims on land and houses. It also contains a 'sanctity of law' clause, prescribing that anyone attempting to repeal the law will have their property confiscated to Apollo and be permanently exiled. The mix of Carian and Greek names (e.g., Kasbollis, Panuasis) reflects the multi-ethnic composition of the city.
