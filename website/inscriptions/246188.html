This inscription represents the textual captions of the Madaba Map, a 6th-century AD mosaic floor in the Church of Saint George in Madaba, Jordan. It is the oldest surviving original cartographic depiction of the Holy Land, including Jerusalem and the Nile Delta. The text identifies biblical sites, tribal allotments, and major Byzantine cities, serving as both a religious and geographical guide for pilgrims. The language is Koine Greek, typical of the Byzantine period in the Levant.
