This inscription (IStr 254) from Stratonikeia in Caria is a detailed record of the civic and religious activities of Demetrios (son of Apollonides Damas) and his wife Areia. It documents their tenure in prestigious roles such as high priests (archiereis), crown-bearers (stephanephoroi), and gymnasiarchs. The text is particularly valuable for its description of 'philotimia' (public-spirited generosity), noting that they provided oil for the gymnasia and hosted inclusive banquets (deipna) for citizens, foreigners, and even slaves. It also mentions the hiring of musicians (kroumata) for festivities that lasted late into the night, illustrating the social and cultural life of the Hellenistic/Roman city.
