This inscription (IG IIÂ² 649+) is a major Athenian honorific decree from 293/2 BC, during the archonship of Olympiodoros. Proposed by the influential politician Stratokles of Diomeia, it honors Philippides of Paiania for his extensive public service, including military command as a strategos, religious duties as an agonothetes, and the performance of various liturgies (trierarchies and choregies). The decree grants him a golden crown, a bronze statue in the Agora, and permanent dining rights (sitesis) in the Prytaneion. The text is reconstructed from multiple fragments and contains significant restorations.
