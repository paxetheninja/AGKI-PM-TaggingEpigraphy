This inscription (LSS 38) is a significant example of a sacred law from Delphi, dating to approximately 425 BC. It outlines the logistical and religious requirements for a sacred delegation (theoria). It specifies the rations (grain, meat, wine) for the architheoroi and their staff, which includes a seer, herald, flute-player, and various naval officers (helmsman, boatswain). The text also details the distribution of sacrificial hides (derma) and the authority of the Council to impose fines (zemiai) on those who fail to maintain order, reflecting the intersection of religious practice and civic administration in the sanctuary.
