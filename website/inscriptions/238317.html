AI Analysis Summary

This inscription, identified as LSS 38 (Sokolowski), is a significant example of a Delphic sacred law from the late 5th century BC. It regulates the organization of a sacred delegation (theoria), listing the required personnel such as the archetheoros (leader), mantis (seer), and various ship crew members (kybernetes, keleustes). It specifies the rations (meat, wine, barley cakes) and sacrificial portions (pelanos, skins) due to these individuals. Furthermore, it establishes a system of fines for the 'kosmionta' (magistrate) in case of negligence, requiring the Council (boule) to record such penalties. The mention of 'Aeginetan obols' provides economic context for the period.
