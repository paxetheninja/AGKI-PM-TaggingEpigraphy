AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a monumental honorific list from Lindos, Rhodes, dating to the early 1st century AD. It records the extensive public and private recognition of a citizen named Lapheides. The text details honors from the 'entire people' (the Rhodian state), the three constituent cities of the Rhodian synoecism (Lindos, Ialysos, and Kamiros), and a multitude of religious associations (koina) such as the Dionysiastai, Serapiastai, and Isiaistai. The honors include golden crowns (chrysois stephanois), bronze statues (andrianti chalkoi), and painted portraits (eikoni). Notably, it also commemorates an athletic victory in a chariot race, highlighting the honoree's elite status and participation in panhellenic or local games.
