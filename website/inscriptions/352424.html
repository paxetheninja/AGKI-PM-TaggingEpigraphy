AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal honorific decree from the city of Priene, dating to the 2nd century BC. It honors the Samothracian poet Herodes, son of Poseidonios, for his literary works and his virtuous conduct during his stay in the city. The text specifically highlights his poetic compositions regarding Dardanos, Eetion, and the mythological wedding of Cadmus and Harmonia. As a reward, the city grants him a gold crown to be announced at the Dionysia festival and bestows the status of proxenos upon him and his descendants. The decree was ordered to be inscribed on a stone stele and erected in the sanctuary of Athena Polias.
