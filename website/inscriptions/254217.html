AI Analysis Summary

This is a Samian decree from the late 4th century BC honoring Pylos of Aegina, son of Sosigenes. The text is significant for its mention of the festivals Antigoneia and Demetreia, which date the inscription to the period of Antigonid influence over Samos. The decree grants Pylos citizenship, proxeny, and the right to own property, and records his enrollment into the tribe Chesieus, the chiliastys Oinopes, and the hekatostys Elandridai. The document was to be set up in the Heraion, the sanctuary of Hera on Samos. The transcription provided contains numerous lacunae, justifying a fragmentary classification.
