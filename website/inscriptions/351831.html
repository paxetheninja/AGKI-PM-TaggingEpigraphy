AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a Hellenistic decree from the city of Myus, dating to the mid-2nd century BC (ca. 158 BC). It details the administrative and religious procedures for the sale of a priesthood (hierosyne) and the allocation of public revenues. The decree also mandates the erection of a statue of the king (likely Attalus II of Pergamon) and the publication of the text on the parastas (doorpost) of the Temple of Apollo Termintheus. It further describes the dispatch of ambassadors to the king to ensure the continued honor of the god and the welfare of the people.
