AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a formal decree of the Thessalian League (Koinon) dating to approximately 49/48 BC. It honors a judicial commission from Miletus, including judges and their secretary Philtes, for their fair conduct. The decree grants them proxeny, citizenship across all Thessalian cities, and other honors. It mandates the publication of the text on stone pillars in the sanctuary of Zeus Eleutherios in Larisa and the sanctuary of Apollo Delphinios in Miletus. The document names Philokrates as the strategos and Pausanias of Gomphoi as the guarantor of the proxeny.
