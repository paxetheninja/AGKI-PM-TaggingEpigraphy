AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a significant fragment of the Delian temple accounts (hieropoioi records) from approximately 200 BC. It documents the leasing of sacred estates or public buildings (hiera). The text follows a standard administrative formula: naming the property, the lessee, the rental price, and the guarantors (eggyoi). Crucially, it includes a 'paradosis' or inventory of the property's condition at the time of the lease, listing architectural elements such as doors (thyrai), sheds (kleisia), ox-stalls (boostaseis), and agricultural features like fig trees (sykai). This ensured that the sacred property was maintained and returned in proper order. The mention of the Gymnasium and Ekklesiasterion indicates the involvement of major public civic structures in these administrative records.
