AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a decree of the Koinon of the Islanders (League of the Islanders) dating to the Hellenistic period. It honors Timon of Syracuse for his financial assistance to the league, specifically for providing silver at a favorable rate for grain purchases without charging the usual exchange fee (kollubos). The decree specifies that two stelae be erected: one in the sanctuary of Apollo on Delos and one in the sanctuary of Poseidon and Amphitrite on Tenos. The text mentions Rhodian silver, which was a standard currency in the region at the time. The ambassadors listed at the end represent different member islands of the league (Tenos and Siphnos).
