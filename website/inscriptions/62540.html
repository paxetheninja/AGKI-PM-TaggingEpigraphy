AI Analysis Summary

This inscription is a classic example of Delian administrative records from the 4th century BC, specifically the accounts of the Amphictyony. It lists the leases (misthosis) of sacred properties belonging to the Sanctuary of Apollo on Delos. The text follows a formulaic structure: the property or house (oikia) being leased, the name of the lessee (often with an Athenian demotic, indicating the period of Athenian administrative control), the rent amount, and the name of the guarantor (egguetes). Notable properties mentioned include a smithy (chalkeion). The presence of numerous Athenian demotics (Aphidnaios, Alopekethen, Prasieus, etc.) confirms the historical context of the Athenian oversight of the Delian sanctuary during this period.
